Google Ads vs SEO: Which Is Better
for Your NH Business?

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 9 min read

“Should I do Google Ads or SEO?” is one of the most common questions we hear from NH business owners. The honest answer: it depends on your budget, timeline, and how your customers find you.

This isn’t a sales pitch for SEO (even though that’s what we do). We’ll give you a genuinely honest comparison so you can make the right decision for your business.

The Quick Answer

  • Need customers this week? Google Ads.
  • Want to build a sustainable lead pipeline? SEO.
  • Have $2,000+/month? Both.

Now let’s dig into why.

Google Ads: Pay to Play

How It Works

You bid on keywords. When someone searches that keyword, your ad appears at the top of Google. You pay every time someone clicks (pay-per-click, or PPC). The ad disappears the moment you stop paying.

What Google Ads Costs in NH

Average cost-per-click varies dramatically by industry:

  • Home services (plumber, HVAC, electrician): $15-45/click
  • Legal (personal injury, DUI): $50-150/click
  • Medical/dental: $8-25/click
  • Restaurants/retail: $2-8/click
  • Auto repair: $5-15/click
  • Real estate: $3-12/click

With a typical 3-5% conversion rate, you need about 20-33 clicks to get one lead. For a Manchester plumber, that’s $300-1,500 per lead from ads alone.

Most NH small businesses need at least $1,000-2,500/month in ad spend (plus $500-1,000/month for management) to see meaningful results from Google Ads.

Pros of Google Ads

  • Instant visibility. Turn on ads today, appear at the top of Google today.
  • Precise targeting. Target specific cities, zip codes, even income levels.
  • Measurable. You know exactly how much each lead costs.
  • Scalable. Spend more, get more (up to a point).
  • Great for testing. Quickly validate which keywords and messages convert.

Cons of Google Ads

  • Expensive over time. Costs never go down. You’re renting visibility, not building it.
  • Click fraud. Competitors and bots click your ads, costing you money.
  • Ad blindness. Many searchers skip ads entirely. 70-80% of clicks go to organic results.
  • No lasting value. Stop paying = disappear instantly. Zero residual benefit.
  • Bidding wars. As competitors enter the market, costs rise.

SEO: Build to Own

How It Works

You optimize your website, Google Business Profile, content, and online presence to rank higher in organic (non-paid) results. This includes technical fixes, content creation, link building, and ongoing optimization.

What SEO Costs in NH

According to our research on SEO pricing for NH businesses:

  • DIY: $0-200/month (your time + tools)
  • Freelancer: $500-1,500/month
  • Agency (small market like NH): $1,000-3,000/month
  • Agency (competitive industries): $2,500-5,000/month

Pros of SEO

  • Compounding returns. Work done today keeps paying dividends for months or years.
  • Higher trust. 70-80% of users skip ads and click organic results. Organic visibility builds credibility.
  • Lower cost per lead over time. After 6-12 months, your cost-per-lead typically drops below Google Ads.
  • Multiple touchpoints. SEO improves your website, Google Maps presence, reviews, and content — all at once.
  • Defensive moat. Once you rank, it’s hard for competitors to knock you off quickly.

Cons of SEO

  • Takes time. Expect 3-6 months before significant results. Not ideal if you need leads tomorrow.
  • Less predictable. Google’s algorithm changes can shift rankings unexpectedly.
  • Requires consistency. Stop doing SEO and competitors will eventually overtake you.
  • Harder to measure directly. Attribution is messier than ads (though it’s not impossible).

The Real Cost Comparison

Let’s compare a Nashua HVAC company spending $2,000/month over 12 months:

Google Ads: $2,000/month

  • Total spend: $24,000/year
  • Average CPC: $25
  • Clicks: ~960/year
  • At 4% conversion: ~38 leads/year
  • Cost per lead: ~$630
  • Month 13 with no spend: 0 leads

SEO: $2,000/month

  • Total spend: $24,000/year
  • Months 1-3: minimal leads (building foundation)
  • Months 4-6: 5-15 leads/month
  • Months 7-12: 15-30 leads/month
  • Total leads year 1: ~120-180
  • Cost per lead: ~$130-200
  • Month 13 with no spend: still generating 20+ leads (rankings don’t vanish overnight)

In year 1, SEO typically delivers 3-5x more leads per dollar than Google Ads for local service businesses. The gap widens in year 2 and beyond because your SEO foundation is already built.

When Google Ads Makes More Sense

  1. New business launch. You just opened and need customers now while SEO builds.
  2. Seasonal promotions. Running a winter special? Ads get immediate eyeballs.
  3. Competitive emergency. A competitor just moved into your territory and you need to defend market share.
  4. Testing a new service. Want to see if there’s demand for a new offering before investing in SEO content.
  5. Low-competition keywords. If CPCs are under $5 in your industry, ads can be very profitable.

When SEO Makes More Sense

  1. Established business. You’ve been around 2+ years and want to grow sustainably.
  2. High CPC industry. If clicks cost $20+, SEO’s long-term value is much better.
  3. Limited budget. $1,000-1,500/month gets real SEO results in NH. Same budget barely moves the needle in ads.
  4. Multiple service areas. Want to rank in Concord, Dover, and Portsmouth? SEO scales better than running separate ad campaigns.
  5. Trust-dependent services. Legal, medical, financial — customers trust organic results more than ads.

The Smart Approach: Both (If Budget Allows)

The best-performing NH businesses we work with use both channels strategically:

The Launch Strategy

  • Months 1-6: 60% budget to ads, 40% to SEO. Ads generate immediate leads while SEO builds foundation.
  • Months 7-12: 40% to ads, 60% to SEO. As organic traffic grows, shift spend toward SEO.
  • Year 2+: 20% to ads (brand defense + seasonal), 80% to SEO. Organic is now your primary lead engine.

How They Work Together

  • Ads inform SEO. Run ads to test which keywords convert best, then focus SEO on those terms.
  • SEO reduces ad costs. When you rank organically for a keyword, you can lower or pause ads for that term.
  • Double presence. Showing up in both ads AND organic results increases total clicks by 25-50%.
  • Retargeting. Use ads to retarget people who found you through organic search but didn’t convert.

Common Mistakes NH Businesses Make

  • Running ads without a good landing page. Sending ad traffic to a slow, ugly website is burning money. Fix the website first.
  • Doing SEO for 2 months, then quitting. SEO is a 6-12 month commitment. Quitting at month 3 wastes everything you invested.
  • Choosing based on price alone. The cheapest option rarely delivers. Choose based on expertise and track record.
  • Not tracking results. Whether ads or SEO, you need to track your metrics to know what’s working.
  • Ignoring Google Business Profile. This is free, it drives map pack rankings, and most NH businesses don’t optimize it properly.

Our Honest Recommendation

If you’re an NH business with a $1,500-3,000/month marketing budget, start with SEO. The math is simply better for local businesses in a market this size.

New Hampshire isn’t New York or LA. The competition is lower, which means SEO results come faster and cost less. A Keene dentist or a Manchester roofer can realistically reach page 1 within 4-6 months with proper optimization.

If you have the budget for both, use ads for immediate leads while SEO builds. Then gradually shift spend toward organic as rankings improve.

The one thing you shouldn’t do? Nothing. Your competitors are investing in one or both of these channels. Every month you wait is a month they get further ahead.
