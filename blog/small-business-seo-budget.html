How to Create an SEO Budget for Your Small Business

Alex Feb 17, 2026 9 min read

"How much should I spend on SEO?" is the most common question we hear from small business owners. The honest answer: it depends on your goals, competition, and timeline. But we can give you realistic numbers and frameworks to make a smart decision.

This guide breaks down SEO budgets for small businesses at every level — from bootstrapped solopreneurs to growing companies ready to invest seriously in growth.

The SEO Budget Framework

Before picking a number, understand what SEO investment buys you:

Monthly Budget What You Get Best For
$0 (DIY)Your own time, free toolsSolopreneurs with more time than money
$300-500Basic optimization, GBP managementNew businesses starting from scratch
$500-1,000On-page SEO, content, review strategyLocal businesses in moderate competition
$1,000-2,000Full local SEO: content, links, technical, GBPSerious growth in competitive markets
$2,000-5,000Aggressive multi-location, content marketingMulti-location or high-competition industries
$5,000+Enterprise-level, multiple cities, paid+organicRegional/national ambitions

For most NH small businesses, $500-1,500/month is the sweet spot where you get meaningful results without overextending. Read our detailed SEO pricing guide for NH businesses.

The $0 Budget: DIY SEO

If you can't invest money, invest time. You can accomplish a lot for free:

Free Actions (High Impact)

  • Set up and optimize GBP — 2 hours, biggest single free action
  • Ask every customer for reviews — 5 minutes per customer
  • Claim free directory listings (Yelp, Facebook, Bing) — 3 hours total
  • Write one blog post per month — 3-4 hours per post
  • Post on GBP weekly — 15 minutes per post

Free Tools

  • Google Search Console — see what searches bring people to your site
  • Google Analytics — track website visitors and behavior
  • Google PageSpeed Insights — check your site speed
  • Google Keyword Planner — basic keyword research

Realistic Timeline

DIY SEO works but is slow. Expect 6-12 months before seeing significant results. Budget 5-10 hours per month minimum. The biggest risk is inconsistency — most DIY efforts fizzle after 2-3 months.

The $300-500 Budget: Getting Started

This budget typically gets you either a freelancer or a basic agency package. What to expect:

What $300-500/Month Covers

  • GBP optimization and monthly management
  • Basic on-page SEO for 5-10 key pages
  • 1 blog post per month
  • Monthly reporting
  • Basic citation building

What It Doesn't Cover

  • Aggressive content marketing
  • Link building campaigns
  • Technical SEO fixes
  • Competitor analysis

Best Use

This budget works best for businesses in low-competition niches or small towns. If you're a specialty business in a smaller NH town (Laconia, Lebanon, Claremont), $300-500 can move the needle. For competitive markets (Manchester, Nashua), it's likely not enough to outrank established players.

The $500-1,000 Budget: The Local Sweet Spot

This is where most NH small businesses should start. You get enough budget for real work without enterprise-level investment.

What $500-1,000/Month Covers

  • Full GBP optimization and weekly posting
  • On-page SEO for all service pages
  • 2-4 blog posts per month
  • Review generation strategy
  • Citation building and cleanup
  • Basic link building (5-10 links/month)
  • Monthly analytics and reporting
  • Keyword tracking

Expected Results

  • Month 1-3: Technical fixes, GBP optimization, initial content. Expect improved GBP visibility.
  • Month 3-6: Rankings start moving up for target keywords. Local Pack appearances for less competitive terms.
  • Month 6-12: Consistent traffic growth. Page 1 rankings for primary keywords. Measurable increase in calls and leads.

ROI Example

A plumber spending $750/month on SEO who gains 10 additional calls per month from organic search — at $200 average per job — generates $2,000/month in new revenue. That's a 2.7x return on their SEO investment, and it compounds as rankings strengthen.

The $1,000-2,000 Budget: Serious Growth

This budget level allows for comprehensive local SEO with meaningful content marketing and link building.

What $1,000-2,000/Month Covers

  • Everything in the $500-1,000 tier, plus:
  • 4-8 blog posts per month targeting keyword clusters
  • Competitive analysis and gap targeting
  • Aggressive link building (10-20 links/month)
  • Multi-location pages if serving multiple towns
  • Technical SEO audit and fixes
  • Schema markup implementation
  • Conversion optimization
  • Landing page optimization

Best Use

This level is for businesses in competitive industries — lawyers, dentists, HVAC, plumbing in mid-to-large NH cities. You're not just maintaining — you're actively outranking competitors.

How to Allocate Your SEO Budget

Regardless of your total budget, here's how to divide it for maximum impact:

Category % of Budget Why
Content creation30-40%Blog posts, service pages, location pages
GBP + reviews15-20%Posts, photo updates, review management
Link building15-25%Outreach, directory submissions, partnerships
Technical SEO10-15%Speed, mobile, schema, crawl fixes
Reporting + strategy10-15%Analytics, keyword tracking, planning

In the first 3 months, shift more budget toward technical and on-page fixes (the foundation). After that, shift toward content and links (the growth engine).

Red Flags: How to Avoid Wasting Your Budget

Not all SEO spending is equal. Watch for these red flags when choosing an agency:

  1. "Guaranteed #1 rankings" — No one can guarantee this. Google controls rankings, not agencies.
  2. Long-term contracts — Good agencies don't need to lock you in. Month-to-month is ideal.
  3. No reporting — If they can't show you what they're doing, they're not doing much.
  4. Extremely cheap ($99/month) — At this price, you're getting automated reports, not real SEO work. An actual human can't do meaningful SEO for $99.
  5. Focus on vanity metrics — If they only talk about domain authority instead of traffic and leads, be skeptical.
  6. No transparency about tactics — You should know exactly what work is being done each month.
  7. Black hat techniques — Buying links, keyword stuffing, or hidden text will get you penalized.

How to Maximize ROI on Every Dollar

  1. Start with the highest-impact actions — GBP optimization and reviews give the fastest returns
  2. Focus on conversion, not just traffic — A website that converts makes every visitor more valuable
  3. Target low-competition keywords first — Win easy battles before taking on hard ones
  4. Combine SEO with other channels — Read our complete visibility guide
  5. Track everything — Know exactly which keywords and pages drive revenue
  6. Be patient but persistent — SEO compounds over time. Month 12 ROI is dramatically better than month 3.
  7. Don't spread too thin — Better to dominate one city than be mediocre across five

The "Free 30-Day Trial" Approach

Still unsure? Many agencies (including us) offer a trial period. Here's why this works:

  • You see actual work being done — not just proposals
  • You can evaluate communication and reporting quality
  • You see early indicators of competence (technical fixes, content quality)
  • Zero risk — if it doesn't feel right, you walk away

A 30-day trial won't show ranking results (that takes months), but it will show you whether the agency is competent, communicative, and doing real work.

Bottom Line

SEO is an investment, not an expense. The right budget delivers returns that compound year after year — unlike ads, which stop working the moment you stop paying.

For most NH small businesses: start at $500-1,000/month, commit to 6 months minimum, and track results monthly. If the ROI is positive (it usually is by month 4-6), increase your investment. If it's not working, either your agency isn't doing good work or you need to adjust strategy.

The worst budget is $0 combined with $0 of your own time. Do something — even DIY work is better than nothing. Your competitors who invest in SEO are capturing the customers who should be yours.
