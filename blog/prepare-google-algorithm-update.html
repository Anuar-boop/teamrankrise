How to Prepare Your Website for a Google Algorithm Update

By Alex Feb 17, 2026 10 min read

Google makes thousands of changes to its search algorithm every year. Major "core updates" roll out several times annually and can dramatically shift rankings overnight. For NH business owners, these updates feel random and terrifying. But here's the secret: sites built on fundamentals don't fear updates — they benefit from them.

Understanding Google Algorithm Updates

Types of updates

Update Type Frequency Impact
Core updates 3-4 per year Broad ranking changes across all queries and industries
Spam updates 2-3 per year Target manipulative tactics (link spam, thin content)
Helpful content updates 1-2 per year Reward genuinely useful content, demote low-quality
Local/Maps updates Periodic Change local pack rankings and map results
Minor daily updates Continuous Small tweaks, usually unnoticeable

The Update-Proof Framework: E-E-A-T

Google's quality guidelines center on E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Every major algorithm update since 2022 has reinforced these principles. If your site demonstrates E-E-A-T, updates help you.

Experience

Does your content show first-hand experience? For a local business, this means:

  • Real project photos and case studies
  • Specific examples from your work ("We recently helped a Manchester restaurant increase foot traffic by 40%...")
  • Details that only someone who does this work would know

Expertise

Do you know your subject thoroughly? Show it through:

  • Detailed, accurate content that goes deeper than surface-level advice
  • Credentials, certifications, or years of experience prominently displayed
  • Answers to specific, nuanced questions in your field

Authoritativeness

Are you recognized in your field? Build this through:

  • Customer reviews that mention your expertise
  • Backlinks from relevant industry or local sources
  • Mentions in local news or industry publications

Trustworthiness

Can users trust your site and business? Ensure:

  • SSL certificate (HTTPS)
  • Clear contact information on every page
  • Privacy policy and terms of service
  • Accurate, up-to-date business information
  • Real trust signals throughout your site

8 Steps to Update-Proof Your Website

1. Audit your content quality

Go through every page on your site and ask: "Would I trust this page if I were looking for this service?" If any page feels thin, generic, or unhelpful, improve or remove it.

  • Every page should have 500+ words of unique, useful content
  • Each page should serve a clear purpose for a specific user need
  • Remove or consolidate pages with duplicate content

2. Improve your Core Web Vitals

Google uses page experience signals as ranking factors. Check these in Google Search Console:

  • LCP (Largest Contentful Paint): Under 2.5 seconds
  • INP (Interaction to Next Paint): Under 200 milliseconds
  • CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift): Under 0.1

Our speed optimization guide covers the specific fixes.

3. Build topical authority

Google increasingly rewards sites that demonstrate deep expertise in a specific topic rather than thin coverage of many topics. For a local business, this means:

  • Cover your niche thoroughly with detailed content
  • Interlink your content to create topical clusters
  • Be the most comprehensive resource for your service + location combination

4. Keep content fresh

Outdated content signals neglect. Set a quarterly review schedule:

  • Update statistics and dates
  • Add new information or services
  • Fix any broken links
  • Refresh meta descriptions and titles if needed

5. Diversify your traffic sources

Don't rely 100% on Google organic. If an update hits, having other channels cushions the blow:

  • Email marketing (owned audience)
  • Google Business Profile (separate from website rankings)
  • Direct traffic (brand recognition)
  • Referral traffic from directories and partners

6. Monitor your rankings consistently

You can't fix what you don't see. Track your rankings weekly so you catch changes early:

  • Use Google Search Console for impression and position data
  • Monitor Google Analytics for traffic changes
  • Set up alerts for significant drops

7. Build a strong backlink profile

Sites with quality, natural backlinks are more resilient to updates. Avoid:

  • Buying links (spam updates target this)
  • Link exchange schemes
  • Low-quality directory spam

Instead, earn links through quality content, local partnerships, and community involvement. See our local backlink strategies.

8. Prioritize user experience

Every update pushes toward better user experience. Ask yourself:

  • Can users find what they need within 3 clicks?
  • Is the site mobile-friendly?
  • Are there annoying popups or intrusive ads?
  • Can users easily contact you from any page?
  • Is the site accessible to all users?

What to Do During a Google Update

If your rankings drop

  1. Don't panic. Rankings often fluctuate during rollout (typically 2 weeks). Wait for the update to complete.
  2. Check the update type. Is it a core update, spam update, or helpful content update? Each targets different issues.
  3. Analyze what changed. Which pages lost rankings? Which queries? Look for patterns.
  4. Compare to competitors. Did they gain what you lost? What are they doing differently?
  5. Make improvements, not reactions. Improve content quality, not keyword density. Add value, don't manipulate.

If your rankings improve

  1. Understand why. Identify which pages gained and what they have in common.
  2. Double down. Create more content following the same quality standards.
  3. Don't get complacent. Rankings gained from one update can shift in the next. Keep improving.

The Local Business Advantage

Here's good news for local businesses: local businesses are naturally more resilient to algorithm updates than national sites. Why?

  • Local intent is stable: People always need local plumbers, dentists, and restaurants. That demand doesn't change with algorithm updates.
  • GBP is a separate system: Your Google Business Profile rankings have their own algorithm that's updated less frequently than organic search.
  • Lower competition: There are only so many plumbers in Manchester NH. National sites have thousands of competitors; you might have 20.
  • E-E-A-T is natural: As a local business, you automatically have real-world experience, expertise, and a verifiable location — exactly what Google wants to see.

The Update-Proof Checklist

Run through this quarterly to stay prepared:

  • All pages have 500+ words of unique, helpful content
  • Site loads under 3 seconds on mobile
  • Core Web Vitals pass in Search Console
  • All content is accurate and up-to-date
  • Internal links connect related pages
  • No broken links or 404 errors
  • GBP is fully optimized and active
  • Reviews are recent and being generated consistently
  • Backlinks are from quality, relevant sources
  • Site works perfectly on mobile devices
  • Contact information is consistent everywhere
  • Analytics and Search Console are monitored weekly
