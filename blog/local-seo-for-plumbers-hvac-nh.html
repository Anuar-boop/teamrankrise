Local SEO for Plumbers & HVAC Companies in New Hampshire

Alex Feb 17, 2026 11 min read

It's 2 AM in January. A pipe bursts in a Manchester basement. The homeowner grabs their phone and searches "emergency plumber near me." The plumber who shows up first on Google gets a $500+ service call. The plumber who doesn't show up gets nothing — and never even knows the call existed.

For plumbing and HVAC companies in New Hampshire, local SEO isn't optional — it's the difference between a phone that rings constantly and one that stays silent. This guide covers every strategy specific to getting plumbing and HVAC companies found on Google in NH.

Why Plumbers and HVAC Companies Need SEO

Home service businesses have the highest search intent of almost any local industry. When someone searches for a plumber or HVAC tech, they need help now. That urgency translates directly into calls and booked jobs.

The numbers tell the story:

  • 97% of consumers search online for local services before calling
  • "Near me" searches for home services have grown 150%+ year over year — see our near-me optimization guide
  • 78% of local mobile searches result in a purchase within 24 hours
  • The average plumbing job is $300-$800; a furnace replacement is $4,000-$8,000
  • One new customer per week from organic search = $15,000-$40,000+ per year in additional revenue

Meanwhile, paid advertising keeps getting more expensive. HomeAdvisor leads cost $15-80 each, Google Ads for "plumber near me" in NH run $25-60 per click, and Angi membership fees keep climbing. Smart budgeting means investing in SEO that builds lasting visibility instead of renting temporary ad placement.

Google Business Profile: Your #1 Priority

For plumbers and HVAC companies, your Google Business Profile is the single most important ranking factor. When someone searches "plumber near me," the Map Pack shows up above all organic results — and that's where most clicks go.

GBP Setup for Plumbing/HVAC

Primary category: Choose the most specific category for your main service. If you primarily do plumbing, select "Plumber" — not "Home Services." If HVAC is your core, use "HVAC Contractor." Check our complete GBP categories guide for industry-specific recommendations.

Secondary categories to add:

  • Plumber + Drain Cleaning Service + Water Heater Installation Service
  • HVAC Contractor + Air Conditioning Contractor + Furnace Repair Service
  • Add "Emergency Plumber" if you offer 24/7 service

Service areas: Since most plumbers and HVAC companies travel to customers, you're a service area business (SAB). Set your service radius to cover the cities you actually serve. In NH, a typical radius covers Manchester, Nashua, Concord, and surrounding towns.

Photos That Get Clicks

Plumbing and HVAC companies often neglect GBP photos, which is a missed opportunity. Upload:

  • Before/after shots — clogged drain → clear drain, old furnace → new install
  • Your truck(s) — builds trust, shows you're a real business with branded vehicles
  • Team photos — customers want to see who's coming to their home
  • Equipment — modern equipment signals professionalism
  • Completed installs — new water heaters, mini splits, bathroom remodels

Aim for 50+ photos on your profile. Companies with more photos get 35% more clicks to their website.

Reviews: The Trust Multiplier

Home services live and die by reviews. A plumber with 150 five-star reviews will always outperform one with 12 reviews, even if the 12-review plumber is technically better. Read our full guide on getting more Google reviews.

Review strategy for plumbers/HVAC:

  1. Ask at the moment of relief — right after you fix the problem, when the customer is most grateful
  2. Text a review link — don't hand them a card; send a direct Google review link via SMS
  3. Follow up next day — "Hi [Name], wanted to make sure your [drain/furnace/AC] is working well. If so, a quick Google review helps us help more homeowners like you."
  4. Respond to every review — mention the specific service ("Glad we could get your furnace running before that cold snap!")

Target: 5+ new reviews per week. In 6 months, you'll have 130+ reviews — likely more than any competitor in your area.

Website Must-Haves for Plumbing & HVAC Sites

Your website needs to do two things: rank on Google and convert visitors into phone calls. Here's what works for home service sites:

Individual Service Pages

Don't list all services on one page. Create dedicated pages for each service:

  • Emergency Plumbing
  • Drain Cleaning
  • Water Heater Installation & Repair
  • Sewer Line Repair
  • Bathroom & Kitchen Plumbing
  • Furnace Repair & Installation
  • AC Repair & Installation
  • Ductless Mini Split Installation
  • Boiler Service
  • Indoor Air Quality

Each page should target its specific keyword ("drain cleaning manchester nh"), include pricing guidance, and have a clear call-to-action. See our guide on creating service pages that rank and convert.

City-Specific Landing Pages

If you serve multiple cities (most NH plumbers do), create pages for each market:

  • "Plumber in Manchester, NH" — mention Manchester neighborhoods, landmarks, common plumbing issues in older Manchester homes
  • "HVAC Repair Nashua, NH" — reference Nashua's climate challenges, typical home sizes, heating costs
  • "Emergency Plumber Concord, NH" — Concord-specific content, not just the same page with the city name swapped

Each city page needs unique content. Google penalizes thin duplicate pages with just the city name changed. Include local details — mention neighborhoods, landmarks, and specific challenges homeowners face in that area.

Conversion Elements

Your website visitors need to call you in seconds, not minutes:

  • Click-to-call phone number — large, visible, on every page. On mobile, it should be one tap
  • 24/7 emergency badge — if you offer emergency service, make it impossible to miss
  • Trust badges — licensed, insured, bonded. NH plumber license number visible
  • "Call now" sticky bar — fixed to bottom of screen on mobile
  • Response time promise — "On-site within 60 minutes" or "Same-day service guaranteed"
  • Pricing transparency — even ballpark ranges ("drain cleaning starting at $149") reduce friction

Make sure your site loads fast — a homeowner with a flooded basement isn't waiting 8 seconds for your page to load. They'll hit back and call the next result. Read our speed optimization guide.

Content Strategy: The Seasonal Calendar

Plumbing and HVAC businesses have massive seasonal search patterns in NH. Your content should match them:

Winter (December – February)

  • "How to prevent frozen pipes in NH" — searches spike during cold snaps
  • "Signs your furnace needs repair" — November through January
  • "Emergency heating repair [city]" — create pages before the season hits
  • "How to winterize your plumbing" — October publish for November traffic

Spring (March – May)

  • "AC tune-up checklist" — publish in March for April searches
  • "Spring plumbing maintenance tips" — melting snow reveals pipe problems
  • "Sump pump maintenance guide" — spring flooding season
  • "Central air vs ductless mini split" — homeowners plan AC purchases

Summer (June – August)

  • "AC not cooling" — July peak searches
  • "Emergency AC repair [city]" — pages should already exist and be indexed
  • "Water heater replacement guide" — year-round but spikes in summer
  • "Outdoor plumbing for deck/pool" — NH homeowners invest in outdoor living

Fall (September – November)

  • "Furnace tune-up before winter" — September/October publishing
  • "How to prepare your home for local winter" — comprehensive guide
  • "Boiler vs furnace: which is right for your NH home" — comparison content
  • "Radiant floor heating cost" — renovation season content

Publish seasonal content 6-8 weeks before peak search demand. Google needs time to index and rank your pages. If you publish a frozen pipe article in January, it won't rank until February — too late.

How to Outrank HomeAdvisor, Angi, and Thumbtack

These lead aggregators often dominate search results for home service keywords. Here's how to compete:

They have authority. You have relevance and locality.

  1. Target hyper-local keywords — HomeAdvisor ranks for "plumber near me" but struggles with "emergency plumber dover nh" or "frozen pipe repair keene nh." The more local and specific, the better your odds.
  2. Dominate the Map Pack — lead generation sites can't appear in the Google Maps 3-pack. That's exclusively for local businesses. Optimize your GBP and you own space they literally cannot compete for.
  3. Build local backlinks — links from NH-specific sites (NH Business Review, local chambers of commerce, town newspapers) carry more weight for local searches than the national links these platforms have. See our guide to getting local backlinks in NH.
  4. Add schema markup — LocalBusiness, Service, and Review schema help Google understand exactly what you do and where. Aggregators use generic markup; yours should be specific.
  5. Create better content — your "drain cleaning in Manchester, NH" page can include specific details about Manchester's older sewer systems, local pricing, and before/after photos of actual jobs you've done in Manchester. HomeAdvisor can't match that.

The goal isn't to outrank them for every keyword — it's to surround them. When someone searches for plumbing/HVAC in your area, your website shows up in organic results, your GBP shows up in the Map Pack, and your name shows up in review sites. That multi-channel presence beats a single HomeAdvisor listing.

Review Generation for Home Service Companies

Reviews are the lifeblood of plumbing and HVAC marketing. Here's a system that works:

The Post-Job Review System

  1. Complete the job and confirm the customer is satisfied
  2. While still on-site: "Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It really helps other homeowners find reliable service." Hand them your phone with the review page open, or text them the link
  3. 2 hours later: Send a thank-you text with the direct review link
  4. Next day: If no review yet, send a polite follow-up
  5. Monthly: Email past customers a review request (batch your last 30 days of jobs)

What to Do About Negative Reviews

Every plumber gets them — the customer who expected a $50 fix for a $500 problem, or the one who blames you for a pre-existing issue. The key:

  • Respond within 24 hours — professionally, empathetically, without being defensive
  • Take it offline — "We'd like to make this right. Please call us at (603) 605-8532 so we can discuss."
  • Never argue publicly — future customers read your responses, not the complaint. Your calm, professional response builds more trust than the negative review destroys.

A business with 200 reviews and a 4.7 rating is more trustworthy than one with 15 reviews and 5.0 stars.

8 Common SEO Mistakes Plumbers Make

  1. No website at all — relying entirely on HomeAdvisor/Angi. You're renting someone else's platform and competing with every other plumber they list.
  2. One-page website — a single page with all services listed. Google needs individual pages to rank for individual keywords.
  3. No Google Business Profile — or a profile that was set up once and never updated. An incomplete GBP is almost as bad as no GBP.
  4. Duplicate city pages — copying the same content and just swapping city names. Google catches this and may penalize all of them.
  5. No reviews strategy — waiting for reviews to come naturally. They won't. You need a system.
  6. Ignoring mobile — 73% of plumber searches happen on mobile. If your site isn't fast and easy to use on a phone, you're losing the majority of potential customers.
  7. No emergency keywords — missing the highest-intent, highest-value searches. "Emergency plumber [city]" should be a dedicated page.
  8. Paying for leads instead of investing in assets — $80/lead x 10 leads/month = $9,600/year renting leads. That same budget in SEO builds an asset you own forever.

Getting Started

SEO for plumbers and HVAC companies comes down to three things: be findable when emergencies happen, look trustworthy enough to get the call, and deliver great enough service to earn the review.

NH's home service market is competitive but not saturated — especially outside Manchester and Nashua. Companies serving Concord, Dover, Portsmouth, and the Lakes Region have real opportunities to dominate their local markets with consistent local SEO work.

Start with the checklist above. Focus on your GBP first — it's free and delivers the fastest results. Then build out service pages and city pages on your website. Add a review generation system. Publish seasonal content before the demand hits.

In 6 months, you'll wonder why you ever paid $80 per lead for something you could generate for free.
