How to Optimize Your Website for Voice Search in 2026

Alex Feb 17, 2026 10 min read

"Hey Google, find a plumber near me." "Siri, what's the best Italian restaurant in Nashua?" "Alexa, who does roof repair in Manchester NH?"

These aren't hypothetical — they're real searches happening right now. Over 50% of adults use voice search daily, and that number is climbing. For local businesses in New Hampshire, voice search isn't the future — it's today.

The good news: optimizing for voice search doesn't require rebuilding your website. It builds on everything you're already doing with local SEO. Here's what to focus on.

How Voice Search Differs from Typed Search

Understanding the difference is key to optimization:

Typed Search Voice Search
"plumber manchester nh" "Who's the best plumber in Manchester New Hampshire?"
"dentist open saturday" "Is there a dentist open on Saturday near me?"
"hvac repair cost" "How much does it cost to repair a furnace?"
Short, keyword-focused Conversational, question-based, longer

Key takeaway: Voice searches are 3-5x longer than typed searches and almost always phrased as questions or commands.

1. Optimize Your Google Business Profile

This is the single most important thing for voice search. When someone asks "find a [service] near me," voice assistants pull results primarily from Google Business Profile data.

Make sure yours has:

  • Complete and accurate business info — name, address, phone, hours (especially holiday hours)
  • Primary category exactly right — "Plumber" not "Plumbing Service" if that's what people search for
  • Secondary categories — add all relevant ones (up to 9)
  • Service area defined for all cities you serve in NH
  • Q&A section populated — add your own FAQs (voice assistants sometimes read these)
  • Regular GBP posts — shows Google your profile is active

2. Add FAQ Schema to Every Page

Schema markup tells search engines the structure of your content. FAQ schema is especially powerful for voice search because it matches the question-and-answer format perfectly.

How to Add FAQ Schema

Add this JSON-LD to any page with questions and answers:

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [{
    "@type": "Question",
    "name": "How much does a plumber cost in Manchester NH?",
    "acceptedAnswer": {
      "@type": "Answer",
      "text": "Most plumbers in Manchester NH charge $75-150 per hour..."
    }
  }]
}

Pages with FAQ schema are 3x more likely to be used as voice search answers.

3. Create Question-Based Content

Voice searchers ask questions. Your content should answer them directly. Here's how to find the questions people ask:

Free Tools for Finding Questions

  • Google's "People Also Ask" — search your service + city and note every question shown
  • Google Autocomplete — type "how to..." or "why does..." + your service keyword
  • AnswerThePublic.com — enter a keyword, get hundreds of question variations
  • Your own customers — what do people ask on the phone or in person?

Content Structure for Voice Answers

Google's voice assistant reads a "featured snippet" — usually 40-60 words. To win that position:

  1. Use the question as an H2 heading — exactly as people ask it
  2. Answer immediately in the first 1-2 sentences (40-60 words)
  3. Then elaborate with more detail below

Example:

H2: How much does an oil change cost in Concord NH?

Answer: A standard oil change in Concord NH typically costs $35-75 for conventional oil and $65-125 for synthetic. Most shops complete the service in 30 minutes or less.

(Then expand with factors that affect price, recommended intervals, etc.)

4. Focus on "Near Me" and Hyperlocal Keywords

76% of voice searches include location-based intent. Optimize for these patterns:

  • "[service] near me" — handled by GBP optimization + NAP consistency
  • "[service] in [city]" — covered by local keyword optimization
  • "[service] open now" — accurate business hours in GBP + schema
  • "best [service] in [city]" — strong reviews + optimized GBP listing
  • "cheapest/affordable [service] near me" — pricing info on your website

Make sure your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) is consistent across all directories. Voice assistants cross-reference multiple sources to verify business information.

5. Make Your Website Lightning Fast

Voice search results load 52% faster than the average web page. Page speed isn't just about user experience — it directly affects whether your page gets selected as a voice answer.

Priority speed optimizations:

  • Compress images (use WebP format)
  • Enable browser caching
  • Minimize JavaScript and CSS
  • Use a CDN (Cloudflare's free tier works great)
  • Ensure your hosting is fast (avoid budget shared hosting)

Target: Under 2 seconds load time on mobile. Test at Google PageSpeed Insights.

6. Write in Conversational Language

Voice search is conversational, so your content should be too. This doesn't mean unprofessional — it means natural.

Stiff / Keyword-Stuffed Natural / Voice-Friendly
"Our plumbing services Manchester NH provide quality plumbing solutions" "We've been fixing plumbing problems in Manchester for 15 years"
"Contact our dental practice for dental services" "Call us to schedule your appointment — we usually have same-week openings"

Read your content out loud. If it sounds robotic, rewrite it. If it sounds like something you'd say to a customer in person, it's right for voice search.

7. Optimize for Featured Snippets

Featured snippets (position zero) are what voice assistants read. To win them:

  • Lists and steps: Use numbered lists for processes — "Step 1: Turn off the water valve..."
  • Definitions: Start with a clear definition — "A French drain is a gravel-filled trench that redirects water..."
  • Tables: Use HTML tables for comparisons — pricing, features, etc.
  • Direct answers: Answer the question in 40-60 words right after the heading

Pages that already rank in the top 10 for a keyword are candidates for featured snippets. Check your rankings and optimize those pages first.

8. Ensure Mobile-Friendliness

Most voice searches happen on mobile devices. If your website isn't mobile-friendly, you're invisible to voice search users.

Quick mobile checklist:

  • Text readable without zooming
  • Buttons large enough to tap (44x44px minimum)
  • Click-to-call phone number prominently displayed
  • No horizontal scrolling required
  • Forms are easy to fill on a phone screen

Voice Search by Device: What Matters Where

Device Data Source What to Optimize
Google Assistant Google Search + GBP GBP, featured snippets, schema
Siri Apple Maps + Google/Bing Apple Maps listing, Yelp profile
Alexa Bing + Yelp Bing Places, Yelp listing
Cortana Bing Bing Places for Business

Action step: Don't just optimize for Google. Claim and complete your listings on Bing Places, Apple Maps, and Yelp to cover all voice assistants. Your citation strategy should include all platforms.

Voice Search Optimization Checklist

Work through this list for your NH business:

  • Google Business Profile complete with all info, Q&A, and regular posts
  • FAQ schema on service pages and blog posts
  • LocalBusiness schema with hours, address, and service area
  • Question-based H2 headings with direct answers (40-60 words)
  • Conversational writing tone throughout website
  • Page speed under 2 seconds on mobile
  • Mobile-responsive design
  • NAP consistency across all directories
  • Bing Places + Apple Maps listings claimed
  • Strong review profile (4.5+ stars, 20+ reviews)
