Internal Linking for SEO:
The Free Strategy Most Businesses Ignore

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 8 min read

Internal links — links between pages on your own website — are one of the most powerful and most overlooked SEO tactics. They’re completely within your control, cost nothing, and can significantly improve how Google understands and ranks your site.

While most local businesses focus on external backlinks, they ignore the links they already control. Here’s how to fix that.

Why Internal Links Matter for SEO

  • Help Google discover pages. Google’s crawler follows links to find new pages. Orphan pages (no internal links pointing to them) may never get indexed.
  • Distribute “link equity.” Pages with many external backlinks pass ranking power to pages they link to internally. A strong homepage can boost service pages through internal links.
  • Establish topical relationships. When your blog post about keyword research links to your content writing guide, Google understands both pages are about SEO — strengthening both.
  • Keep visitors on your site. More internal links = more pages viewed per session = lower bounce rate = positive engagement signal to Google.
  • Guide visitors toward conversion. Strategic links direct readers from informational content toward your service pages and landing pages.

The Ideal Internal Link Structure

Think of your website as a pyramid:

Level 1: Homepage

Links to all major sections (services, about, blog, contact, location pages). This is your most powerful page — it typically has the most external backlinks.

Level 2: Category/Service Pages

Each service page links to related service pages, the homepage, and relevant blog posts. Location pages link to services and the homepage.

Level 3: Blog Posts and Supporting Content

Each post links to relevant service pages (your money pages), other related posts, and location pages where natural.

The rule: Every page should be reachable within 3 clicks from the homepage. Deep-buried pages rank worse.

Internal Linking Best Practices

1. Use Descriptive Anchor Text

Anchor text (the clickable words) tells Google what the linked page is about.

  • Bad: “Click here to learn about our services”
  • Good: “Learn about our local SEO services”
  • Bad: “Read this article for more”
  • Good: “See our Google Business Profile optimization guide”

Include relevant keywords in your anchor text, but vary it. Don’t use the exact same phrase every time you link to a page.

2. Link From High-Authority Pages

Your homepage and most-visited pages carry the most weight. Internal links from these pages boost the linked pages the most.

  • Link from your homepage to your most important service pages
  • Link from your highest-traffic blog posts to conversion pages
  • Link from popular pages to newer pages that need a ranking boost

3. Link Contextually Within Content

Links embedded in body text carry more weight than sidebar or footer links. The surrounding content gives Google context about the link relationship.

  • Link naturally within paragraphs, not in random lists
  • The linked content should be genuinely relevant to what you’re discussing
  • Don’t force links where they don’t make sense

4. Create Content Hubs

A content hub is a “pillar page” (comprehensive guide on a broad topic) that links to multiple related posts, and all those posts link back.

Example hub structure:

  • Pillar: Complete Local SEO Guide for NH
  • Supporting: GBP Optimization
  • Supporting: NAP Consistency
  • Supporting: Local Citations
  • Supporting: Reviews for Local SEO
  • Supporting: Google Maps Rankings

Each supporting page links to the pillar, the pillar links to all supporting pages, and supporting pages cross-link to each other. This creates a dense topic cluster that Google loves.

5. Maintain a Reasonable Number of Links

  • Blog posts: 3-10 internal links per post, depending on length
  • Service pages: 5-15 internal links (to other services, blog posts, location pages)
  • Homepage: Link to all major sections and your top content
  • More links isn’t always better — each additional link dilutes the equity passed slightly

Common Internal Linking Mistakes

  1. Orphan pages. Pages with zero internal links pointing to them. Google may never find them. Check for these regularly.
  2. Only linking from navigation. Nav links are important but carry less weight than contextual body links.
  3. Broken internal links. Links to pages that no longer exist (404 errors) waste link equity and frustrate visitors.
  4. Generic anchor text. “Click here” and “read more” tell Google nothing about the linked page.
  5. Linking to the same page from every post. Linking every blog post to your homepage is wasteful — the homepage already ranks well. Link to deeper pages that need the boost.
  6. No blog-to-service links. Your blog should drive visitors toward your service pages. Every informational post should link to at least one relevant service.
  7. Never updating old posts. When you publish new content, go back and add links from older relevant posts to the new one.

Internal Linking Audit Checklist

Run through this quarterly as part of your SEO checklist:

  • Check for orphan pages (use Google Search Console → Pages → look for “Not indexed”)
  • Verify no broken internal links (use a free crawler like Screaming Frog)
  • Ensure every service page receives at least 3-5 internal links from blog posts
  • Ensure every blog post links to at least 1 service page and 2-3 other posts
  • Review anchor text variety — not all links to a page should use the same text
  • Add links from old posts to any new posts published that quarter
  • Verify homepage links to your top 5-10 most important pages
