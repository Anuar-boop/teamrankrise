NH Seasonal Marketing Calendar:
When to Promote What

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 12 min read

New Hampshire’s four distinct seasons create predictable marketing windows that smart businesses exploit. The plumber who starts promoting frozen pipe services in November beats the one who waits until January. Here’s your month-by-month playbook.

The key principle: promote 4-8 weeks ahead of demand. SEO content takes time to rank, and customers start searching before they buy. Plan ahead to capture early search traffic.

January — February: Deep Winter

Hot Services

  • Heating repair and HVAC emergency service
  • Snow removal and plowing
  • Frozen pipe repair and prevention
  • Indoor renovations (kitchens, bathrooms)
  • Tax preparation (ramps up fast)
  • Gym memberships and fitness (New Year’s resolution crowd)

Marketing Actions

  • Blog post: “How to prevent frozen pipes in NH” or “Winter home maintenance checklist”
  • GBP Post: Promote emergency availability and winter services
  • Email: Post-holiday thank you + winter tips for your customers
  • Start planning: Spring content for March-April (deck building, landscaping, painting)

March — April: Mud Season / Early Spring

Hot Services

  • Spring cleanup (landscaping, yard work)
  • Roof inspections and repair (winter damage)
  • Gutter cleaning
  • HVAC tune-ups (switch from heat to AC prep)
  • Real estate (market heats up)
  • Wedding planning picks up
  • Tax preparation (final push before April 15)

Marketing Actions

  • Blog post: “Spring home maintenance checklist for NH homeowners”
  • GBP Post: Before/after photos of spring cleanup projects
  • Email: “Book your spring service now before the rush” (urgency works here)
  • Reviews: Ask winter customers to leave reviews now — spring searchers will see them
  • Start planning: Summer content for May-June

May — June: Late Spring / Early Summer

Hot Services

  • Landscaping and lawn care (peak season starts)
  • Painting (exterior)
  • Deck building and outdoor living spaces
  • Pool opening and maintenance
  • Pest control (ants, ticks, mosquitoes)
  • Moving companies (school’s ending, people relocate)
  • Restaurant patios open (big for hospitality)

Marketing Actions

  • Blog post: “Best time to schedule exterior painting in NH” or “NH tick prevention guide”
  • GBP Post: Showcase outdoor projects, seasonal menus, new services
  • Location pages: Update service pages with summer-specific keywords
  • Sponsor: Local 5K races, youth sports leagues, community events
  • Start planning: Fall content for September-October

July — August: Peak Summer

Hot Services

  • AC repair and installation (peak HVAC season)
  • Roofing (best weather for installations)
  • Tourism and hospitality (Lakes Region, White Mountains, Seacoast)
  • Boat repair and marine services
  • Septic services
  • Tree services
  • Home inspections (real estate peak)

Marketing Actions

  • Blog post: “Signs your AC needs replacement” or “Best summer activities in [City], NH”
  • GBP Post: Summer project showcases, customer spotlights
  • Email: Summer maintenance tips + booking reminders for fall
  • Social media: Share work photos (great weather = great outdoor shots)
  • Start planning: Winter prep content for November-December

September — October: Fall

Hot Services

  • Heating system tune-ups and furnace inspections
  • Chimney cleaning and fireplace service
  • Gutter cleaning (leaf fall)
  • Winterization (plumbing, sprinkler blowouts)
  • Fall foliage tourism (restaurants, B&Bs, attractions)
  • Back-to-school services (tutoring, pediatric dental checkups)
  • Home staging and real estate (fall market push)

Marketing Actions

  • Blog post: “Fall home winterization checklist” or “Getting your furnace ready for NH winter”
  • GBP Post: Fall maintenance offers, foliage-season specials
  • Email: “Don’t wait for the first freeze — book your furnace tune-up now”
  • Backlinks: Fall is great for sponsoring harvest festivals, Oktoberfest events, charity walks
  • Start planning: New Year content and Q1 promotions

November — December: Early Winter / Holidays

Hot Services

  • Emergency heating repair (first cold snap = phones ring)
  • Snow removal contracts (businesses sign up now)
  • Holiday catering and restaurants
  • Gift shops and retail (holiday shopping)
  • Electricians (holiday lighting installations)
  • Indoor remodeling (people want projects done before holidays)

Marketing Actions

  • Blog post: “Preparing your business for the holiday rush” or “Holiday gift guide [your industry]”
  • GBP Post: Holiday hours, gift cards, seasonal offers
  • Email: Holiday greetings + end-of-year promotions
  • Google Ads: Seasonal campaigns for holiday keywords (high competition but high intent)
  • Year in review: Publish a “Year in Review” post — great for content and customer engagement

Year-Round Marketing Essentials

Regardless of season, these should run continuously:

  • Weekly GBP Posts — Never let your profile go inactive
  • Review collection — Ask every customer, every time
  • Monthly email newsletter — Stay top of mind
  • Monthly analytics check — Track what’s working
  • Ongoing SEO — Content creation, link building, technical maintenance

The 4-Week Planning Rule

For every seasonal campaign:

  1. Week 1: Research seasonal keywords and plan content
  2. Week 2: Create and publish blog posts and landing pages
  3. Week 3: Promote via email, GBP posts, and social
  4. Week 4: Launch paid campaigns if using Google Ads

Start this cycle 6-8 weeks before the seasonal peak. SEO content needs time to index and rank.
