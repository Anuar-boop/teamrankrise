Cleaning Service SEO:
How to Get More Cleaning Clients From Google

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 13 min read

Cleaning service business is a recurring revenue machine. Get 20 customers, each paying $150-300 monthly, and you’ve got $3,000-6,000 in guaranteed monthly income. The problem? Everyone’s entering the market. Low barriers to entry mean high competition. Your website gets buried. Potential customers never find you.

This is where local SEO changes everything. When someone searches “house cleaning near me” or “office cleaning [city],” they’re ready to hire. They’re not researching. They’re calling. Getting your cleaning company on that Google Maps list is the difference between thriving and starving.

This is your complete guide to ranking on Google as a cleaning company—residential, commercial, carpet, move-out, post-construction, or specialized services. Whether you run a solo operation or manage a team, these strategies work across all service types and markets.

Why Cleaning Services Need SEO Right Now

Let’s break down why local SEO is non-negotiable for cleaning companies:

The Search Behavior Gap

Customers searching for cleaning services have specific intent. They want to hire someone this week. Consider:

  • “House cleaning near me” gets 15,000+ monthly searches nationally. That’s 15,000 people actively looking for a cleaner.
  • “Office cleaning [city]” gets thousands of searches per month in major markets. Office managers and facility directors are searching right now.
  • “Carpet cleaning [city]” and “Move-out cleaning” have strong search volume with high intent.
  • Mobile searches dominate. People search on their phones during the workday. If you’re not in Google Maps, they hire someone else.

The Recurring Revenue Model

Unlike one-time services, cleaning generates recurring revenue:

  • Residential customer books weekly/bi-weekly: $600-1,200/month per customer
  • Office/commercial contract: $1,000-5,000/month or more
  • Average customer lifetime: 2-3 years
  • Customer Lifetime Value: $15,000-40,000+ per acquisition

This means each organic lead from Google can be worth $10,000-30,000 in total revenue. Even if SEO costs $2,000-5,000 to set up, the ROI is extraordinary.

The Competitive Pressure

Cleaning services have low barriers to entry. Anyone with a mop and vacuum can start. This creates intense local competition. Your strategy:

  • Win through visibility, not price. Rank #1 and customers call you before competitors.
  • Build moat with reviews and reputation. Once you have 50+ reviews, competitors struggle to catch up.
  • Own the Google Maps 3-pack. The top 3 results get 80% of clicks. You need to be there.

Google Business Profile Optimization for Cleaning Companies

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is your most important ranking asset. It’s where Google sends customers first.

Claiming and Verifying Your GBP

  • Go to business.google.com and claim your business
  • If claimed, verify the existing profile or request management access
  • Google sends a postcard to verify. This takes 1-2 weeks
  • While waiting, get 50% of your profile completed

Choosing the Right GBP Categories

Categories are crucial. Choose the most specific one:

  • Primary Category: “House cleaning service” (most specific for residential)
  • Primary Category: “Commercial cleaning service” (for office/commercial)
  • Primary Category: “Carpet cleaning service” (if carpet is your focus)
  • Primary Category: “Janitorial service” (for office/building maintenance)

See our complete GBP categories guide for all available options for cleaning.

Secondary Categories to Add

  • Maid service
  • Janitor service
  • Move-out cleaning
  • Post-construction cleaning
  • Window cleaning service (if applicable)
  • Upholstery cleaning
  • Carpet cleaning

Complete GBP Profile Checklist

  • Business name: Exact legal name (no keyword stuffing like “Best House Cleaning NYC”)
  • Phone and address: Accurate and consistent across web. NAP consistency is critical
  • Business hours: Include all availability (7 AM-6 PM weekdays, Saturday 9-4, etc.)
  • Business description (750 characters): “Professional house cleaning and commercial cleaning services for [service area]. Residential deep cleaning, office maintenance, move-out cleaning, and carpet cleaning. Fully bonded and insured. Eco-friendly products available. Free estimate by phone.”
  • Services list: Add individual services with descriptions:
    • Regular house cleaning (weekly/bi-weekly)
    • Deep cleaning (quarterly/annual)
    • Move-in/move-out cleaning
    • Office cleaning and janitorial
    • Carpet cleaning
    • Post-construction cleanup
    • Window cleaning
  • 30+ photos: Before/after cleaning photos, team photos, office, van, equipment, testimonials. More photos = higher rankings
  • Video: 15-30 second intro or team video (optional but boosts engagement)
  • Google Posts: Weekly updates (special offer, cleaning tips, seasonal maintenance, new service)
  • Q&A section: Answer common questions before customers ask:
    • “What products do you use?”
    • “Are you bonded and insured?”
    • “What areas do you serve?”
    • “How much does house cleaning cost?”
    • “Do you do one-time or recurring cleaning?”
  • Attributes: Mark yourself as “Women-owned,” “Eco-friendly,” “Bonded and insured,” etc.

Residential vs. Commercial Landing Pages: Different Customers, Different Keywords

Residential and commercial cleaning customers search differently. They have different needs, budgets, and timelines.

Residential Cleaning Page Strategy

Target keywords: “House cleaning near me,” “House cleaning [city],” “Weekly house cleaning,” “Deep cleaning service,” “Move-out cleaning.”

Page focus:

  • Emphasize convenience and time-saving (“Get your Saturdays back”)
  • Trust signals: background checks, bonding, insurance
  • Flexible scheduling (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly, one-time)
  • Eco-friendly options (many homeowners care about this)
  • Before/after photos of actual homes
  • Pricing transparency (“$150-250 for a 2,000 sq ft home”)
  • Customer testimonials from homeowners

Commercial/Office Cleaning Page Strategy

Target keywords: “Office cleaning [city],” “Commercial cleaning service,” “Janitorial service,” “Building cleaning contractor.”

Page focus:

  • Professional reliability (24/7, after-hours, never miss a day)
  • OSHA compliance, EPA-approved products
  • Reference accounts and case studies
  • Customized service plans (tailored to facility type: office, retail, warehouse, medical)
  • Uniform, badged teams
  • Insurance and bonding (commercial levels)
  • Recurring contracts with guaranteed performance

Create separate pages for residential and commercial. Use different copy, keywords, and CTAs. Link between them: “We also serve [businesses/homeowners].”

Service Pages That Rank and Convert

Each major service needs its own page with 500-1,000 words, photos, pricing, and trust signals.

Essential Service Pages

1. Regular House Cleaning (Weekly/Bi-Weekly)

  • Keywords: “Weekly house cleaning [city],” “Bi-weekly cleaning service”
  • Focus: Recurring value, consistency, getting time back
  • Pricing: Transparent pricing for homes by square footage
  • CTA: “Book Your First Cleaning”

2. Deep Cleaning

  • Keywords: “Deep cleaning service [city],” “House deep clean”
  • Focus: What’s included (baseboards, inside fridge, ceiling fans, window sills, etc.)
  • Use cases: Quarterly, seasonal, or as needed
  • Pricing: Usually 2-3x regular cleaning price

3. Move-In/Move-Out Cleaning

  • Keywords: “Move-out cleaning [city],” “End of lease cleaning,” “Move-in cleaning”
  • Focus: Deposit recovery, inspection-ready standards
  • Pain point: Tenants stressed about losing security deposit
  • Pricing: Usually a flat rate or higher hourly rate

4. Office/Janitorial Cleaning

  • Keywords: “Office cleaning [city],” “Commercial janitorial service”
  • Focus: Professional reliability, after-hours service, uniform teams
  • Include: Restroom, break room, trash, floors, desks, windows
  • Pricing: Monthly contract model

5. Post-Construction Cleanup

  • Keywords: “Post-construction cleaning [city],” “Construction debris removal”
  • Focus: Heavy-duty cleanup, specialized equipment, insurance
  • Pain point: Contractors need fast turnover for final inspection
  • Price: Premium (this is skilled, labor-intensive work)

6. Carpet Cleaning

  • Keywords: “Carpet cleaning [city],” “Professional carpet cleaning”
  • Focus: Steam cleaning, stain removal, odor elimination
  • Include: Upholstery cleaning, area rugs, tile/grout
  • Pricing: Per room or square footage

On each service page, include:

  • Clear value proposition
  • What’s included in the service
  • Before/after photos
  • Customer testimonials
  • Pricing (be transparent)
  • Clear CTA: “Get a Free Quote” or “Book Now”
  • Internal links to related pages

Building Trust: Background Checks, Insurance, and Bonding

Cleaning services enter homes and offices. Trust is everything. Customers want proof you’re legitimate and safe.

Your Trust Signals Checklist

  • Background checks: Mention that all team members are background-checked. This is huge for residential customers.
  • Bonding: You’re covered if something gets damaged or stolen. Customers need this peace of mind.
  • Insurance: List your insurance carrier and policy limits. Include on your website.
  • Certifications: Green cleaning certifications, OSHA training, etc.
  • Years in business: “Serving [city] since 2015” builds credibility.
  • Licenses: If your state requires cleaning business licenses, display them.
  • Create a dedicated “Trust & Safety” page or section

On your homepage, add a trust badge: “Bonded & Insured” or “Background Checked Team.” This appears in Google Results and GBP, boosting click-through rate.

Reviews: Your Ranking Powerhouse and Social Proof

Google reviews directly impact rankings and conversion rates. For cleaning services, reviews are crucial because trust is your main barrier to conversion.

Your Systematic Review Strategy

Timing: After First Cleaning (Day 1-3)

  • Text the customer a review link within 24 hours: “Thanks for choosing us! Please leave a review: [Google review link]”
  • Make it easy: Use a shortened URL or QR code on your invoice
  • Include the link in follow-up emails and thank-you cards

Timing: After Recurring Service Starts (Month 1-3)

  • After 3-5 successful cleanings, customers are most satisfied
  • Send a second review request: “We love working with you! Please share your experience”
  • This captures customers who are now confident (not just first-impression)

Multi-Platform Strategy

  • Google (primary ranking factor)
  • Yelp (high-traffic directory)
  • Facebook (local customers see this)
  • Thumbtack (if you use their platform)
  • HomeAdvisor (trusted for home services)

Response Strategy

  • Respond to ALL reviews (positive and negative)
  • Positive: “Thank you for the 5 stars! We can’t wait to clean for you again.”
  • Negative: “We’re sorry to hear that. Please call us at [phone] so we can make it right.”
  • Never ignore or get defensive on negative reviews

Target: 50+ reviews within first year

You don’t need 1,000 reviews. 50-100 reviews at 4.5+ stars is a strong competitive advantage. Most cleaning companies have 5-20 reviews. You’ll own the market.

Local Citations: Tell Google You Exist in Multiple Places

Local citations are directory listings with your business info. They tell Google: “This business is real and operates in this area.”

Tier 1 Citations (Must Claim)

  • Google Business Profile (already done)
  • Apple Maps
  • Bing Places
  • Yelp
  • Facebook Business Page

Tier 2 Citations (Cleaning-Specific Directories)

  • Thumbtack: Get leads and visibility boost
  • HomeAdvisor: Trusted home services directory
  • Nextdoor: Extremely powerful for local neighborhoods. Residential customers check Nextdoor first.
  • BBB (Better Business Bureau): Trust signal, especially for commercial clients
  • Handy: Platform for cleaning and handyman services
  • Angi (Angie’s List): Verified reviews and high trust

Citation Best Practices

  • NAP Consistency: Name, address, phone must be identical across all platforms. One typo ruins the benefit.
  • Website URL consistency: Use www or non-www consistently (not both)
  • Update quarterly: Check that your info is correct everywhere
  • Claim all duplicate listings: Scammers sometimes create fake listings of your business
  • Get backlinks from citations: Use citation URLs in your on-site link strategy

Nextdoor is especially powerful for cleaning. Get testimonials and reviews on Nextdoor. Many neighborhoods have active groups where people ask for recommendations.

Pricing Page SEO: Transparency Drives Conversions

People search “house cleaning prices [city]” before calling. Having a transparent pricing page ranks and converts.

Your Pricing Page Strategy

  • Create a dedicated pricing page: Not buried in a service page
  • Price by service type:
    • Regular cleaning: $150-300 per visit (depending on home size)
    • Deep cleaning: $300-600
    • Move-out cleaning: $400-1,000+
    • Office cleaning: $500-2,000/month on contract
  • Price by home size: “1,000 sq ft: $150 | 1,500 sq ft: $200 | 2,000 sq ft: $250”
  • Pricing frequency: Show weekly, bi-weekly, monthly rates and discounts
  • What’s included: List exactly what’s cleaned
  • What’s NOT included: External carpet cleaning, pressure washing, organizing, etc.
  • Additional services: Upcharge for carpet, windows, inside fridge, etc.
  • CTA: “Get a Custom Quote” with a form

Keywords to target on pricing page: “house cleaning cost [city],” “house cleaning prices,” “how much does cleaning cost,” “office cleaning rates.”

Transparency builds trust and reduces call volume from price shoppers who can’t afford you. You’ll get more qualified leads.

Publish 2-3 blog posts per month. Link them to service pages and GBP. These posts rank for long-tail keywords and drive steady traffic.

Service Area Pages: Rank in Multiple Cities

If you serve multiple cities, create location-specific pages. This tells Google you operate in those areas and helps you rank locally.

Service Area Page Strategy

  • Create one page per service area: /house-cleaning-chicago.html, /office-cleaning-dallas.html, etc.
  • Customize each page: Include city name, neighborhoods you serve, local landmarks, local testimonials
  • Don’t duplicate: Each page should have unique content. Don’t just swap city names.
  • Link between pages: “We also serve [neighboring cities]” with links
  • Use schema markup: LocalBusiness schema with service areas listed

For example, your Chicago page might say: “We serve residential and commercial clients in Chicago, Evanston, Oak Park, and surrounding suburbs. Our typical response time is 48-72 hours for one-time cleanings and next-day service for recurring clients.”

Tracking ROI: Measure What Matters

You need to know if your SEO efforts generate actual paying customers. Generic metrics don’t matter.

Your Key Metrics

  • Phone calls from organic search: Ask every customer: “How did you find us?” Track Google/organic responses.
  • Form submissions: Track quote requests and contact form fills from organic traffic
  • GBP actions: Monitor your Google Business Profile Insights monthly
    • Phone clicks
    • Direction requests
    • Website visits
    • Search impressions
  • Keyword rankings: Track top 10-15 keywords monthly (pick your highest-intent keywords)
  • Organic traffic: Use Google Analytics 4 to track sessions, session duration, bounce rate
  • Bookings from organic: The ultimate metric. Track how many customers and jobs came from organic search

ROI Calculation

Let’s say:

  • Customer Lifetime Value: $8,000 (recurring cleaning for 2-3 years)
  • Organic customers per month: 2
  • Monthly organic revenue: 2 × $400 (average cleaning fee) = $800
  • Annual organic revenue: $9,600
  • Year 1 SEO investment: $5,000 (tools, maybe some freelance help)
  • ROI: 192% in Year 1 alone

By Year 2, SEO costs drop (mostly maintenance) but customer acquisition continues. ROI compounds.

When to Hire Professional Help

You can do SEO yourself. Consider professional help if:

  • You’re too busy running the cleaning business (SEO takes 5-10 hours/month)
  • You’re not ranking after 3 months of effort
  • Your competitors are heavily invested in SEO (you need to catch up fast)
  • You serve 5+ cities and need coordinated multi-location strategy
  • You want faster results and can invest $1,000-2,000 per month

If you hire an agency, ensure they:

  • Focus on local SEO (not national rankings)
  • Have experience with cleaning companies
  • Show case studies or client results (real businesses, real growth)
  • Report on actual leads and bookings (not vanity metrics)
  • Don’t make unrealistic guarantees (“#1 ranking guaranteed” is a red flag)
