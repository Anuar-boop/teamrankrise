How to Get Your Business Listed on Google Maps

Alex Feb 17, 2026 10 min read

When someone searches "plumber near me" or "best restaurant in Manchester NH," the first thing they see isn't a website — it's the Google Maps 3-pack. Those three businesses with the map, reviews, and phone number get the majority of clicks.

If your NH business isn't on Google Maps, you're invisible to local searchers. This guide walks you through every step — from creating your listing to ranking in that coveted top 3.

Step 1: Check If You Already Have a Listing

Before creating a new listing, check if one already exists:

  1. Go to Google Maps (maps.google.com)
  2. Search for your exact business name + city
  3. If you see your business, click "Claim this business" or "Own this business?"
  4. If nothing shows up, you'll create a new listing in Step 2

Important: Google sometimes creates listings automatically from directory data. Claiming an existing listing is better than creating a duplicate — duplicate listings can hurt your Maps ranking.

Step 2: Create Your Google Business Profile

Google Maps listings are managed through Google Business Profile (GBP). Here's the quick version:

  1. Go to business.google.com
  2. Click "Manage now" and sign in with a Google account
  3. Enter your business name exactly as it appears in the real world
  4. Choose your primary category (be specific — "Plumber" not "Home Services")
  5. Enter your address or define your service area
  6. Add phone number and website URL
  7. Complete verification (see Step 3)

Address vs. Service Area

Business Type What to Choose Example
Customers visit you Show your address Restaurant, dentist, retail store
You go to customers Service area only (hide address) Plumber, electrician, landscaper
Both Show address + define service area Auto shop that also offers mobile service

Step 3: Verify Your Business

Google needs to confirm you're a real business at that location. Verification methods:

  • Postcard (most common): Google mails a postcard with a 5-digit code to your business address. Takes 5-14 days
  • Phone: Automated call or text with a verification code (available for some businesses)
  • Email: Verification code sent to your business email
  • Video: Record a video showing your business location, signage, and equipment

Don't: Change your business name or address while waiting for verification — it resets the process.

Step 4: Complete Your Profile (100%)

A complete profile ranks better than an incomplete one. Fill out everything:

  • Business description (750 characters max) — include your services and service area naturally
  • Business hours — including special hours for holidays
  • Photos — exterior, interior, team, and work/product photos. Businesses with photos get 42% more direction requests
  • Services/Products — list every service you offer with descriptions
  • Attributes — accessibility, payment methods, amenities (check all that apply)
  • Opening date — adds credibility if you've been in business for years

Step 5: Get Reviews

Reviews are the #1 factor for ranking in the Maps 3-pack. You need:

  • Quantity: More reviews than competitors (aim for 50+ to start)
  • Quality: High star rating (4.5+ stars ideal)
  • Recency: New reviews coming in regularly, not a burst then nothing
  • Responses: Respond to every review — it's a ranking signal

See our complete guide on how to get more Google reviews.

Step 6: Build Local Citations

Citations are mentions of your business name, address, and phone number on other websites. They help Google verify your business is real and located where you say.

Start with these directories:

  • Yelp — high authority, often appears in search results
  • Facebook Business Page
  • Apple Maps (via Apple Business Connect)
  • Bing Places
  • Yellow Pages / YP.com
  • Better Business Bureau
  • Your local Chamber of Commerce
  • Industry-specific directories (Angi, HomeAdvisor, Healthgrades, etc.)

Critical: Your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) must be identical across every listing. Even small differences (St. vs Street) can confuse Google.

Step 7: Optimize for the Maps 3-Pack

Once your listing is live and verified, optimize for higher rankings:

  1. Post weekly — GBP Posts signal activity to Google
  2. Add photos regularly — at least 1 new photo per week
  3. Answer Q&A — add your own FAQs to the Q&A section
  4. Use Google's messaging — enable the chat feature for direct customer contact
  5. Optimize your website — your website's local SEO signals directly affect your Maps ranking
  6. Add LocalBusiness schema to your website

Common Google Maps Mistakes

  • Keyword stuffing your business name: Your GBP name should be your real business name. "John's Plumbing — Best Emergency Plumber in Manchester NH" will get you suspended
  • Using a PO Box or virtual office: Google requires a real physical location for businesses that serve customers at their address
  • Creating multiple listings for one location: Duplicate listings dilute your authority and can result in suspension
  • Ignoring reviews: Not responding to reviews (especially negative ones) hurts rankings
  • Inconsistent information: If your hours on Google don't match your website, it creates confusion and erodes trust
  • No website link: Having a website that links to your GBP (and vice versa) strengthens both
