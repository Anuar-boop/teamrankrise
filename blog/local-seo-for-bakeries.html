Bakery SEO: How to Get More
Orders and Foot Traffic From Google

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 12 min read

Running a bakery isn’t just about great recipes. Your customers need to find you online. Whether they’re searching for “wedding cakes near me,” “artisan bread,” or “custom pastries Manchester NH,” SEO determines if they find you or your competitor. Here’s everything you need to know to dominate local bakery search.

Why Bakery SEO Is Different (and Critical)

Bakeries are visual, local, and seasonal. Unlike generic service businesses, your marketing must showcase beautiful food photography, emphasize location convenience, and capitalize on trends (wedding season, holiday baking). Plus, you’re competing not just with independent bakeries, but with grocery store bakery departments that have massive corporate backing.

Local SEO changes the game. It puts your actual bakery storefront in front of hungry customers at the exact moment they’re searching. The payoff: foot traffic, online orders, and custom order inquiries.

Step 1: Optimize Your Google Business Profile for Bakeries

Your Google Business Profile is your foundation. It appears in Google Maps, local search results, and the knowledge panel. For bakeries, GBP is where 60% of your customers discover you.

Create or Claim Your Bakery Listing

  1. Go to business.google.com
  2. Search for your bakery name (it may already exist — claim it rather than creating a duplicate)
  3. Enter your exact business name, address, and phone number
  4. Verify your listing (postcard, phone, or video method)

Complete Every GBP Field

Business description (750 characters): Don’t waste this space. Write something like:

“Family-owned artisan bakery in Manchester, NH specializing in wedding cakes, custom desserts, fresh-baked bread, and pastries. We use local ingredients, create custom orders for weddings and events, and offer daily delivery. Award-winning chocolate croissants and seasonal specialties. Visit our storefront on Elm Street or order online for pickup.”

Business hours: Update for holidays and special events. Closed on holiday? Update it immediately.

Services/Products menu: List all your offerings with descriptions:

  • Wedding cakes ($150-$800)
  • Custom cakes (minimum $50)
  • Artisan bread (daily baking)
  • Pastries and croissants
  • Catering and event desserts
  • Gluten-free options
  • Online ordering and local delivery

Photos (15-30 minimum): This is make-or-break for bakeries. Add:

  • Storefront exterior (clean, inviting)
  • Inside counter display
  • Hero shots: 3-4 of your best products (wedding cakes, bread, pastries)
  • Process photos: baking, decorating, custom order in progress
  • Team photos (people trust faces)
  • Seasonal specials (holiday cookies, graduation cakes)
  • Catering spreads and event setups
  • Customer deliveries

Attributes: Check all that apply (women-owned, family-owned, takes custom orders, has online ordering, wheelchair accessible, etc.)

Step 2: Build Keyword-Optimized Product Pages

Your GBP listing gets customers to find you, but product pages close the sale. Each major product category should have its own optimized page on your website.

Wedding Cakes Page

Target: “wedding cakes Manchester NH,” “custom wedding cakes,” “wedding cake designs.”

Include:

  • Hero image of your most stunning wedding cake
  • Explanation of your design process and customization options
  • Flavor options, fillings, and frosting choices
  • Price range and lead time (weddings book 6-12 months ahead)
  • Process: consultation, tasting, deposit, final details
  • Portfolio: 12-15 photos of past wedding cakes
  • Testimonials from brides
  • “Book a consultation” CTA with calendar link

Custom Cakes Page

Target: “custom cakes Manchester,” “birthday cakes,” “graduation cakes,” “custom cake designs.”

This is for everyday customers, not just weddings:

  • Birthday cakes (same-day available)
  • Graduation and milestone cakes
  • Corporate event cakes
  • School and sports team cakes
  • Photo cakes and printable designs
  • Minimum order size and pricing
  • “Order online” or “Call for custom quote”

Artisan Bread Page

Target: “artisan bread Manchester NH,” “fresh baked bread,” “sourdough,” “ciabatta.”

  • List bread varieties (sourdough, focaccia, ciabatta, rye, etc.)
  • Daily availability and baking schedule
  • Ingredients and techniques (slow fermentation, wood oven, etc.)
  • Photos of fresh-baked loaves
  • Wholesale availability for restaurants/cafes

Pastries & Croissants Page

Target: “croissants Manchester,” “fresh pastries,” “french bakery NH.”

  • Showcase your bestsellers
  • Daily availability and best times to visit
  • Seasonal offerings
  • Wholesale and bulk order options

Catering & Custom Orders Page

Target: “bakery catering Manchester,” “event desserts,” “corporate catering.”

  • Minimum orders (event type, guest count)
  • Custom menu options
  • Setup and delivery options
  • Lead time requirements
  • Case studies: corporate events, weddings, large gatherings
  • “Request a quote” form with calendar

Step 3: Master Food Photography (Your Secret Weapon)

For bakeries, photography is everything. 87% of customers make purchase decisions based on visuals. This is your most important ranking and conversion factor.

What to Photograph

  • Hero products: Your absolute best 5-7 items. Focus on signature items that set you apart from grocery stores.
  • Process: Hands kneading dough, piping frosting, decorating a wedding cake. Action shots humanize your brand.
  • Lifestyle: A cup of coffee next to a croissant. A family enjoying cake. Show the moment, not just the product.
  • Close-ups: 200% zoom on texture. The lamination in a croissant. The grain in artisan bread. The detail in cake decoration.
  • Full spreads: Your entire pastry case, catering display, or custom order lineup.

Shooting Tips

  • Lighting is everything. Natural window light is best. Avoid harsh direct sun or fluorescent overhead.
  • White balance. Bakery lighting is often warm/yellow. Adjust to show true colors.
  • Props: Add context. Use fresh flowers, flour-dusted boards, vintage cake stands, coffee, real plates.
  • Consistency. Develop a visual style. Warm tones, rustic surfaces, consistent styling across all photos.
  • Update seasonally. Holiday cookies in December. Graduation cakes in June. Wedding cakes in spring/summer.

Where to Use Your Photos

  • GBP gallery (20-30 photos minimum)
  • Product pages on your website (3-5 images per product)
  • Homepage hero section
  • Blog posts about seasonal specialties and custom orders
  • Social media (Instagram is essential for bakeries; post 4-6 times per week)
  • Email marketing to your mailing list

Step 4: Create Seasonal Content That Drives Orders

Content marketing for bakeries is predictable and profitable. Customers literally search for the same things every year.

Blog Post Calendar (Seasonal SEO)

January-February: Valentine’s Day cakes, chocolate-themed desserts, New Year’s resolution healthy baking.

March-April: Easter cakes, spring pastries, wedding season begins (search volume spikes).

May-June: Graduation cakes, school event cupcakes, Father’s Day desserts, wedding cakes peak season.

July-August: Summer fruit cakes, ice cream cakes, birthday party planning, corporate event catering.

September-October: Halloween cupcakes and treats, fall pastries, pumpkin everything.

November-December: Holiday cookies, Christmas cakes, gingerbread, catering for corporate parties.

Blog Post Examples

  • “How to Order a Wedding Cake (6-Month Timeline and Planning Guide)”
  • “Best Graduation Cake Designs for 2026”
  • “Holiday Cookie Flavors: 8 Seasonal Favorites From Our Bakery”
  • “Gluten-Free Wedding Cakes: Just as Delicious, Completely Customizable”
  • “Artisan Bread for Corporate Catering: Fresh, Custom, Delivered”
  • “Our Sourdough Process: Why It Takes 72 Hours”
  • “Custom Cake Consultation: What to Expect (and How to Prepare)”

Step 5: Local Citations Drive Foot Traffic and Delivery Orders

Local citations (mentions of your business name, address, phone on other websites) are ranking factors and customer discovery channels.

Critical Bakery Citation Sites

  • Google Maps (your GBP listing)
  • Yelp — Set up a business account, claim your listing, optimize it with photos and details. Respond to every review. Bakeries get tons of Yelp traffic.
  • DoorDash — If you deliver or allow pickup orders, this is a must. Your menu appears in-app, and you get massive visibility.
  • Uber Eats — Same as DoorDash. You may also allow delivery if you’re in delivery zones.
  • Grubhub — Add your bakery if you accept delivery/takeout orders.
  • WeddingWire — Essential for wedding cake providers. Build a portfolio, get reviews, appear in “best wedding cake bakers near me” searches.
  • The Knot — Another wedding planning site where couples actively search for cake vendors.
  • Bing Places — Set up a Bing Business Profile (same info as GBP). Reaches older demographics and Bing users.
  • Apple Maps — Claim and optimize your business listing for Apple user searches.

Key rule: Your NAP (name, address, phone) must be identical across all platforms. Any variation (abbreviations, missing suite number, different phone format) hurts your local ranking.

Step 6: Compete With Grocery Store Bakeries (and Win)

Whole Foods, Trader Joe’s, Safeway, and Shaw’s have massive marketing budgets. But they have one critical weakness: they don’t own their local search presence the way you do.

Your Advantages

  • Specialization. You offer custom wedding cakes; they don’t. Own that niche.
  • Stories. You have recipes, traditions, family history. Share them. People buy from people, not corporations.
  • Local community. Grocery stores are national brands. You’re part of the neighborhood. Emphasize that.
  • Customization. They can’t personalize orders. You can. That’s your moat.
  • Reviews. Ask customers to leave reviews (and mention you in them). Grocery stores can’t compete on authentic customer testimonials.

Tactics

  • Target “custom cake” keywords that grocery stores ignore.
  • Sponsor local events. School fundraisers, community festivals, sports leagues. Post photos and get citations.
  • Partner with wedding planners, event coordinators, florists. They refer customers directly to you.
  • Teach the story. Blog post: “Why Artisan Bread Tastes Better Than Store-Bought (And How We Make It).” Education = authority = preference.

Step 7: Optimize Your Custom Order Page

Custom orders are where you beat the competition. But customers need to understand the process.

Your Custom Order Page Should Include

  • Clear process flow: Step 1: Submit request → Step 2: Schedule consultation → Step 3: Confirm design → Step 4: Deposit due → Step 5: Delivery.
  • Lead times: Wedding cakes (minimum 2 weeks), birthday cakes (3-7 days), same-day options (limited).
  • Pricing guide: Base prices for different cake sizes/complexity levels. Per-person pricing for catering.
  • Customization options: Flavors, fillings, frosting types, design styles (modern, rustic, elegant, whimsical, etc.).
  • Deposits and payment: Are deposits nonrefundable? Do you take credit cards? Payment plan options?
  • Gallery: 20-30 photos of past custom orders. Show diversity of styles.
  • Testimonials: Video or text quotes from customers who ordered custom cakes.
  • Contact CTA: Phone, email, calendar link for consultations. Make it ridiculously easy to request a quote.

Step 8: Track Walk-In vs. Online Orders (Know Your Numbers)

To optimize SEO spending, you need data.

Metrics to Track

  • GBP insights: Google provides free data on clicks, calls, direction requests, and website visits from your GBP listing. Check it weekly.
  • Google Analytics: Which landing pages convert best? Where are your customers coming from? Mobile vs. desktop?
  • Phone tracking: Use a local phone number for your GBP and website. Track calls separately from in-person visits.
  • UTM parameters: When you post links on social media or email, add tracking codes to know which channels drive traffic.
  • Survey question: Simple ask at checkout: “How did you hear about us?” (Google search, Google Maps, social media, friend, walk-by, etc.)
  • Seasonal patterns: Wedding cake orders peak April-June. Identify your own seasonality and plan content.

Step 9: Build Review Authority

Reviews are ranking factors and trust builders. Bakeries with 100+ reviews dominate search results. Here’s how:

Ask Every Customer

  • Verbal request at checkout: “If you enjoyed our croissants, we’d love a Google review. It only takes 30 seconds and really helps.”
  • Printed card in every bag with QR code linking to your Google review page.
  • Email after delivery/catering event: “Thanks for choosing us! We’d appreciate your feedback on Google.”
  • Social media: “Tag us in your photos and leave us a Google review!”

Respond to Every Review

  • Positive reviews: Thank them by name. Be specific about what they loved (“So glad you loved our chocolate croissants!”). Mention something unique about your bakery.
  • Negative reviews: Don’t get defensive. Acknowledge the issue, apologize, offer to make it right. Ask them to reach out privately.
  • Speed matters: Respond within 24 hours. Shows you care.

Step 10: Link Building for Bakeries

Backlinks are a ranking factor. For bakeries, focus on hyper-local, relevant links.

Backlink Opportunities

  • Local wedding blogs and directories. “Best Wedding Cake Bakers in New Hampshire.” Pitch to get included.
  • Food blogger reviews. Send samples to local food bloggers. Ask for an honest review with a link to your site.
  • Local chamber of commerce. Join, get listed, link to their directory.
  • Business associations. Bakery guilds, women-owned business directories, etc.
  • Local media. When you sponsor an event or win an award, ask the local news outlet to link to your site.
  • School and nonprofit fundraisers. If you donate baked goods to a school event, the school may link to your business on their website.

Step 11: Social Proof and Authority Signals

Bakeries live on social media. Use it for SEO.

Instagram (Non-Negotiable for Bakeries)

  • Post 4-6 times per week (daily during peak seasons).
  • Use location tags (tags Manchester, NH bakery, etc. — improves local search visibility).
  • Use hashtags strategically (#ManchesterBakery, #CustomWeddingCakes, #ArtisanBread, #LocalBakeryNH).
  • Share behind-the-scenes: the 4 AM dough mixing, decorating process, team moments.
  • Engage with followers: respond to comments within 2 hours.
  • Link to your website and GBP in bio and post captions.

Facebook

  • Post 2-3 times per week.
  • Join local community groups and engage (without spamming).
  • Run local events (in-store tastings, decorating classes, seasonal specials).
  • Use Facebook Events to announce limited-time offerings.

Step 12: Schema Markup for Bakeries

Schema markup tells Google what your content means. For bakeries, it’s critical.

Implement These

  • LocalBusiness schema: On your homepage (name, address, phone, hours, description).
  • Product schema: On every product page (wedding cakes, bread, pastries). Include image, price, description.
  • Recipe schema: If you share recipes on your blog.
  • AggregateRating schema: Pulls your Google review rating into search results.
  • Organization schema: On your homepage (name, logo, social media profiles, contact info).
