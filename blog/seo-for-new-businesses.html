SEO for New Businesses
How to Rank on Google From Scratch

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 13 min read

You just launched your business. Congratulations. Now comes the hard part: getting customers. You’ve got no reviews, no website history, no Google credibility. But here’s the good news: you can start ranking on day one with the right strategy. This guide covers the first 30 days and beyond.

Days 1-7: Immediate Actions (Priority 1)

Claim Your Google Business Profile Immediately

This is not optional. Go to business.google.com and claim your business today. Ideally, Google hasn’t created a listing yet (since you’re brand new), so you’ll create one from scratch.

See our complete GBP setup guide for step-by-step instructions. But here’s the minimum:

  • Business name (exactly, no keywords added)
  • Local address (real, not virtual)
  • Local phone number (not toll-free)
  • Primary business category (specific, not generic)
  • Website URL (link to your homepage)
  • Verify via postcard, phone, or email

Verification takes 1-14 days. Start this immediately.

Register Your Domain

If you haven’t already, register a domain that includes your business name and ideally your location. Examples:

  • smithplumbing.com (best)
  • smith-plumbing-manchester.com (local keyword, okay)
  • plumbingmanchesternh.com (keyword-stuffed, avoid)

Use a registrar like Namecheap or GoDaddy. Avoid sketchy resellers.

Set Up Google Analytics and Search Console

These free tools are essential for tracking SEO performance.

  • Google Search Console: Shows your rankings, clicks, impressions, and search queries. Go to search.google.com/search-console
  • Google Analytics: Tracks website visitors, traffic sources, and user behavior. Go to analytics.google.com

Add your website to both and verify ownership. These will be your SEO dashboards.

Days 8-14: Website Foundation (Priority 2)

Build or Redesign for SEO

Your website is your SEO engine. It needs to be:

  • Mobile-first responsive. 90% of local customers search on mobile. See our mobile SEO guide.
  • Fast. Under 3 seconds load time. See page speed optimization.
  • Structured data markup. Add Schema.org markup for local business, your address, phone, hours, and reviews.
  • SSL certificate (HTTPS). Non-HTTPS sites don't rank. It's table-stakes.
  • Clear navigation. Users and Google should understand your site structure in 3 clicks.

Use a CMS like WordPress with SEO plugins (Yoast, Rank Math) or hire a developer who understands SEO fundamentals.

Create Your Core Pages

At minimum, you need:

  • Homepage: Clearly state what you do, where you serve, and why customers should choose you. Include local keywords naturally.
  • About page: Tell your story. When did you start? Why? What makes you different? This builds trust and E-A-T.
  • Services page: Each major service gets a dedicated page (or section) with descriptions and why you’re the best choice.
  • Contact page: Prominent contact form, address, phone, hours, map. Make it easy to reach you.
  • Blog: Start a simple blog for content marketing (more on this later).

Week 2: Initial Keyword Research (Priority 1)

Find Your Keywords

You need to know what people search for in your industry and location. Tools:

  • Google Search Console: Shows what keywords you’re already being found for (starting week 2-3).
  • Google Autocomplete: Type your service + location into Google. The dropdown shows real searches.
  • Google Trends: See search volume trends for keywords over time.
  • Free tools: Ubersuggest, AnswerThePublic, KeywordTool.io
  • Paid tools: Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz (Ahrefs and SEMrush have 7-day trials)

Target Keywords by Type

  1. Brand keywords: Your business name (you’ll rank easily for these)
  2. Local keywords: “[Service] [Your City]” (e.g., “plumber Manchester NH”)
  3. Category keywords: “[Service] near me” and “best [service] [area]”

Focus first on category + local keywords. These are the most valuable for your business.

Days 15-21: Building Your Citation Foundation (Priority 1)

What Are Citations?

Citations are online mentions of your business name, address, and phone (NAP) on directories and websites. They tell Google your business is real and where you operate.

Must-Claim Directories

These are non-negotiable for new businesses:

  • Google Business Profile: Already claimed (if you followed step 1)
  • Yelp: Claim your Yelp page at biz.yelp.com
  • Apple Maps: Go to mapkit.js.apple.com/moc
  • Facebook Business Page: Create a business page, not a personal profile
  • Your local Chamber of Commerce: List your business on their directory
  • Industry-specific directories: HVAC contractors go to HVAC directories, dentists to dental directories, etc.

Citation Best Practices

  • NAP consistency: Your name, address, and phone must match EXACTLY across all directories. Smith Plumbing vs. Smith's Plumbing breaks rankings.
  • Use local address: Never use PO boxes, virtual offices, or co-working spaces (unless you have a dedicated private office).
  • Include your website URL: Every directory listing should link back to your site.
  • Use category tags: Select the most specific business category available.

Week 3: Getting Your First Reviews (Priority 1)

Why Reviews Matter for New Businesses

New businesses have zero reviews. This is a huge problem because:

  • Customers don't trust businesses with no reviews
  • Google prioritizes businesses with more reviews
  • Reviews are a major ranking factor for local search

So your goal: get 10-15 reviews in your first 30 days. Here’s how.

Getting Your First Reviews

  1. Ask friends and family first. Not ideal (Google prefers diverse reviewers), but you need some reviews to build momentum.
  2. Offer a small incentive (ethically). “Leave us a review on Google and we'll give you 10% off your next visit.” Don’t pay for reviews; that violates Google’s terms.
  3. Add a link to your email signature. Include a link to your Google review page. Make it easy.
  4. Add a review request to your homepage. A simple “Love our service? Leave us a review!” with links to Google, Yelp, and Facebook.
  5. After every customer interaction, ask in person or via text.”We'd love a review on Google. Here's the link: [link]”
  6. Create a QR code linking to your Google review page. Display it on signage or receipts.

Week 4: Content Strategy and First Posts (Priority 2)

Why New Businesses Need Content

Established competitors have years of content and links. You start with zero. Content is your competitive advantage because you can:

  • Target keywords competitors haven't
  • Answer customer questions they ignore
  • Build topical authority faster
  • Drive social and referral traffic

First Month Content Strategy

  1. Create 4 foundational blog posts.
    • “What is [Your Service]? Complete Beginner's Guide”
    • “[Service] Cost: What to Expect”
    • “How to Choose a [Service] Provider”
    • “[Service] Common Mistakes”
  2. Each post: 1,500-2,000 words. Depth ranks better than short posts.
  3. Target your local keywords. Use keywords in title, headings, and first 100 words.
  4. Link to your service pages. Every blog post should link to your main website pages.
  5. Add schema markup. Use LocalBusiness, Article, and BreadcrumbList schema.
  6. Promote on social. Share every post on Facebook, Instagram, and Google Business Profile.

Publishing Schedule

Start with 1-2 posts per week. Quality over frequency. A well-researched post beats 5 mediocre posts.

First 90 Days: Realistic Timeline

What should you expect?

  • Days 1-30: Claim GBP, set up website, build citations. Few organic visitors. Maybe 5-20 per week.
  • Days 31-60: GBP verified. First 10-20 reviews live. Website ranking for brand keywords. Google sends you 50-150 organic visits per month.
  • Days 61-90: You're appearing in local search for some category keywords. Blog posts starting to rank. 150-400 organic visits per month.

This assumes consistent effort. Skipping weeks kills momentum. See how long SEO takes for full timelines.

First 12 Months: Year 1 Roadmap

By month 12, a well-optimized new business should:

  • Rank in the Google local pack for primary keywords
  • Have 50-100+ reviews (depending on industry)
  • Get 300-1000+ organic website visits per month
  • Appear on the first page of Google for local searches
  • Have published 30-50 blog posts on target topics
  • Dominate branded search (your business name)

Month 1-3: Foundation (GBP, website, citations, first reviews). Limited visibility.

Month 4-6: Momentum (20-30 reviews, 30-50 blog posts). Start appearing in local search for some keywords.

Month 7-9: Authority (40-60+ reviews, 50+ blog posts, backlinks from citations and social shares). Ranking for more keywords. Organic traffic ramping up.

Month 10-12: Dominance (80-100+ reviews, 60+ blog posts, established as local authority). Traffic 3-5x higher than month 1.

Common New Business SEO Mistakes

  1. Skipping GBP. Without a Google Business Profile, you won’t appear in local search. This is the #1 mistake.
  2. Inconsistent NAP information. Smith Plumbing on Google, Smith's Plumbing on Yelp, Smith Plumbing Co. on your website. Google gets confused. Choose ONE name and use it everywhere.
  3. Building a website nobody can find. A beautiful website means nothing if Google can't crawl it or users can't navigate it. Focus on SEO fundamentals first, design second.
  4. No content strategy. Expecting to rank without creating content. Competitors have years of rankings. You catch up with consistent content.
  5. Not asking for reviews. Reviews don't appear magically. You have to ask. Every customer should be asked.
  6. Expecting fast results. SEO takes time. If someone promises top rankings in 30 days, they're lying or using sketchy tactics.
  7. Hiring cheap SEO “experts.” A $99/month SEO company isn't an expert. They're using automation and outdated tactics. Invest in quality, or do it yourself following this guide.
  8. Ignoring mobile optimization. 90% of your customers will find you on mobile. A desktop-only site loses 9 out of 10 leads.

Should You Hire an SEO Agency?

New businesses often ask: “Should I DIY SEO or hire an agency?”

Do it yourself if:

  • You have time to learn and implement (10+ hours/week)
  • You're comfortable with basic technical concepts
  • Your industry has low competition
  • You have a limited budget

Hire an agency if:

  • Your industry is highly competitive (plumbing, dentistry, law)
  • You need results in 90 days (not months)
  • You want expert guidance on strategy
  • You have budget ($1,500-5,000/month for serious results)

Either way, understand the fundamentals. An informed business owner makes better decisions.

Your First 30 Days Checklist

  1. Claim and set up Google Business Profile
  2. Register your domain
  3. Set up Google Search Console and Analytics
  4. Build mobile-responsive website with core pages
  5. Add Schema.org local business markup
  6. Research target keywords
  7. Claim Yelp, Apple Maps, Facebook Business Page
  8. Submit to 20+ local directories (NAP consistent)
  9. Create 4 blog posts and optimize for keywords
  10. Get 10-15 reviews on Google and Yelp
  11. Set up review request system (email, text, QR code)
  12. Share first blog posts on social media

Complete this checklist, and you’ll be ahead of 90% of new businesses on SEO.
