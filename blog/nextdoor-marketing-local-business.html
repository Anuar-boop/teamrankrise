How to Use Nextdoor for Local Business Marketing

Alex Feb 17, 2026 12 min read

Every local business knows about Google, Facebook, and Yelp. But there is one platform that most businesses completely overlook — and it might be the most targeted local marketing channel available: Nextdoor. With over 90 million verified users across 340,000+ neighborhoods, Nextdoor is where your neighbors go to ask "who is a good plumber?" and "any recommendations for a dentist nearby?"

Unlike Facebook or Instagram where you are competing with cat videos and vacation photos, Nextdoor is purpose-built for local conversations. When someone asks for a recommendation on Nextdoor, they are ready to hire. This guide shows you how to take full advantage of it.

What Makes Nextdoor Different

Nextdoor is fundamentally different from other social platforms, and that matters for your marketing strategy:

  • Verified users — Every member must verify their home address. No bots, no fake accounts. These are real people who live in your service area.
  • Hyper-local — Content is organized by neighborhood. You reach people within miles of your business, not random followers across the country.
  • High trust — Recommendations from verified neighbors carry significantly more weight than anonymous online reviews.
  • Intent-driven — Most business-related posts on Nextdoor are people actively looking for services ("Can anyone recommend an electrician?").
  • Low competition — Most businesses have not set up a Nextdoor Business Page. You face far less competition than on Google or Facebook.
  • Recommendation engine — When neighbors recommend your business, those recommendations show up permanently on your Business Page.

Setting Up Your Nextdoor Business Page

Nextdoor Business Pages are free to create and manage. Here is how to set one up:

Step-by-Step Setup

  1. Go to business.nextdoor.com
  2. Click "Claim your free Business Page"
  3. Search for your business (Nextdoor may already have a listing from user recommendations)
  4. If found, claim it. If not, create a new one.
  5. Verify your business through the options provided (phone, email, or postcard)
  6. Complete your profile — every field matters

Optimizing Your Business Page

  • Business name — Use your real business name (same as on Google and your website)
  • Category — Choose the most relevant category. This determines which recommendation prompts your business appears in.
  • Address — Your real business address, matching all other platforms
  • Phone number — Consistent with your GBP and website (NAP consistency)
  • Website URL — Link to your homepage or a dedicated landing page
  • Business story — Write a compelling description. Mention your neighborhood, years in business, and what makes you different.
  • Photos — Upload your logo, team photos, and photos of your work. Businesses with photos get significantly more engagement.
  • Hours — Keep them current

The Power of Nextdoor Recommendations

Nextdoor recommendations are the platform's equivalent of Google reviews — but arguably more powerful for local businesses.

How Recommendations Work

  • Any Nextdoor member can recommend a business they have used
  • Recommendations appear on your Business Page permanently
  • When someone in the neighborhood searches for your service category, businesses with the most recommendations rank higher
  • Recommendations also appear in the news feed when neighbors ask for suggestions

How to Get More Recommendations

  • Ask your customers — After completing a job, ask if they are on Nextdoor and would recommend your business
  • Add it to your follow-up — Include Nextdoor alongside Google when requesting reviews: "If you are happy with our work, we would love a recommendation on Google or Nextdoor"
  • Include a link — Share your Nextdoor Business Page URL in follow-up emails and texts
  • Respond to every recommendation — Thank people publicly. It shows you are engaged and encourages others to recommend you.

Free Marketing: Business Posts

Nextdoor allows businesses to publish free posts that appear in the news feeds of nearby neighborhoods. This is one of the most underused free marketing tools available to local businesses.

What to Post

  • Helpful tips — "5 signs your furnace needs servicing before winter" or "How to prepare your lawn for spring"
  • Seasonal reminders — "Do not forget to schedule your AC tune-up before summer" or "Ice dam season is here — here is what to watch for"
  • Community involvement — "We donated supplies to [local school]" or "Proud to sponsor [local event]"
  • Special offers — "15% off for neighbors this month" (Nextdoor users respond well to neighbor-exclusive deals)
  • Behind the scenes — Team photos, project before-and-afters, milestone celebrations
  • Emergency alerts — "We are available for emergency [service] this weekend" during storms or emergencies

What NOT to Post

  • Hard sales pitches ("Buy now! Limited time!")
  • Posts that feel like ads rather than neighbor-to-neighbor communication
  • Anything controversial or political
  • Too-frequent posts (1-2 per month is the sweet spot for free posts)

Post Best Practices

  • Write like a neighbor, not a corporation. Casual, friendly tone.
  • Include a photo — posts with photos get 3-5x more engagement
  • Ask a question to encourage comments ("What is your biggest [industry] challenge?")
  • Respond to every comment on your posts
  • Share genuinely useful information, not just promotions

Paid Advertising: Local Deals and Neighborhood Sponsorships

Nextdoor offers paid advertising options for businesses that want more reach. These tend to be surprisingly affordable compared to Google Ads or Facebook Ads.

Local Deals

  • Promoted offers that appear in the news feed of nearby neighborhoods
  • You set your budget and targeting radius
  • Pricing is typically cost-per-impression based
  • Good for: seasonal promotions, new customer offers, event promotion

Neighborhood Sponsorships

  • Your business is featured as the sponsor for specific neighborhoods
  • Your logo and message appear at the top of the neighborhood page
  • Builds long-term brand awareness in your target area
  • Good for: building name recognition, establishing authority

When to Use Paid vs Free

  • Start free — Set up your Business Page, get recommendations, and post regularly for at least 2-3 months
  • Test paid — If free posts are generating engagement and leads, test Local Deals with a small budget
  • Scale what works — Track which offers get the most responses and double down

Responding to Recommendation Requests

The most valuable posts on Nextdoor for businesses are recommendation requests. These are posts like "Does anyone know a good landscaper?" or "Need a plumber ASAP — any suggestions?"

The Rules

  • Businesses can respond to recommendation requests in their category
  • Your response appears alongside neighbor recommendations
  • Be helpful and professional — do not be pushy
  • Include your contact info and what makes you a good fit

Effective Response Template

Here is a framework that works well:

  1. Introduce yourself and your business briefly
  2. Mention relevant experience or specialization
  3. Reference your neighborhood connection ("We have served [neighborhood] for X years")
  4. Offer to help with a specific next step ("Happy to give you a free estimate")
  5. Include your phone number or website

Keep it under 100 words. Neighbors appreciate brevity.

Nextdoor and SEO: The Connection

Nextdoor does not directly impact your Google rankings the way Google reviews or backlinks do. But it contributes to your overall online presence in meaningful ways:

  • Citation signal — Your Nextdoor Business Page is another consistent mention of your NAP online, which contributes to citation building
  • Brand mentions — When neighbors mention your business in posts and recommendations, those are brand signals
  • Website traffic — Clicks from your Nextdoor page to your website generate referral traffic, which is a positive engagement signal
  • Review diversity — Having recommendations on Nextdoor alongside reviews on Google, Yelp, and Facebook shows Google your business is mentioned across multiple platforms
  • Local authority — Active participation in neighborhood conversations builds your reputation as a trusted local business

Which Businesses Benefit Most from Nextdoor

Some businesses see better results on Nextdoor than others. Here are the categories that perform best:

Top Performers

  • Home services — Plumbers, electricians, HVAC, landscapers, painters, roofers, handymen. These are the most-requested categories on Nextdoor.
  • Contractors and remodelers — Homeowners constantly ask neighbors for contractor recommendations
  • Pet services — Veterinarians, groomers, pet sitters, dog walkers
  • Auto services — Mechanics, detailers, body shops
  • Real estate agents — Neighborhood expertise is your selling point, and Nextdoor IS the neighborhood
  • Restaurants and cafes — "Where should we eat tonight?" is a common Nextdoor question
  • Child care and tutoring — Parents on Nextdoor constantly ask for these recommendations

Good but Less Active

  • Healthcare (dentists, chiropractors, doctors)
  • Professional services (accountants, lawyers, financial advisors)
  • Fitness (gyms, personal trainers, yoga studios)
  • Retail (boutiques, specialty shops)

Common Nextdoor Marketing Mistakes

  1. Treating it like Facebook — Nextdoor users expect neighbor-to-neighbor conversation, not corporate marketing speak
  2. Posting too often — Over-posting gets you flagged as spammy. 1-2 business posts per month is appropriate.
  3. Being too salesy — Lead with helpful information, not discounts and promotions
  4. Ignoring recommendations — When someone recommends you, respond and thank them. Every time.
  5. Not monitoring recommendation requests — Check Nextdoor regularly for people asking for your type of service
  6. Inconsistent NAP — Your Nextdoor business name, address, and phone must match Google and your website
  7. No photos — Business Pages without photos get far less engagement
  8. Creating a personal account as your business — Use the official Business Page feature, not a personal profile

Getting Started: Your First 30 Days

Week 1

  • Create and claim your Nextdoor Business Page
  • Complete every field with accurate information
  • Upload your logo, team photo, and 3-5 work photos
  • Write a compelling business story

Week 2

  • Ask your 3-5 most loyal customers to recommend you on Nextdoor
  • Publish your first business post (a helpful tip related to your industry)
  • Monitor recommendation requests in your category

Week 3-4

  • Respond to any recommendation requests you find
  • Publish a second business post (seasonal advice or behind-the-scenes content)
  • Thank any customers who left recommendations
  • Consider testing a Local Deal with a small budget

Ongoing Monthly Routine

  • 1-2 business posts per month (helpful content, not ads)
  • Monitor and respond to recommendation requests weekly
  • Respond to all new recommendations within 24 hours
  • Keep hours and contact info current
  • Upload new photos monthly

Bottom Line

Nextdoor is the most underutilized local marketing platform in 2026. While every business fights for visibility on Google and Facebook, Nextdoor offers a low-competition environment filled with verified neighbors who are actively looking for local services.

The setup takes 15 minutes. The ongoing time investment is minimal. And the leads that come from Nextdoor are among the highest-quality local leads you can get — because they come with a built-in trust factor that no ad or search result can match: a recommendation from someone who lives on their street.

Set up your Business Page this week. Ask your happy customers for recommendations. Start posting helpful content. In 90 days, you will have another reliable channel bringing customers to your door.
