Why Online Reviews Are the #1 Factor for Local SEO in 2026

By Alex · Feb 17, 2026 · 9 min read

93% of consumers say online reviews influence their purchasing decisions. For local businesses in New Hampshire, reviews don't just affect whether someone chooses you — they directly affect whether Google shows you at all.

Google's local ranking algorithm weighs three primary factors: relevance, distance, and prominence. Reviews are the biggest component of prominence. More reviews, better ratings, and recent review activity all push you higher in the Google Map Pack.

How Reviews Affect Your Rankings

Google uses reviews in several specific ways:

  • Review count — businesses with more reviews rank higher, all else being equal
  • Average rating — 4.0+ is the threshold where you start getting taken seriously. 4.5+ is ideal
  • Review recency — a business with 10 reviews in the last month outranks one with 100 reviews from 2 years ago
  • Review velocity — steady, consistent reviews signal an active, healthy business
  • Keywords in reviews — when customers mention your services and location in reviews, it reinforces your relevance for those terms
  • Review responses — Google has confirmed that responding to reviews improves local rankings

This is why review management isn't just a customer service task — it's an SEO strategy. We covered specific review-getting tactics in our dedicated guide on how to get more Google reviews.

The Review Platforms That Matter Most

1. Google Reviews (Most Important)

Google reviews directly feed into your Map Pack ranking. This is priority #1. Every effort should start here. Your Google Business Profile is where most customers will see and leave reviews.

2. Industry-Specific Platforms

  • Home services: Angi, HomeAdvisor, Houzz
  • Healthcare: Healthgrades, Vitals, Zocdoc
  • Restaurants: Yelp, TripAdvisor, OpenTable
  • Legal: Avvo, Martindale-Hubbell
  • Automotive: CarFax, RepairPal

3. Facebook

Facebook recommendations show up in search results and influence consumer trust. They're less impactful for SEO than Google reviews but still matter for conversion.

4. Yelp

Yelp reviews appear in Google search results and can rank for your business name. They're especially important for restaurants, retail, and personal services.

The Review Response Strategy

Responding to reviews is as important as getting them. Here's how to handle different scenarios:

Positive Reviews (5 stars)

Always respond within 24-48 hours. Thank the customer by name, reference something specific about their experience, and mention a service keyword naturally.

Good example: "Thank you so much, Sarah! We're glad the emergency plumbing repair went smoothly and that we could get your hot water back on the same day. We appreciate you trusting us with your Manchester home."

Notice the response naturally includes "emergency plumbing repair" and "Manchester" — keywords that help your local SEO.

Negative Reviews (1-3 stars)

This is where most businesses make mistakes. The response isn't for the unhappy customer — it's for the hundreds of future customers who will read it.

  • Respond within 24 hours
  • Apologize for their experience (even if you disagree)
  • Don't argue or get defensive
  • Take the conversation offline ("Please call us at [number] so we can make this right")
  • Show that you care about fixing the issue

A bad review with a thoughtful, professional response actually increases trust for potential customers. It shows you care and take responsibility.

Review Velocity: Why Consistency Beats Volume

Getting 50 reviews in one week and then nothing for 6 months looks unnatural to Google. A steady stream of reviews — 2-4 per month for most local businesses — signals a consistently active business.

Here's a realistic review cadence by business type:

  • High-volume (restaurants, retail): 8-12 reviews/month
  • Medium-volume (home services, healthcare): 3-6 reviews/month
  • Low-volume (professional services, B2B): 1-3 reviews/month

Don't chase numbers — chase consistency. A Dover dentist with 3 new reviews every month will outrank a competitor with 200 old reviews and no new ones.

The Keywords-in-Reviews Advantage

When customers mention specific services and locations in their reviews, it strengthens your relevance for those terms. You can't tell customers what to write (and shouldn't), but you can influence it:

  • Ask specific follow-up questions: "We're glad you liked the kitchen remodel! Would you mind sharing your experience on Google?"
  • Send review requests after completing specific services — the customer naturally mentions what you did
  • Time your requests well — right after the customer expresses satisfaction, not days later when details fade

A review that says "Great plumber in Nashua! Fixed our water heater same day" is SEO gold. It contains the service keyword, the location, and positive sentiment — all in a user-generated format Google trusts.

What NOT to Do With Reviews

  • Never buy fake reviews — Google's algorithms detect patterns and will remove them (or worse, penalize your profile)
  • Never offer incentives for reviews — "Leave a review, get 10% off" violates Google's terms
  • Never review-gate — sending happy customers to Google and unhappy ones to a private form is detectable and frowned upon
  • Never ignore negative reviews — an unanswered 1-star review hurts more than having no reviews at all
  • Never ask employees or family to leave reviews — Google can trace IP addresses and account connections

The penalties for manipulating reviews are severe — your Google Business Profile can be suspended, removing you from Maps entirely. Read about more SEO mistakes to avoid.

Reviews and the Decision Funnel

Reviews don't just help you rank — they convert visitors into customers at every stage:

  1. Search results: Star ratings in the Map Pack make your listing stand out (schema markup helps here — see our schema guide)
  2. Consideration: Potential customers read 5-10 reviews before deciding. Recent, relevant reviews close the deal
  3. Decision: A mix of detailed reviews across different services shows depth and reliability
  4. Post-purchase: When customers see their review responded to, they're more likely to return and refer others

Your Review Action Plan

Start here this week:

  1. Audit your current reviews — how many do you have on Google? What's your average rating? When was the last one?
  2. Respond to all existing reviews — especially unanswered negative ones. It's never too late
  3. Create a review request process — identify when and how to ask satisfied customers (our 9 strategies guide has the playbook)
  4. Set a monthly target — aim for the review cadence appropriate for your business type
  5. Monitor weekly — set up Google alerts or check your GBP dashboard every week

For businesses in Manchester, Concord, Portsmouth, Keene, and across NH — reviews are the single highest-impact activity you can do for your local SEO. No amount of technical optimization can overcome a thin review profile.
