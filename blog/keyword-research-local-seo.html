Keyword Research for Local SEO:
Find the Terms Your Customers Search

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 10 min read

Every page on your website should target specific keywords that your customers actually search for. Guess wrong, and you rank for terms nobody uses. Research right, and you attract people who are ready to buy.

Local keyword research is different from national keyword research. You’re not competing with the entire internet — just businesses in your area. That’s both an advantage and a limitation. Here’s how to find the right terms.

How Local Keywords Work

Local keywords typically follow three patterns:

  1. [Service] + [City] — “plumber Manchester NH”, “dentist Nashua NH”
  2. [Service] + “near me” — “plumber near me”, “dentist near me”
  3. [Specific need] + [Location modifier] — “emergency pipe repair southern NH”

Google automatically adds location context based on the searcher’s GPS/IP, so “plumber near me” from someone in Manchester shows Manchester results. But you still need explicit city keywords on your pages to rank for both patterns.

Step 1: Brainstorm Your Core Services

Before touching any tool, write down every service you offer. Be specific.

Example for a plumbing company:

  • Emergency plumbing
  • Drain cleaning
  • Water heater repair
  • Water heater installation
  • Sewer line repair
  • Bathroom remodel
  • Kitchen plumbing
  • Pipe repair
  • Faucet installation
  • Toilet repair
  • Sump pump installation
  • Frozen pipe thawing (big in NH)

Each of these can become a page or section on your website. The more specific you get, the less competition you face.

Step 2: Add Location Modifiers

Take each service and pair it with every city/town you serve:

  • Emergency plumbing Manchester NH
  • Emergency plumbing Nashua NH
  • Emergency plumbing Concord NH
  • Drain cleaning Manchester NH
  • Drain cleaning Nashua NH

For multi-location businesses, this creates your location page keyword targets.

Step 3: Use Free Tools to Validate and Expand

Google Search (Free)

The best free keyword tool is Google itself:

  1. Autocomplete: Start typing your service in Google and see what it suggests. These are real searches people make.
  2. “People also ask”: The expandable questions in search results show related queries your customers have.
  3. “Related searches”: At the bottom of the results page, Google shows related terms.

Google Keyword Planner (Free with Google Ads account)

  • Shows monthly search volume estimates for any keyword
  • Suggests related keywords you might have missed
  • Shows competition level (useful for Google Ads planning too)
  • Filter by location (set to New Hampshire)

Google Search Console (Free)

If your site is already getting some traffic, Search Console shows exactly what queries people use to find you. This is real data, not estimates. See our SEO tracking guide for setup.

Other Free Tools

  • AnswerThePublic — Shows questions people ask about any topic
  • Ubersuggest (limited free) — Search volume and keyword ideas
  • Google Trends — Shows seasonal patterns (very useful for seasonal businesses)

Step 4: Categorize and Prioritize

Not all keywords are equal. Prioritize based on:

Search Intent (Most Important)

  • Transactional (highest value): “emergency plumber Manchester NH” — they need service NOW
  • Commercial investigation: “best plumber Manchester NH” — comparing options
  • Informational: “how to unclog a drain” — looking for DIY help (still valuable for content marketing)

Competition Level

  • High competition: “plumber NH” — every plumber in the state targets this
  • Medium competition: “plumber Manchester NH” — city-specific, more attainable
  • Low competition: “frozen pipe repair Bedford NH” — very specific, easy to rank for

Business Value

  • High value: Keywords that lead to big-ticket services (remodeling, installations)
  • Medium value: Regular services (repairs, maintenance)
  • Low value: Informational queries (but great for blog content)

Step 5: Map Keywords to Pages

Every important keyword should have a dedicated page. Don’t try to rank one page for 20 different keywords.

  • Homepage: Your main keyword + brand (“Manchester NH Plumber | Smith Plumbing”)
  • Service pages: One page per major service (“Water Heater Installation in Manchester, NH”)
  • Location pages: One page per city (“Plumbing Services in Dover, NH”)
  • Blog posts: Informational and long-tail keywords (“How to Prevent Frozen Pipes in NH Winters”)

This structure ensures you’re not competing with yourself (keyword cannibalization) and each page has a clear purpose.

  • [service] [city] NH
  • emergency [service] [city]
  • [service] company near me
  • [service] cost [city] NH
  • best [service] [city]

  • [specialty] accepting new patients [city]
  • [procedure] near me
  • [specialty] office [city] NH

  • [cuisine type] restaurant [city] NH
  • best [food] near me
  • [cuisine] delivery [city]
  • restaurants open now [city]

Professional Services

  • [service] [city] NH
  • [service] for [audience] [city]
  • affordable [service] near me
  • [service] consultation [city]

Common Keyword Research Mistakes

  1. Targeting only broad terms. “Plumber” is nearly impossible to rank for. “Emergency plumber Manchester NH” is achievable and more valuable.
  2. Ignoring long-tail keywords. “How much does a water heater installation cost in NH” has lower volume but much higher conversion rate.
  3. Not including the state. “Plumber Manchester” could mean Manchester, NH or Manchester, UK or Manchester, CT. Always include “NH.”
  4. Stuffing keywords. Using “Manchester NH plumber” 50 times on one page hurts rankings. Use naturally, 3-5 times per page.
  5. Copying competitors’ keywords. Use competitor analysis for inspiration, but find your own angles.
  6. Never updating. Search behavior changes. Review your keyword strategy quarterly.

Quick-Start Keyword Research Process

  1. List all your services (be specific)
  2. Pair each service with your top 3-5 cities
  3. Google each combination and note autocomplete suggestions
  4. Check “People also ask” for question-based keywords
  5. Use Google Keyword Planner to check volume
  6. Create a spreadsheet: keyword | volume | intent | target page
  7. Prioritize: high-intent + low-competition keywords first
  8. Write or optimize one page per keyword group
