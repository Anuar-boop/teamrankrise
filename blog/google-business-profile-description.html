How to Write a Google Business Profile Description That Ranks

By Alex Feb 17, 2026 8 min read

Your Google Business Profile description is 750 characters of prime real estate. Most businesses waste it with generic copy like "We provide quality services at affordable prices." Here's how to write a description that actually helps you rank higher and converts readers into customers.

Does Your GBP Description Affect Rankings?

Let's address this directly: Google has stated that the business description does not directly impact ranking. However, it matters for two important reasons:

  1. Keyword relevance signals: Google highlights matching keywords in your description when they match a search query. This improves click-through rates.
  2. Conversion impact: A well-written description convinces people to choose you over competitors. More clicks, calls, and visits send positive behavioral signals that do influence rankings over time.

Think of your description as a short sales pitch that happens to include relevant keywords naturally. Not a ranking hack — but a genuine competitive advantage.

Google's Rules for GBP Descriptions

Before writing, know the rules. Google will reject or edit descriptions that violate their guidelines:

Allowed Not Allowed
Services you provide Phone numbers or URLs
What makes you unique Promotional language ("Best deal!" "50% off!")
Service area mentions ALL CAPS text
History and background Offensive or inappropriate content
Specializations Keyword stuffing
Years in business Misleading information

You have exactly 750 characters (not words — characters, including spaces). Google displays the first ~250 characters before a "Read more" link, so front-load the important information.

The Formula: 4 Elements of a Strong Description

Element 1: What you do (first sentence)

Lead with your primary service and location. This is the most important sentence because it appears before the "Read more" cutoff.

Template: [Business Name] provides [primary service] for [customer type] in [city/area], NH.

Element 2: What makes you different (sentence 2-3)

Why should someone choose you? Include your strongest differentiator: years of experience, specialization, certifications, or unique approach.

Element 3: Services list (sentence 3-4)

Naturally list your key services. This is where relevant keywords appear organically.

Element 4: Trust and credibility (final sentence)

End with something that builds confidence: licensing, awards, guarantee, or a simple commitment statement.

5 Real Description Templates

Template 1: Service Business (Plumber, Electrician, HVAC)

[Business Name] provides residential and commercial plumbing services throughout Manchester, Nashua, and southern New Hampshire. With over 15 years of experience, we specialize in emergency repairs, water heater installation, drain cleaning, and bathroom remodeling. Our licensed plumbers arrive on time, explain the problem clearly, and provide upfront pricing before any work begins. Family-owned and fully insured, we've earned 200+ five-star reviews from NH homeowners who trust us to get the job done right.

Template 2: Professional Service (Accountant, Lawyer, Dentist)

[Business Name] is a family dental practice serving patients in Concord, NH and the surrounding communities. We provide comprehensive dental care including cleanings, fillings, crowns, Invisalign, and cosmetic dentistry for patients of all ages. Our modern office features digital X-rays and same-day crowns for your convenience. Dr. [Name] and our team focus on making every visit comfortable, especially for patients with dental anxiety. Accepting most insurance plans and offering flexible payment options.

Template 3: Restaurant or Food Service

[Business Name] serves authentic Italian cuisine in downtown Portsmouth, NH. Our menu features handmade pasta, wood-fired pizzas, and fresh seafood sourced from local suppliers. Family-owned since 2015, we offer dine-in, takeout, and catering for events of all sizes. Our private dining room accommodates groups up to 40 guests. Open seven days a week for lunch and dinner. Reservations recommended on weekends.

Template 4: Retail or E-commerce

[Business Name] is a locally-owned garden center and nursery in Salem, NH. We carry a wide selection of annuals, perennials, trees, shrubs, and organic gardening supplies suited to the New Hampshire growing climate. Our knowledgeable staff provides free planting advice and landscape design consultations. We also offer seasonal workshops, custom container planting, and delivery service within 20 miles. Serving southern NH and northern MA gardeners since 2008.

Template 5: Home Service (Cleaning, Landscaping, Moving)

[Business Name] provides professional house cleaning services for homes and apartments in the greater Nashua and Merrimack Valley area of New Hampshire. We offer recurring weekly, bi-weekly, and monthly cleanings as well as deep cleans, move-in/move-out cleaning, and post-construction cleanup. All our cleaners are background-checked, insured, and use eco-friendly products. Same-week booking available. Trusted by 500+ NH families for reliable, thorough cleaning since 2019.

Keyword Strategy for Your Description

Primary keywords to include naturally

  • Your main service: "plumbing," "dental care," "house cleaning"
  • Your city/area: "Manchester NH," "southern New Hampshire," "Seacoast area"
  • Specific services: "drain cleaning," "Invisalign," "deep cleaning"

Keywords to avoid forcing in

  • "Near me" — Google doesn't match this in descriptions. See our "near me" optimization guide.
  • "Best" / "#1" / "top-rated" — promotional language that Google may flag
  • Competitor names — against Google's guidelines
  • Repetitive location names — "Manchester NH plumber Manchester New Hampshire" reads as spam

Use the keyword research approach

Before writing, check what terms people actually search for your service in your area. Include those terms naturally — once each is enough.

Writing Tips for Maximum Impact

Front-load the first 250 characters

Only the first ~250 characters show without clicking "Read more." Put your service, location, and strongest differentiator here. Don't waste it on "Welcome to our business" or "We're proud to serve."

Write for humans, not Google

Your description is read by people deciding whether to call you. Write naturally, clearly, and persuasively. If it sounds like a robot wrote it, rewrite it.

Include specific numbers

Numbers build credibility: "15 years of experience," "200+ five-star reviews," "serving 500+ families." Specifics are more believable than vague claims.

Mention what customers care about

Based on what customers commonly ask about:

  • Do you offer free estimates?
  • Are you licensed and insured?
  • What areas do you serve?
  • Do you accept insurance?
  • Are you available for emergencies?

Skip the generic filler

These phrases waste your 750 characters:

  • "We provide quality services at affordable prices" — says nothing specific
  • "Customer satisfaction is our #1 priority" — every business says this
  • "Call us today for a free quote" — you can't include CTAs in descriptions
  • "Welcome to [business name]" — wastes your most valuable characters

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Leaving it blank: 40%+ of businesses have no description. Any description beats no description.
  2. Keyword stuffing: "Plumber Manchester NH plumber Nashua NH plumber Concord NH" will get flagged or ignored.
  3. Including phone numbers or URLs: Google will reject these. Your phone and website are in separate GBP fields.
  4. Using ALL CAPS: Looks spammy and violates guidelines.
  5. Copying your website's about page: Write specifically for the GBP format — 750 characters, no links, no formatting.
  6. Never updating it: If you've added services, moved locations, or changed your approach, update the description to match. Check our full GBP guide for all optimization steps.

The Quick Audit

Review your current description against this checklist:

  • Does the first sentence mention your primary service and location?
  • Is it close to the full 750 characters? (Using less wastes opportunity)
  • Does it include at least 3 specific services you offer?
  • Does it mention what makes you different from competitors?
  • Would a potential customer learn something useful from reading it?
  • Is it free of phone numbers, URLs, and promotional language?
  • Does it read naturally — not like a list of keywords?

If you answered "no" to any of these, it's time for a rewrite.

Beyond the Description: Complete GBP Optimization

Your description is just one piece of your GBP. For maximum local visibility, also optimize:

  • Complete profile setup
  • Weekly GBP posts
  • Review generation strategy
  • Review response protocol
  • Photos (aim for 20+ high-quality images)
  • Q&A section (answer common questions proactively)
