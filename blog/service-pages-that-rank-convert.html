How to Write Service Pages That Rank on Google and Convert Visitors

Alex Feb 17, 2026 10 min read

Your service pages are where SEO meets sales. They need to rank on Google for transactional keywords AND convince visitors to pick up the phone or fill out a form.

Most NH business websites have one of two problems: service pages that read like a brochure (no SEO), or service pages stuffed with keywords (no conversion). Here's how to build pages that do both.

The Anatomy of a High-Performing Service Page

Every service page needs these 10 elements, in roughly this order:

1. Keyword-Optimized H1 Heading

Your H1 should include the service name and location. Keep it natural:

Good: "Emergency Plumbing Services in Manchester, NH"

Bad: "Best #1 Emergency Plumber Manchester NH Plumbing Services"

2. Compelling Opening Paragraph

The first 2-3 sentences should:

  • Acknowledge the problem the customer has
  • Position your service as the solution
  • Include your target keyword naturally
  • Mention your location/service area

3. Phone Number Above the Fold

People searching for local services often want to call immediately. Make your phone number visible without scrolling — especially on mobile. Use a click-to-call link: <a href="tel:+1XXXXXXXXXX">

4. Service Description (500+ Words)

This is where most service pages fail. They have 50 words and no real content. Google needs substance to rank your page. Include:

  • What the service includes (be specific)
  • Who it's for (homeowners, businesses, etc.)
  • Your process (what happens when they hire you)
  • What makes you different from competitors
  • Common problems you solve

Aim for 500-1,000 words minimum. Don't pad with fluff — every sentence should be useful.

5. Pricing Information

Many businesses avoid listing prices. But here's the thing: people search for pricing, and if you answer the question, you capture that traffic. You don't need exact prices — ranges work:

  • "Drain cleaning typically costs $150-350 depending on the severity"
  • "Our SEO packages start at $XXX/month"
  • "Free estimates — call for a custom quote"

Transparency builds trust and filters out unqualified leads. See how we handle pricing.

6. Social Proof

Embed Google reviews or testimonials directly on the service page. Include:

  • Star rating + review count
  • 2-3 specific testimonials related to that service
  • Customer name and city (with permission)

7. Service Area Mentions

Naturally mention the cities you serve. Don't just list them at the bottom — weave them into the content:

"We provide emergency plumbing services throughout southern NH, including Manchester, Nashua, Concord, and the surrounding communities. Our trucks are equipped to handle any emergency within 60 minutes."

8. FAQ Section

Add 3-5 frequently asked questions at the bottom of each service page. This captures voice search queries and can win featured snippets. Mark up with FAQ schema for extra visibility.

9. Clear CTA (Call to Action)

Every service page needs at least 2 CTAs — one above the fold and one at the bottom. Use action-oriented language:

  • "Call Now for a Free Estimate" (with phone number)
  • "Schedule Your Free Inspection"
  • "Get a Quote in 60 Seconds"

See our landing page optimization guide for more CTA strategies.

10. Schema Markup

Add LocalBusiness and Service schema to help Google understand exactly what service this page is about, your location, hours, and pricing.

One Page Per Service: Why It Matters

Don't put all your services on one page. Create a dedicated page for each service:

Bad Good
/services (lists everything) /services/emergency-plumbing
Targets "plumber manchester nh" Targets "emergency plumber manchester nh"
Competes with itself Each page targets a unique keyword

A plumber might have pages for: emergency plumbing, drain cleaning, water heater installation, bathroom remodeling, pipe repair, and sewer line services. Each targets different keywords and attracts different customers.

Title Tags and Meta Descriptions for Service Pages

Your title tag is the most important on-page SEO element. Format:

[Service] in [City], NH — [Business Name]

Examples:
"Emergency Plumbing in Manchester, NH — Quick Flow Plumbing"
"Roof Repair in Nashua, NH — Granite State Roofing"
"Family Dentistry in Concord, NH — Smile Care Dental"

Meta descriptions should be compelling and include a call to action:

"Need emergency plumbing in Manchester NH? Quick Flow Plumbing offers 24/7 service with 60-minute response times. Licensed, insured, 5-star rated. Call (603) 555-0123."

Internal Linking Strategy for Service Pages

Your service pages should be among the most linked-to pages on your site:

  • Navigation menu: Link to your main services page, which links to individual service pages
  • Homepage: Feature your top 3-5 services with links
  • Blog posts: Every relevant blog post should link to the appropriate service page. See our internal linking guide
  • City pages: If you have location pages, link from each city page to your services
  • Footer: Include service links in your footer navigation

Common Service Page Mistakes

  • Too short: A 100-word page can't rank for competitive keywords. Write 500+ words of useful content
  • No unique content: Copying your competitors' service descriptions won't rank. Write original content about YOUR process and expertise
  • Missing phone number: If someone has to hunt for your contact info, they'll call a competitor instead
  • No testimonials: Social proof on service pages can increase conversions by 30-50%
  • Generic stock photos: Real photos of your team, equipment, and completed work build more trust
  • Keyword stuffing: "We offer plumbing services Manchester NH plumber Manchester plumbing" reads terribly and Google penalizes it
  • No mobile optimization: Most local searches are mobile. If your service page is hard to use on a phone, you lose

Service Page Checklist

  • H1 includes service name + city/area
  • Title tag under 60 characters with keyword
  • Meta description under 155 characters with CTA
  • Phone number visible above the fold
  • 500+ words of useful, unique content
  • Pricing info (even if just a range or "free estimate")
  • 2-3 testimonials/reviews specific to this service
  • FAQ section with 3-5 questions
  • Service area mentioned naturally
  • CTA button/form above fold AND at bottom
  • LocalBusiness + Service schema markup
  • Internal links from blog posts and other pages
  • Mobile-optimized with click-to-call
