Google Business Profile Posts:
What to Post and How Often

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 8 min read

Google Business Profile Posts are one of the most underused free marketing tools available to local businesses. They show up directly in Google Search and Maps, yet fewer than 20% of businesses use them regularly. That’s your advantage.

Think of GBP Posts as free ads that appear when people search for your business or your service category. They cost nothing, take 5 minutes to create, and can drive calls, website visits, and foot traffic. Here’s how to use them effectively.

What Are GBP Posts?

Posts are short updates (up to 1,500 characters) with an optional photo that appear on your Google Business Profile. They show up in:

  • Your business’s Knowledge Panel (right side of search results)
  • Google Maps listing
  • Discovery searches (“plumber near me”)
  • Mobile search results

Posts expire after 7 days (except event posts, which stay until the event date passes). This means you need to post consistently to always have an active post visible.

The 5 Post Types

1. Update Posts (Most Common)

General business updates, tips, news, or behind-the-scenes content.

Best for: Weekly content to keep your profile active and fresh.

Example: “Just finished a beautiful kitchen remodel in Manchester! Check out the before and after. Planning a renovation? Call us for a free estimate.”

2. Offer Posts

Special deals, discounts, or promotions with an optional coupon code.

Best for: Seasonal promotions, new customer offers, slow-period boosts.

Example: “Winter HVAC tune-up: $99 (reg. $149). Schedule before March 1st. Keeps your system running efficiently all season.”

3. Event Posts

Upcoming events with a title, date/time, and details.

Best for: Open houses, workshops, community events, seasonal sales.

Example: “Free Home Buying Seminar — Saturday, March 8th at our Nashua office. Learn about the NH market, mortgage options, and first-time buyer programs. RSVP to save your spot.”

4. Product Posts

Showcase specific products with photos, prices, and descriptions.

Best for: Retail stores, restaurants (menu items), specialty shops.

Example: “New arrival: locally roasted maple bourbon coffee. $14.99/bag. Only at our Concord shop.”

5. COVID/Health Updates (Still Available)

Health and safety policies. Less relevant now but still available for communicating safety measures.

What to Post: Content Calendar

Post at least once per week. Here’s a monthly rotation that keeps content fresh without being repetitive:

Week 1: Project Showcase / Behind the Scenes

  • Photo of recent work
  • Brief description of the project
  • City/neighborhood mention
  • CTA: “Need [service]? Call us today.”

Week 2: Helpful Tip or Seasonal Advice

  • Share a genuinely useful tip related to your industry
  • Link to a relevant blog post for more detail
  • Position yourself as the expert
  • CTA: “Read our full guide” or “Questions? We’re here to help.”

Week 3: Offer or Promotion

  • Use the Offer post type
  • Clear deal terms and expiration
  • Make it genuinely valuable
  • CTA: “Book Now” or “Claim This Offer”

Week 4: Social Proof / Customer Spotlight

  • Highlight a great review (with permission)
  • Share a customer success story
  • Thank your community
  • CTA: “Join our happy customers. Get a free quote.”

Post Optimization Tips

Photos

  • Always include a photo. Posts with images get 10x more engagement than text-only posts.
  • Use real photos, not stock. Show your actual work, team, and location.
  • Optimal size: 1200 x 900 pixels (4:3 ratio)
  • Show people when possible. Photos with faces get 38% more engagement.

Text

  • Lead with value. First 80 characters are visible before “Read more” — make them count.
  • Include keywords naturally. Mention your service and city: “Emergency plumbing repair in Manchester, NH”
  • Keep it concise. 150-300 characters is ideal. People won’t read a wall of text.
  • Always include a CTA. Every post should tell the reader what to do next.

CTA Buttons

Google lets you add a CTA button to each post. Use them:

  • Book — best for service businesses with online scheduling
  • Order Online — best for restaurants and retail
  • Learn More — links to your website for more info
  • Call Now — best for immediate-need services (emergency, medical)
  • Get Offer — for promotional posts

How GBP Posts Help Your Rankings

Google doesn’t say posts directly boost rankings, but they signal an active, engaged business:

  • Activity signal. Regular posts tell Google your business is active and current. Abandoned profiles rank lower.
  • Keyword relevance. Using service and location keywords in posts reinforces what your business does and where.
  • Engagement. When people click your posts, view them, and take action, Google sees positive user signals.
  • Click-through rate. More engaging profile → more clicks → stronger ranking signals.
  • Differentiation. In competitive map pack results, active posts make your listing stand out visually.

Industry-Specific Post Ideas

Home Services

  • Before/after project photos
  • Seasonal maintenance tips (“3 things to check before NH winter”)
  • Emergency availability announcements
  • Team certifications or training completed

Restaurants

  • New menu items or daily specials
  • Event announcements (live music, trivia night)
  • Seasonal ingredients (“Fresh NH maple in our spring menu”)
  • Behind-the-kitchen photos

Healthcare

  • Health tips relevant to the season
  • New services or technology
  • Staff spotlights
  • Insurance or payment updates

Professional Services

  • Regulatory changes affecting clients
  • Tax tips (for accountants), legal updates (for lawyers)
  • Awards or recognitions
  • Community involvement

Common Mistakes

  • Posting once and forgetting. Posts expire after 7 days. One-and-done doesn’t work.
  • All promotions, no value. Mix in helpful content. Follow the 80/20 rule from email marketing: 80% value, 20% promotion.
  • No photo. Text-only posts get buried. Always include an image.
  • Generic stock photos. Google and customers can tell. Use real photos.
  • No CTA button. Every post should have one. Don’t leave clicks on the table.
  • Ignoring insights. GBP tells you how many views and clicks each post gets. Use this data to improve.

Getting Started in 10 Minutes

  1. Log into your Google Business Profile
  2. Click “Add update”
  3. Choose post type (start with Update)
  4. Add a photo of recent work
  5. Write 2-3 sentences: what you did, where, and a CTA
  6. Add a CTA button
  7. Publish
  8. Set a weekly reminder to post again

It’s that simple. Five minutes a week keeps your profile fresh and your business visible in local search.
