Local SEO ROI:
How to Calculate the Value of Ranking #1

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 9 min read

The #1 question every business owner asks before investing in SEO: “Is it worth it?” The answer is math, not opinion. Here’s how to calculate exactly what ranking on Google’s first page is worth to your business.

The Simple ROI Formula

Monthly SEO value = Monthly searches × Click-through rate × Conversion rate × Average customer value

Let’s break down each variable.

Step 1: Monthly Search Volume

How many people search for your service in your area each month? Use keyword research tools to find this.

Example NH search volumes:

  • “plumber Manchester NH” — ~320 searches/month
  • “dentist Nashua NH” — ~260 searches/month
  • “HVAC repair Concord NH” — ~140 searches/month
  • “roofer NH” — ~480 searches/month
  • “personal injury lawyer Manchester NH” — ~170 searches/month

Remember: these are just the primary keywords. Each service has dozens of variations (“plumber near me”, “emergency plumbing”, “drain cleaning”) that collectively drive 3-5x the primary keyword volume.

Step 2: Click-Through Rate by Position

Your Google ranking position determines what percentage of searchers click your listing:

  • Map Pack Position 1: ~25-35% click rate
  • Map Pack Position 2: ~15-20% click rate
  • Map Pack Position 3: ~10-15% click rate
  • Organic Position 1: ~28-32% click rate
  • Organic Position 2: ~15-17% click rate
  • Organic Position 3: ~10-11% click rate
  • Page 2: ~1-2% click rate (effectively invisible)

If you’re on page 2, you’re getting almost zero traffic from search. Moving to page 1 is a 10-30x increase in visibility.

Step 3: Website Conversion Rate

Of the people who click to your website, what percentage become a lead (call, form fill, booking)?

  • Average local business website: 2-5%
  • Well-optimized landing page: 8-15%
  • Poor website: Under 1%

We’ll use 5% (a realistic target for a decent local business website).

Step 4: Average Customer Value

What’s a new customer worth to your business? Include the full lifetime, not just the first transaction.

  • Plumber: $250-500 per job, 2-3 jobs per year from repeat customers = $500-1,500/year
  • Dentist: $1,200-2,500 per year (cleanings + procedures)
  • HVAC: $3,000-8,000 (installation) or $200-400/year (maintenance)
  • Restaurant: $30-60 per visit, 12-24 visits per year = $360-1,440/year
  • Lawyer: $2,000-50,000+ per case
  • Roofer: $5,000-15,000 per project

Putting It All Together: Real Examples

Example 1: Plumber in Manchester, NH

  • Combined monthly searches (all plumbing keywords): ~800
  • Map Pack Position 1 click rate: 30%
  • Clicks per month: 800 × 30% = 240 visitors
  • Conversion rate: 5%
  • Leads per month: 240 × 5% = 12 leads
  • Close rate: 50% (half of leads book the job)
  • New customers per month: 6
  • Average job value: $400
  • Monthly revenue from SEO: $2,400
  • Annual revenue from SEO: $28,800

If SEO costs $750-1,500/month, that’s a 2-3x monthly ROI.

Example 2: Dentist in Nashua, NH

  • Combined monthly searches: ~600
  • Map Pack Position 1 click rate: 28%
  • Clicks per month: 600 × 28% = 168 visitors
  • Conversion rate: 6% (appointment booking)
  • New patient inquiries per month: 168 × 6% = 10 inquiries
  • Booking rate: 60%
  • New patients per month: 6
  • Annual patient value: $1,800
  • First-year patient value from SEO: $10,800/month
  • Cumulative after 12 months (72 new patients): $129,600/year ongoing

SEO for dentists compounds — each new patient keeps coming back for years.

Example 3: HVAC Company in Concord, NH

  • Combined monthly searches: ~500
  • Map Pack Position 2 click rate: 18%
  • Clicks per month: 500 × 18% = 90 visitors
  • Conversion rate: 5%
  • Leads per month: 90 × 5% = 4.5 leads
  • Close rate: 40%
  • New customers per month: ~2
  • Average job value: $4,500 (mix of repairs and installations)
  • Monthly revenue from SEO: $9,000
  • Annual revenue from SEO: $108,000

Even at Position 2, just 2 jobs per month from SEO generates massive ROI.

Compare SEO to Other Marketing Channels

  • SEO: $750-2,000/month, generates leads for years after you stop (unlike ads)
  • Google Ads: $15-50+ per click, stops the instant you stop paying
  • Social media ads: $5-25 per lead, lower intent than search traffic
  • Print/radio: $500-5,000+ per campaign, hard to track ROI
  • Referrals: Free but unpredictable and unscalable

SEO is the only marketing channel where the asset you build (rankings, content, backlinks) continues generating value after you invest.

The Cost of NOT Doing SEO

If you’re on page 2 while competitors are on page 1, you’re not just missing traffic — you’re actively losing it to them.

Using the plumber example: if your competitor ranks #1 and gets 12 leads/month that should have been yours, that’s:

  • $2,400/month in lost revenue
  • $28,800/year in lost revenue
  • 6 new customers/month going to your competitor instead
  • Those customers leave reviews for your competitor, making their ranking even stronger

The gap widens every month you wait. SEO compounds — both for you and against you.

When SEO Might NOT Be Worth It

To be honest, SEO isn’t right for every business:

  • Brand new businesses that need revenue THIS WEEK should start with Google Ads while SEO builds in the background
  • Businesses with very low customer value (under $50/customer and no repeat business) may not see enough ROI
  • Businesses in extremely low-search-volume areas (very small rural towns) may not have enough search demand
  • Businesses that can’t handle more customers (fully booked already) should optimize pricing, not marketing

For most NH businesses serving a city of 20,000+ people with an average customer value over $200, SEO math works out very favorably.

Calculate Your Own ROI

  1. Estimate your total monthly keyword search volume (use Google Keyword Planner)
  2. Assume 20-30% click rate for Position 1 (or current position rate)
  3. Apply your website’s conversion rate (or assume 3-5%)
  4. Multiply by your close rate
  5. Multiply by average customer value
  6. Compare to monthly SEO investment

If the math shows at least 2x return, SEO is a strong investment. Most local businesses see 3-10x returns once rankings mature (typically 4-8 months).
