Apple Maps vs Google Maps: Where Should Your Business Be Listed?

Alex Feb 17, 2026 12 min read

When local business owners think about maps, they think about Google. And they should — Google Maps is dominant. But there is a massive blind spot: Apple Maps. With over 1 billion active Apple devices worldwide and Apple Maps as the default on every iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Apple Watch, ignoring it means ignoring a huge chunk of your potential customers.

The short answer to "where should I be listed?" is both. But the strategies for each platform are different. This guide covers how each platform works, how to optimize your presence on both, and where to focus your limited time for maximum results.

The Market Reality in 2026

Google Maps

  • Market share: Roughly 70-75% of map usage globally
  • Monthly active users: Over 2 billion
  • Default on: Android devices (85% of smartphones globally)
  • Integrated with: Google Search, Google Assistant, Chrome, Gmail
  • Business tool: Google Business Profile (free)

Apple Maps

  • Market share: Roughly 20-25% of map usage globally, higher in the US
  • Monthly active users: Over 1 billion (estimated)
  • Default on: Every iPhone, iPad, Mac, Apple Watch, HomePod, CarPlay
  • Integrated with: Siri, Safari, Messages, Mail, Spotlight Search
  • Business tool: Apple Business Connect (free)

Why Apple Maps Matters More Than You Think

In the United States, iPhone market share is over 55%. That means more than half of American smartphone users have Apple Maps as their default. When someone asks Siri "find a plumber near me," it uses Apple Maps. When someone clicks an address in Safari or Messages, it opens Apple Maps. When someone uses CarPlay for navigation, it defaults to Apple Maps.

Most businesses have never claimed their Apple Maps listing. That is free visibility you are leaving on the table.

Google Maps: What You Need to Know

You probably already know the basics of Google Maps optimization through your Google Business Profile. Here is a quick summary of the key factors:

How Rankings Work

  • Proximity — How close you are to the searcher
  • Relevance — How well your profile matches the search query (categories, keywords, services)
  • Prominence — Reviews, backlinks, citations, brand strength

Google Maps Optimization Checklist

  • Claim and verify your Google Business Profile
  • Choose the right primary and secondary categories
  • Complete every field: hours, phone, website, services, attributes, description
  • Upload 50+ high-quality photos
  • Post weekly using GBP posts
  • Generate and respond to reviews consistently
  • Build citations with consistent NAP
  • Add LocalBusiness schema to your website

Apple Maps: The Untapped Opportunity

Apple Business Connect (formerly Apple Maps Connect) is the tool for managing your business on Apple Maps. It launched a major overhaul in 2023 and has been steadily adding features since. Here is everything you need to know.

Setting Up Apple Business Connect

  1. Go to businessconnect.apple.com
  2. Sign in with your Apple ID (or create one for your business)
  3. Search for your business — Apple likely already has a listing from data partners
  4. Claim your listing and verify ownership
  5. Complete your profile with all available information

What You Can Optimize on Apple Business Connect

  • Business name and category — Choose the most accurate category
  • Address and phone number — Must match your GBP and website exactly
  • Hours of operation — Including special hours
  • Photos — Upload your best photos (logo, cover, interior, products)
  • Showcases — Apple's equivalent to Google Posts. Promote offers, events, and announcements. These appear directly on your Apple Maps listing.
  • Action links — Direct links for ordering, reservations, appointments
  • Place cards — Rich business information displayed when someone taps your listing

Apple Maps Ranking Factors

Apple has not published their ranking algorithm, but based on testing and observation, these factors matter:

  • Proximity — Same as Google, closer businesses appear higher
  • Category accuracy — Matching the right category to the search
  • Yelp reviews — Apple Maps pulls reviews from Yelp, not its own review system
  • Website quality — Apple crawls your website for business information
  • Data accuracy — Consistent NAP across data providers (Yelp, Foursquare, TripAdvisor)
  • Business Connect completeness — Claimed and fully completed profiles rank better
  • Showcases activity — Regular Showcases signal an active business

Why Yelp Suddenly Matters Again

Here is something most business owners do not realize: Apple Maps uses Yelp for reviews and ratings. When an iPhone user sees your business on Apple Maps, the stars and reviews they see come from Yelp.

This means your Yelp profile has a direct impact on how your business appears to over half of US smartphone users — even if they never visit Yelp directly.

Yelp Optimization for Apple Maps

  • Claim your Yelp Business page at biz.yelp.com
  • Complete every field: hours, photos, services, specialties
  • Respond to all Yelp reviews (especially recent ones)
  • Upload high-quality photos regularly
  • Ensure your NAP matches your Google Business Profile and website exactly
  • Do not ask for Yelp reviews explicitly (Yelp penalizes this) — but do provide great service that naturally generates them

Siri and Voice Search

When someone asks Siri "find a dentist near me" or "best pizza place nearby," Siri uses Apple Maps data to answer. With voice search growing every year, this is increasingly important.

How to Optimize for Siri

  • Claim Apple Business Connect — Siri pulls directly from this data
  • Accurate categories — Siri matches queries to categories
  • Complete hours — "What is open right now?" is a common Siri query
  • Strong Yelp reviews — Siri factors in ratings when recommending businesses
  • Website with structured data — Schema markup helps Siri understand your business

The Bing Maps Factor

While we are talking about non-Google maps, Bing Maps deserves a mention. Bing Places for Business powers:

  • Bing search results (still about 6-8% of US search market)
  • Microsoft Cortana
  • Windows Maps
  • Some in-car navigation systems

Claim your Bing Places listing at bingplaces.com. It takes 10 minutes and you can import directly from your Google Business Profile.

The Complete Multi-Map Strategy

Here is the priority order for setting up and optimizing your business across all map platforms:

Priority 1: Google Business Profile (Week 1)

  • Claim, verify, and fully optimize
  • This is still where the majority of map searches happen
  • Generates the most reviews, traffic, and calls

Priority 2: Apple Business Connect (Week 1-2)

  • Claim and complete your profile
  • Upload photos and set up Showcases
  • Reaches all iPhone users — over half the US market

Priority 3: Yelp Business (Week 2)

  • Claim and optimize — feeds into Apple Maps reviews
  • Upload photos, respond to reviews, complete all fields

Priority 4: Bing Places (Week 2-3)

  • Quick setup — import from Google Business Profile
  • Smaller audience but zero competition from other businesses ignoring it

Ongoing Maintenance

  • Keep hours updated across all platforms (especially holidays)
  • Upload new photos to all platforms monthly
  • Post GBP Posts weekly and Apple Showcases biweekly
  • Respond to reviews on Google and Yelp within 24 hours
  • Ensure NAP consistency across all platforms

Common Mistakes

  1. Only optimizing Google — You miss 25-30% of map users by ignoring Apple Maps
  2. Inconsistent information — Different hours or phone numbers across platforms confuses customers and search algorithms
  3. Ignoring Yelp — Even if you hate Yelp, it powers Apple Maps reviews. Manage it.
  4. Not claiming Apple Business Connect — Your listing exists whether you claim it or not. Unclaimed listings have errors.
  5. Forgetting about Siri — Over 500 million people use Siri. It uses Apple Maps data.
  6. Not updating all platforms — When you change hours, you need to update Google, Apple, Yelp, and Bing

Bottom Line

Google Maps is still king for local search. Optimize it first and give it the most attention. But in 2026, ignoring Apple Maps is like ignoring half of your potential customers in the US. The setup takes 30 minutes. The ongoing maintenance takes 15 minutes per month. The potential return is thousands of impressions and dozens of new customers who would never have found you otherwise.

Claim your business on Google Business Profile, Apple Business Connect, Yelp, and Bing Places. Keep your information consistent across all four. That is the foundation. Everything else is optimization on top of that foundation.
