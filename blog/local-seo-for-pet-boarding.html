Local SEO for Pet Boarding and Kennels: Pack Your Facility with Google

By Alex Feb 17, 2026 17 min read

Pet boarding is a recession-resistant, high-margin business. Pet owners spend over $6 billion annually on boarding and pet sitting in the US alone. Holiday weekends, summer vacations, and business travel create consistent demand. Yet most boarding facilities operate at 40-60% capacity during peak seasons because they're not visible on Google when pet parents search for boarding options.

When someone needs to board their dog or cat, they don't look in the local newspaper anymore — they Google "dog boarding near me" and pick from the top results. If your facility isn't showing up in that Google Maps Pack, a competitor with better online visibility is capturing your bookings. More critically, you're leaving 30-40% of your potential annual revenue unclaimed, simply because you're not where pet parents are searching.

This guide covers the specific SEO strategies that work for pet boarding facilities, kennels, and pet hotels. From optimizing your Google Business Profile to targeting high-intent pet owner keywords, here's how to fill your facility year-round.

Why Pet Boarding Needs Local SEO

Pet boarding has unique characteristics that make local SEO exceptionally effective:

  • Recurring, predictable demand — holidays, summers, business travel. Pet owners plan ahead and search months in advance.
  • Location dependency — pet parents won't drive more than 10-15 minutes for boarding. Local SEO targets exactly this radius.
  • "Near me" search dominance — "dog boarding near me" generates 350,000+ monthly searches nationally. Holiday weeks double this volume.
  • Trust-based decision — pet owners read reviews obsessively before boarding their beloved pets. High review counts directly drive bookings.
  • High booking value — average boarding stay is 5-7 days at $30-60/day. Single booking = $150-420 revenue. Add dog training, grooming, or daycare, and it's $300-800+ per booking.
  • Seasonal capacity premium — your facility is most available in low seasons when nobody is searching. SEO helps you fill slow periods too.

The math: rank #1 for "dog boarding near me" and capture 40-60 additional bookings per year. At average $300 per booking, that's $12,000-18,000+ in incremental annual revenue from organic search.

Google Business Profile: Your Most Important Booking Channel

For pet boarding, your Google Business Profile is your sales engine. 70%+ of local pet boarding searches include "near me," and Google Maps dominates that result page. Optimizing GBP directly increases bookings.

Choose the Right Categories

Your primary category should be:

  • Pet boarding or Dog boarding facility — most recognizable to pet parents
  • Kennels or Dog kennel — if you emphasize outdoor facilities or larger-scale operations

Add secondary categories:

  • Dog daycare — if you offer day boarding
  • Dog grooming service — if you offer grooming
  • Pet hotel — if you want to emphasize premium positioning
  • Dog training — if you offer training programs

Optimize Your Business Description

Your description must answer every question a pet parent has before trusting you with their dog:

  • Maximum occupancy and typical availability during peak seasons
  • Individual vs. group play options and ratios
  • Indoor and outdoor facilities descriptions
  • Special accommodations (senior dogs, anxious dogs, medical needs)
  • Meal provision and dietary accommodation
  • Emergency medical care and veterinary oversight
  • Staff qualifications and experience
  • Webcam or daily update options
  • Additional services (grooming, training, daycare)
  • Rates and length-of-stay discounts

Photos That Convert

Pet parents judge boarding facilities primarily on photos. Upload lots of high-quality images showing:

  • Clean, spacious indoor play areas
  • Outdoor run areas and exercise yards
  • Individual kennels or suites (especially if heated/cooled)
  • Dogs playing together and socializing happily
  • Staff interacting with dogs (playing, petting, training)
  • Reception area and check-in process
  • Feeding station and food quality
  • Sleeping/rest areas with comfortable bedding
  • Emergency care setup if you have veterinary oversight

Update photos monthly, especially photos of happy dogs. Fresh, happy dog photos signal active operations and boost conversions significantly.

Post Weekly Updates

Use Google Posts to:

  • Share photos from recent bookings ("Happy dogs playing in the yard!")
  • Announce availability for upcoming holiday periods
  • Share customer testimonials and dog photos
  • Offer promotions ("Book 5 stays, get 1 free" or "New customer 10% off")
  • Share pet care tips and dog health information
  • Highlight staff introductions and team members

Keyword Strategy for Pet Boarding

Pet boarding keywords are highly predictable and searchable. Target all of them:

Core Location Keywords

  • "dog boarding near me" — highest volume, highest intent
  • "dog boarding [city name]" — city-level searches
  • "dog boarding [neighborhood/district]" — hyper-local
  • "pet boarding near me" — includes cats and other pets
  • "kennels near me" — alternative terminology
  • "best dog boarding [city]" — quality-focused searches
  • "dog boarding open now" — urgent/immediate need

Service-Specific Keywords

  • "dog daycare near me" — different from overnight boarding, different searchers
  • "dog boarding with training" — higher value service
  • "cat boarding near me" — separate keyword market from dogs
  • "luxury pet hotel [city]" — premium positioning
  • "dog boarding for anxious dogs" — special needs, lower competition
  • "dog boarding for senior dogs" — demographic-specific (lower competition)
  • "dog boarding with webcam" — technology-focused pet parents

Decision and Research Keywords

  • "how much does dog boarding cost" — pricing research (high-value)
  • "best dog boarding [city]" — actively comparing options
  • "dog boarding reviews [city]" — review-heavy research
  • "dog boarding vs dog sitter" — consideration stage
  • "what to expect at dog boarding" — educational (lower intent but builds familiarity)
  • "dog boarding for the first time" — nervous new customers (high conversion if reassured)

Seasonal and Holiday Keywords

  • "dog boarding Thanksgiving [city]" — seasonal spikes (book early)
  • "dog boarding Christmas [city]" — peak season searches
  • "dog boarding summer vacation [city]" — high-volume seasonal
  • "last minute dog boarding [city]" — emergency/urgent bookings

Website Optimization for Pet Boarding Facilities

Your website must make the booking process frictionless and address every pet parent concern:

Essential Pages

  • Homepage — clear value prop, facility photos, services list, prominent booking/reservation button
  • Services page — overnight boarding, daycare, training, grooming, special services with pricing
  • Facility tour — detailed breakdown of indoor areas, outdoor yards, kennels, amenities
  • Pricing page — rates by stay length, breed size, services. Include any discounts (multi-day, frequency, off-season).
  • Testimonials & reviews page — aggregate your best reviews with owner photos
  • Photo gallery — extensive before-boarding and happy-in-facility photos
  • Staff & care page — staff bios, qualifications, veterinary oversight information
  • FAQs — what to bring, health requirements, pick-up/drop-off procedures, cancellation policy

High-Converting Content

  • "Complete guide to boarding your dog for the first time" — build confidence in nervous first-timers
  • "Dog boarding cost: what you should expect to pay" — transparent pricing content
  • "How to prepare your dog for boarding" — helpful pre-boarding guide
  • "Dog boarding myths debunked" — build trust through education
  • "Our boarding process explained" — walk through check-in through pick-up
  • "Special needs: boarding anxious, senior, or medical-condition dogs" — underserved niche
  • "Why socialization matters: the benefits of dog daycare" — market expansion

Technical Must-Haves

  • Mobile-first design — 75%+ of pet boarding searches are mobile
  • Online booking system — Mindbody, Acuity Scheduling, or similar. One-click booking drives conversions.
  • Click-to-call button — answer questions immediately
  • Photo gallery prominence — photos are the #1 trust builder
  • Testimonial carousel — happy customer quotes with pet photos
  • Schema markup — LocalBusiness schema + review schema + AggregateRating
  • Fast page speed — under 2.5 seconds on mobile

Building Review Authority for Pet Boarding

Reviews are the #1 conversion factor for pet boarding. Pet parents won't board their dogs anywhere without strong reviews:

Why Reviews Drive Pet Boarding Bookings

Unlike many services, pet boarding decisions are heavily emotional. Pet parents read reviews to verify their dogs will be safe, happy, and well-cared-for. A facility with 150+ 4.7-star reviews will fill to 80% capacity. One with 20 reviews might be at 50%, even if equally good.

How to Get More Reviews

  • Post-pickup follow-up email — send personalized message with photos and review link within 2 hours of pickup
  • SMS reminders — text 24 hours after pickup: "How was [Dog's Name]? Leave a review →"
  • Staff requests — train staff to ask for reviews at pickup (in-person > digital)
  • Incentive offers — "Leave a Google review and get $10 off your next stay"
  • Loyalty program tie-in — review requirement for loyalty status bonus
  • Photo contests — "Post your favorite boarding photo with a review to enter our monthly drawing"
  • QR codes at pickup — make review submission zero-friction

Response Strategy

Respond to every review within 24 hours:

  • Positive reviews — thank them by dog's name, mention a specific interaction, invite them back
  • Reviews about happiness/play — confirm those dogs loved our staff, mention upcoming new activities
  • Negative reviews about cost — explain value (staff ratios, facility, care level)
  • Negative reviews about staff/care — take seriously, offer to discuss offline, invite them to revisit

Competing with Dog Sitters, App-Based Boarding, and Rover

Platforms like Rover, Wag, and Care.com offer on-demand boarding from home-based providers. Here's how to compete:

  • Professionalism advantage — you're a licensed, insured facility. Many Rover sitters are not. Highlight this.
  • Socialization premium — your dogs play together. Home sitters can't offer this. Market group play as behavioral enrichment.
  • Consistency — same staff, same facility. Rover sitters vary. Build trust through familiarity.
  • Medical capability — you can provide basic medical care, medication administration. Rover sitters often cannot.
  • Multiple dogs advantage — families with multiple dogs prefer bringing all to one facility vs. multiple sitter visits

Target keywords like "professional dog boarding [city]" and "licensed dog boarding facility" to capture quality-conscious pet parents.

Seasonal Strategy and Peak-Period Booking

Pet boarding is highly seasonal. Maximize this with SEO:

  • Launch seasonal campaigns 8-10 weeks before holidays — Thanksgiving, Christmas, and summer vacation book early
  • Target seasonal keywords — "dog boarding Thanksgiving," "holiday dog boarding [city]"
  • Create seasonal content — "How to prepare your dog for holiday boarding" posts, published 2 months pre-holiday
  • Email past customers — reach out 10 weeks before peak seasons to re-book regulars
  • Special off-season offers — discounts for slow periods to build consistent year-round utilization
  • Group bookings from employers — corporate partnerships (offer "employee pet boarding discount")

Local Citations and Pet-Specific Directories

Build local authority by listing on every relevant directory:

  • General directories — Yelp, Google Maps, Yellow Pages, Foursquare, Apple Maps
  • Pet-specific directories — Rover, Care.com (yes, as a facility option), PetFinder, DogTime
  • Local directories — Chamber of Commerce, city guides, neighborhood blogs
  • Veterinary-related — many vets link to boarding options on their websites; reach out to local vets
  • Pet insurance directories — many pet insurance companies maintain boarding facility directories

Ensure NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency across all listings. Google uses this to build local authority.

Multi-Location Pet Boarding SEO

If you operate multiple locations, treat each as a distinct optimization opportunity:

  • Separate GBP for each location with unique photos, hours, and location-specific content
  • Individual location pages on your website describing neighborhood, capacity, staff, and local amenities
  • Location-specific reviews — encourage reviews for specific locations, not just your business overall
  • Neighborhood marketing — create hyper-local content for each facility's area

Measuring Pet Boarding SEO Success

Track these metrics to measure SEO ROI:

  • Google Maps views — monthly visibility in local search (GBP Insights)
  • Direction requests — how many people get directions
  • Phone calls from Google — calls from GBP listing
  • Website booking requests — online reservation form submissions
  • "Dog boarding near me" ranking — your position for primary keyword
  • Booking volume growth — total bookings per month, tracked separately
  • Capacity utilization — percentage of available kennels/beds booked monthly
  • Review count and rating growth — month-over-month improvement

Most pet boarding facilities see 30-50% capacity increase within 4-6 months of consistent SEO effort.
