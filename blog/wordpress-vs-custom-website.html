WordPress vs Custom Website:
Which Is Right for Your NH Business?

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 9 min read

Choosing the right platform for your website is one of the most important decisions you’ll make for your online presence. The wrong choice can cost you thousands in rework, limit your SEO potential, and frustrate you for years.

Let’s cut through the noise and compare the main options honestly.

The Four Main Options

1. WordPress (Self-Hosted)

WordPress powers 43% of all websites on the internet. It’s the most popular content management system by far.

Best for: Businesses that want flexibility, plan to blog regularly, and need to grow over time.

Cost:

  • Hosting: $15-50/month (SiteGround, Cloudways, WP Engine)
  • Theme: $0-80 one-time (free themes work fine; GeneratePress/Astra/Kadence are solid paid options)
  • Professional setup: $2,000-8,000 if hiring a developer
  • Ongoing: $0-50/month for plugin updates, security, backups

SEO strengths:

  • Full control over title tags, meta descriptions, URLs, and heading structure
  • Yoast SEO or Rank Math plugins make optimization straightforward
  • Easy to add schema markup
  • Can install caching plugins for speed
  • Unlimited pages and blog posts
  • Full access to HTML for custom modifications

Downsides:

  • Requires maintenance (updates, security patches, backups)
  • Can be slow if you use a bloated theme + too many plugins
  • Steeper learning curve than Squarespace/Wix
  • Security vulnerabilities if not maintained (but this is manageable)

2. Squarespace

A website builder with beautiful templates and an intuitive drag-and-drop editor.

Best for: Creative businesses (photographers, artists, restaurants) that prioritize design and don’t need heavy SEO customization.

Cost: $16-49/month (all-inclusive: hosting, SSL, templates)

SEO strengths:

  • Clean, mobile-responsive templates
  • Basic SEO settings built in (titles, descriptions, URLs)
  • SSL included
  • No maintenance required

Downsides:

  • Limited SEO customization (no schema plugins, limited control over code)
  • Slower than WordPress or custom sites
  • Can’t install third-party SEO tools
  • Harder to add custom functionality
  • You don’t own the platform — you’re renting

3. Wix

Another website builder, more flexible than Squarespace but with trade-offs.

Best for: Very small businesses that need something quick and cheap, and don’t plan to invest heavily in SEO.

Cost: $17-35/month

SEO strengths:

  • Wix SEO Wiz provides step-by-step optimization guidance
  • Better SEO controls than it had years ago (the “Wix is bad for SEO” reputation is partly outdated)
  • Easy drag-and-drop editing

Downsides:

  • Historically slower than other options (improving but still behind)
  • Code bloat from the builder adds unnecessary weight
  • Difficult to migrate away from Wix if you outgrow it
  • Limited server-side optimization options

4. Custom-Built (HTML/CSS/JS or Headless CMS)

A website built from scratch by a developer, or using a modern framework (Next.js, Astro, etc.) with a headless CMS for content management.

Best for: Businesses that want maximum speed, SEO control, and a unique design. Also great for simple sites (5-15 pages) that don’t need frequent content updates.

Cost:

  • Development: $1,500-10,000+ depending on complexity
  • Hosting: $0-20/month (static sites can be hosted free on Cloudflare Pages, Netlify, or GitHub Pages)
  • Ongoing: minimal (no plugins to update, no CMS security patches)

SEO strengths:

  • Fastest possible load times (no CMS overhead)
  • Perfect PageSpeed scores achievable
  • Complete control over every line of code, metadata, and structure
  • No bloat from unused themes/plugins
  • Free hosting options mean lower total cost of ownership

Downsides:

  • Need a developer for changes (unless you know code)
  • No built-in admin panel for content editing (unless using a headless CMS)
  • Higher upfront cost if you need a complex site
  • Finding a good developer can be challenging

The SEO Comparison

Here’s how they stack up for local SEO specifically:

Factor WordPress Squarespace Wix Custom
Page Speed Good* Average Average Excellent
SEO Control Excellent Good Good Excellent
Schema Markup Easy Limited Limited Full
Blog Capability Excellent Good Good Manual**
Ease of Updates Good Excellent Excellent Dev needed

*WordPress speed depends on theme/plugin choices. Can be excellent or terrible.
**Custom sites can include a headless CMS for easy content management.

Our Recommendation for local Businesses

If You Blog Regularly and Plan to Scale

Go with WordPress. It offers the best balance of SEO control, flexibility, and ease of content management. Most local businesses will be best served here.

If You Want Maximum Speed and Have a Simple Site

Go custom. A 5-10 page service business site built with clean HTML/CSS will load faster than anything else, cost less to host (often free), and score 95+ on PageSpeed. You’ll need a developer for changes, but for a site that rarely changes, this is ideal.

If Design Matters Most and SEO Is Secondary

Go with Squarespace. The templates are genuinely beautiful and the editing experience is smooth. Just know you’re trading SEO flexibility for design convenience.

If Budget Is Extremely Tight

Start with Wix or Squarespace. Getting something online is better than having nothing. You can always migrate later when budget allows. A basic site with good GBP optimization will still help you rank.

Platform Migration: When to Switch

Signs it’s time to switch platforms:

  • Your site loads slowly and you’ve exhausted all optimization options
  • You need SEO features your current platform doesn’t support
  • You’ve outgrown the design limitations
  • Monthly costs have crept above what a better platform would cost
  • Your competitors are outranking you partly due to platform limitations

Migration is possible without losing rankings if done carefully (301 redirects, preserve URLs where possible, maintain all content). But it’s easier to choose right the first time.
