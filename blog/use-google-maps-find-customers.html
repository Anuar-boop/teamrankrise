How to Use Google Maps to Find New Customers

By Alex Feb 17, 2026 10 min read

Google Maps is the most underutilized marketing channel for local businesses. Over 1 billion people use Google Maps every month, and for local service searches, the Map Pack (top 3 results) captures 42% of all clicks. Here's how to turn your Maps presence from a pin on a map into a customer acquisition machine.

Why Google Maps Matters More Than Your Website

For local businesses, Maps often outperforms your website as a lead source. Consider:

  • 86% of people use Google Maps to find local businesses
  • Map Pack results appear above organic website results in search
  • People can call you directly from Maps without ever visiting your website
  • Direction requests from Maps often lead to same-day visits
  • It's completely free — no ad spend required

If you haven't claimed your Google Maps listing yet, that's step zero. Everything below assumes you have a verified Google Business Profile.

The Map Pack: Your #1 Goal

When someone searches "[service] near me" or "[service] [city]," Google shows a map with 3 businesses highlighted — the Map Pack. Getting into those 3 spots is the most valuable local SEO achievement because:

  • They appear before all organic results
  • They include your star rating, review count, and phone number
  • Users can call or get directions with one tap
  • The visual prominence creates immediate trust

Our full Google Maps ranking guide covers the technical details of how to rank in the Map Pack.

7 Strategies to Get Customers from Google Maps

Strategy 1: Optimize your GBP for maximum visibility

Google's algorithm for Maps rankings weighs three factors: relevance, distance, and prominence. You can influence all three:

Factor What It Means How to Optimize
Relevance How well your listing matches the search Specific categories, complete services section, detailed description
Distance How close you are to the searcher Accurate address, service area definition
Prominence How well-known and trusted you are Reviews, citations, backlinks, website quality

Relevance and prominence are where you can make the biggest impact. Follow our complete GBP optimization guide.

Strategy 2: Generate reviews that sell

Reviews aren't just for rankings — they're the primary decision factor for Maps users. When someone sees three businesses in the Map Pack, they compare:

  1. Star rating (4.5+ is the target)
  2. Number of reviews (more = more trustworthy)
  3. Recent reviews (recent activity signals an active business)
  4. Review content (specific mentions of services and quality)

A business with 75 reviews at 4.8 stars will get 3-5x more clicks than one with 8 reviews at 4.5 stars — even if the second business ranks higher.

Build your review portfolio with our review generation guide and first 50 reviews strategy. Always respond to every review.

Strategy 3: Use Google Maps posts

GBP posts appear in your Maps listing and can showcase:

  • Offers: "20% off first-time customers this month"
  • Updates: "Now offering Saturday appointments"
  • Events: "Free workshop: Home winterization tips — Dec 15"
  • Products: Highlight specific services with photos

Post weekly. Each post stays visible for 7 days. This keeps your listing fresh and gives searchers more reasons to choose you.

Strategy 4: Add photos that convert

Businesses with 100+ photos get 520% more calls than those with fewer than 5. But not all photos are equal:

Photos that drive action

  • Team photos: Put a face to the business — builds immediate trust
  • Work examples: Before/after, completed projects, finished products
  • Your location: Storefront, interior, parking — helps people find you
  • Your vehicles/equipment: Shows you're a real, equipped operation

Photo tips

  • Add 2-3 new photos per week (consistency > volume)
  • Use real photos, not stock images
  • Good lighting matters — take photos during daytime
  • Include your logo or branding when relevant

Strategy 5: Answer questions proactively

The Q&A section on your Google Maps listing is an opportunity most businesses ignore. Proactively add your own FAQs:

  • "What are your hours?" (even though it's listed — people still ask)
  • "Do you offer free estimates?"
  • "What areas do you serve?"
  • "Do you accept [insurance/payment method]?"
  • "How quickly can you schedule an appointment?"

Ask the question from a personal Google account, then answer it from your business account. This pre-empts common objections and gives searchers instant answers.

Strategy 6: Build citations that boost Maps rankings

Google cross-references your business information across the web. Consistent citations on trusted directories reinforce your Maps listing credibility.

Priority: ensure your NAP (name, address, phone) is identical on Google Maps, Yelp, Apple Maps, Bing, Facebook, and your website.

Strategy 7: Track your Maps performance

Google provides free insights showing exactly how your Maps listing performs. Check monthly:

  • Search queries: What people search to find you
  • Search views vs Maps views: Where people discover you
  • Actions: Calls, direction requests, website clicks
  • Photo views: Compared to competitors in your category

Use this data with Google Analytics and Search Console for the complete picture. Our tracking guide shows how.

Advanced Maps Tactics

Geotag your photos

Photos taken at your business location automatically include GPS coordinates. These geotagged photos reinforce your location to Google. Always take photos on-site with a smartphone (GPS enabled), not in an office or at home.

Encourage keyword-rich reviews

When customers naturally mention your services and location in reviews ("Great plumbing service in Nashua — fixed our water heater same day"), it helps Google associate you with those searches. You can encourage this by asking specific questions in your review request: "What service did we provide for you?"

Complete the products/services section

Many businesses skip this section. List every service you offer with a description and price range. This helps Google match you to more specific searches.

Enable messaging

Turn on the messaging feature in your GBP so customers can text you directly from Maps. Some customers prefer texting over calling. Set up auto-replies for after-hours messages.

Common Maps Mistakes Costing You Customers

  1. Wrong business hours: Nothing kills trust faster than showing "Open" when you're closed. Update hours for every holiday.
  2. Wrong category: "Home Services" is too broad. "Plumber" is specific. The right primary category is critical for relevance.
  3. No reviews: If you have zero reviews, you're invisible next to competitors with 50+.
  4. Outdated photos: Photos from 5 years ago make your business look neglected.
  5. Duplicate listings: Having two listings for the same business confuses Google and dilutes your reviews.
  6. Not responding to reviews: 53% of customers expect a response within a week. Silence looks like you don't care.
