How to Create a Google Business Profile
(Step-by-Step 2026 Guide)

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 10 min read

No Google Business Profile = invisible to local customers. It’s the single most important thing you can do for your local SEO. Setting one up takes 20 minutes. Getting it optimized takes a bit longer. Here’s the complete guide.

What Is Google Business Profile?

Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is Google’s free tool that lets you control how your business appears in Google Maps and local search results. When someone searches “plumber near me” or “dentist Manchester NH,” the map results they see come from GBP listings.

A complete, optimized GBP listing is the foundation of local SEO. Without it, you won’t appear in the map pack — period.

Before You Start

You’ll need:

  • A Google account (personal Gmail works fine)
  • Your business name (exactly as it appears everywhere)
  • Your business address
  • Your business phone number (local number preferred)
  • Your website URL
  • Your business hours
  • Your business category (you’ll choose from Google’s list)

Step 1: Go to Google Business Profile

  1. Go to business.google.com
  2. Sign in with your Google account
  3. Click “Manage now” or “Add your business to Google”

Important: First, search for your business name. Google may have already created a listing from public data. If it exists, you’ll “claim” it rather than creating a new one. Duplicate listings confuse Google — never create a second one.

Step 2: Enter Your Business Information

Business Name

Use your real business name — exactly as it appears on your signage, business cards, and legal registration. Don’t add keywords, locations, or marketing phrases.

  • Correct: “Smith Plumbing”
  • Wrong: “Smith Plumbing — Best Emergency Plumber Manchester NH”

Adding keywords to your business name violates Google’s guidelines and can get your listing suspended.

Business Category

Choose your primary category carefully — it’s the most important ranking factor for the map pack.

  • Be as specific as possible. “Emergency Plumber” is better than “Plumber” if that’s your main service.
  • You can add up to 9 secondary categories later. Add all relevant ones.
  • If your exact service isn’t listed, pick the closest match. Google has 4,000+ categories.

Address

  • Storefront businesses: Enter your full address. It will appear on your listing and Google Maps.
  • Service-area businesses (you go to customers): Enter your address but check “I deliver goods and services to my customers” and hide your address. Then define your service area by city or radius.
  • Never use: PO boxes, virtual offices, UPS Store addresses, or co-working spaces (unless you have a dedicated private office).

Phone Number

Use a local phone number, not a toll-free 800 number. Local numbers signal to Google that you’re actually in the area. Make sure this matches the number on your website and all other listings.

Website

Link to your homepage or a dedicated landing page. For multi-location businesses, each GBP listing should link to its specific location page.

Step 3: Verify Your Business

Google needs to confirm you’re real. Verification methods (Google chooses which ones to offer):

  1. Postcard by mail (most common): Google mails a postcard with a 5-digit code to your business address. Takes 5-14 days. Enter the code when it arrives.
  2. Phone verification: Google calls or texts a code to your business phone. Instant.
  3. Email verification: Code sent to your business email. Instant.
  4. Video verification: Record a video showing your storefront, address, and business operations. Takes 1-5 business days for review.

Do not change your business name or address while waiting for verification. This resets the process.

Step 4: Complete Your Profile (Optimization)

A bare listing won’t rank. Complete every field. See our detailed GBP optimization guide for advanced tips.

Essential Fields to Complete

  • Business description: 750 characters max. Describe what you do, where you serve, and what makes you different. Include your city and service keywords naturally.
  • Business hours: Set regular hours and update for holidays. Inaccurate hours lead to bad reviews.
  • Services/Products: List every service you offer with descriptions and prices (if applicable).
  • Photos: Add at least 10 photos — exterior, interior, team, and work examples. Businesses with 100+ photos get 520% more calls than average. Use real photos, not stock.
  • Attributes: Select all relevant attributes (women-owned, veteran-owned, wheelchair accessible, etc.)
  • Opening date: Add your founding year — longevity builds trust.

Ongoing Tasks

  • Post weekly. GBP Posts keep your profile active and visible.
  • Collect reviews. Ask every customer. Respond to every review within 24 hours.
  • Answer questions. Monitor the Q&A section and answer promptly.
  • Update for changes. New hours, services, photos — keep everything current.
  • Add schema markup to your website to reinforce GBP data.

Common GBP Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Keyword-stuffed business name. Google will suspend you for this.
  2. Wrong category. Choosing “Plumbing supply store” when you’re a plumber. This tanks your map ranking.
  3. Fake address. Virtual offices and PO boxes get caught and suspended.
  4. Inconsistent information. Your GBP name, address, and phone must match your website and all citations exactly.
  5. No photos. Listings without photos get 42% fewer direction requests.
  6. Ignoring reviews. Not responding to reviews signals you don’t care. Always respond, especially to negative ones.
  7. Creating duplicate listings. Never create multiple listings for the same location. Search first, claim if it exists.
  8. Setting and forgetting. GBP isn’t “set it and forget it.” Regular activity is a ranking factor.

What Happens After Setup

After verification (1-14 days), your business will start appearing in Google Maps and local search results. However, ranking well takes ongoing effort:

  • Week 1-2: You may appear for brand name searches (“Smith Plumbing Manchester”)
  • Month 1-3: With optimization, you may start appearing for category searches (“plumber Manchester NH”)
  • Month 3-6: Consistent reviews, posts, and SEO work can push you into the map pack for competitive terms
  • Month 6+: Sustained effort compounds. Strong GBP + website SEO + reviews = dominant local presence
