The Ultimate NH Small Business
SEO Checklist (2026 Edition)

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 15 min read

This is everything your New Hampshire business needs to check, fix, and implement to rank on Google in 2026. Print it. Bookmark it. Work through it methodically. Each item links to our detailed guide on that topic.

Google Business Profile (The Foundation)

Your Google Business Profile is the most important factor for Google Maps rankings. Complete every item:

  • Claim and verify your Google Business Profile
  • Business name matches your real-world name exactly
  • Address is accurate and formatted consistently
  • Phone number is local (603 area code preferred in NH)
  • Primary category is the most specific match for your business
  • Add 3-5 secondary categories
  • Business description filled out (750 characters, include services and cities)
  • Hours are accurate, including holiday hours
  • All services listed individually with descriptions
  • At least 20 photos (exterior, interior, team, work examples)
  • Website URL points to your homepage
  • Appointment URL set up (if applicable)
  • Products section filled out (if applicable)
  • Q&A section has owner-answered questions
  • Publishing Google Posts weekly (updates, offers, events)

Website Technical SEO

The technical foundation must be solid. Full technical checklist here.

  • HTTPS enabled (SSL certificate installed)
  • Mobile-responsive design (test on actual phone)
  • Page speed: under 3 seconds on mobile
  • Core Web Vitals passing (LCP < 2.5s, INP < 200ms, CLS < 0.1)
  • XML sitemap submitted to Google Search Console
  • Robots.txt file isn’t blocking important pages
  • No duplicate content issues
  • All images compressed and using WebP format
  • All images have descriptive alt text
  • Clean URL structure (no ?id=123 parameters)
  • 404 page exists and helps users navigate
  • No broken internal links
  • Schema markup implemented (LocalBusiness, FAQ, Service, Review)
  • Canonical tags on all pages

On-Page SEO

Every page on your site should have these elements optimized:

  • Unique title tag (under 60 characters) with target keyword
  • Unique meta description (under 160 characters) with call-to-action
  • One H1 tag per page matching the page topic
  • H2/H3 heading structure with relevant subtopics
  • Target keyword appears naturally in first 100 words
  • Internal links to 3-5 related pages (using descriptive anchor text)
  • City/location name mentioned naturally in content
  • Contact information visible (phone, email) on every page
  • Clear call-to-action on every page
  • Content is helpful and comprehensive (not thin)

Content Strategy

Content is the fuel for SEO. At minimum:

  • Homepage with clear value proposition (500+ words)
  • Individual service pages for each service you offer (500-1,000 words each)
  • About page with your story, team, credentials
  • Contact page with full NAP, hours, form, and map
  • City/location pages for each area you serve (e.g., Manchester, Nashua, Concord)
  • Blog with 5+ posts answering common customer questions
  • Publish 1-2 new blog posts per month
  • Content targets questions your customers actually ask
  • Each piece links to other relevant content on your site
  • Content demonstrates experience, expertise, authority, and trust (E-E-A-T)

Reviews & Reputation

Reviews directly impact rankings and conversion rates:

  • At least 20 Google reviews (aim for 50+)
  • Average rating of 4.2+ stars
  • Respond to every review (positive and negative)
  • Respond within 24-48 hours
  • Active review request process (ask after every job)
  • 2-4 new reviews per month minimum
  • Reviews on Yelp, Facebook, and industry-specific platforms
  • No fake reviews (Google penalizes this)

Citations & NAP Consistency

Your business information must be identical everywhere:

  • NAP (Name, Address, Phone) format is standardized
  • Same NAP on website footer, contact page, and all directories
  • Claimed and completed listings on Google, Apple Maps, Bing, Yelp, Facebook
  • Listed in major data aggregators (Data Axle, Neustar Localeze, Foursquare)
  • Listed in NH-specific directories (Chamber of Commerce, NH.gov)
  • Listed in industry-specific directories
  • Old/incorrect listings found and fixed
  • No duplicate listings on any platform
  • Citations checked quarterly for accuracy

Link Building

Backlinks from quality sources boost your authority:

  • Joined local Chamber of Commerce (with link back to your site)
  • Listed in relevant industry associations
  • Sponsor local events or organizations (earn links from event pages)
  • Create link-worthy content (guides, resources, local data)
  • Reach out to local media for coverage opportunities
  • Build relationships with complementary businesses for cross-linking
  • Claim unlinked mentions of your business
  • No paid link schemes or spammy directory submissions

Analytics & Tracking

You can’t improve what you don’t measure:

  • Google Search Console set up and verified
  • Google Analytics 4 installed
  • Form submissions tracked as conversions
  • Click-to-call tracked as conversions
  • Google Business Profile insights monitored monthly
  • Keyword rankings tracked monthly (top 10-20 target keywords)
  • Monthly SEO check-in (15 minutes) on your calendar
  • Baseline metrics documented before starting SEO

Competitive Awareness

Know what you’re up against and stay ahead:

  • Identified top 3-5 SEO competitors
  • Analyzed their GBP (reviews, categories, photos)
  • Reviewed their website (pages, content depth, speed)
  • Checked their backlink sources
  • Found content/authority gaps to exploit
  • Quarterly competitor check scheduled

Advanced (When Basics Are Done)

Only focus on these after completing everything above:

  • Voice search optimization (FAQ content, conversational tone)
  • Seasonal content strategy
  • Google Ads for immediate lead generation while SEO builds
  • Video content for GBP and website
  • Local PR and media outreach
  • A/B testing headlines and calls-to-action

The Priority Order

If you’re starting from scratch, work through these in order:

  1. Week 1: Claim and optimize Google Business Profile
  2. Week 2: Fix technical issues (speed, mobile, SSL, schema)
  3. Week 3: Optimize existing page titles, descriptions, and content
  4. Week 4: Set up analytics (Search Console + GA4)
  5. Month 2: Build Tier 1 citations, start review acquisition
  6. Month 3: Create missing service and location pages
  7. Month 4-6: Start blogging, build citations, earn backlinks
  8. Month 6+: Scale content, refine strategy based on data

You don’t need to do everything at once. Consistent progress over time wins. Even checking off 2-3 items per week puts you ahead of 90% of NH businesses.
