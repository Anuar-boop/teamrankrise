How to Get Local Backlinks in NH:
15 Strategies That Work

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 11 min read

Backlinks from local NH websites are one of the strongest ranking signals for local SEO. A link from the Manchester Chamber of Commerce is worth more for your local rankings than a link from a random national blog. Here are 15 strategies to earn them.

Why Local Backlinks Matter More

Google uses backlinks to understand geographic relevance. A link from a .gov NH website, a local newspaper, or a Manchester-based business tells Google: “This business is legitimate and relevant to this area.”

You don’t need thousands of backlinks. Most local markets can be won with 20-50 quality local links. Your competitors probably have fewer than 15.

Tier 1: Easy Wins (Get These First)

1. Chamber of Commerce Membership

Join your local Chamber of Commerce. In NH, these include Manchester, Nashua, Greater Concord, Dover, Portsmouth, and Keene chambers. Membership typically costs $200-500/year and includes a member directory listing with a link to your website.

Bonus: Chamber events and networking can also generate referrals.

2. Better Business Bureau

BBB accreditation includes a profile with a backlink. local businesses can join through the BBB of New Hampshire. Cost is based on your business size.

3. Industry Associations

Every industry has professional associations with member directories:

  • NH Home Builders Association
  • NH Bar Association (lawyers)
  • NH Dental Society
  • NH Restaurant and Lodging Association
  • NH Auto Dealers Association
  • Associated Builders and Contractors of NH/VT

4. Local Business Directories

Beyond the main citations (Yelp, Yellow Pages), NH-specific directories include:

  • NH.com business directory
  • VisitNH.gov (for tourism-related businesses)
  • NH Made (for NH-made products)
  • City-specific directories (Nashua Community, Manchester Connect, etc.)

5. Your Suppliers and Partners

Companies you buy from or partner with often have “Our Customers” or “Partners” pages. Ask to be featured with a link.

Tier 2: Moderate Effort, High Value

6. Local News and Media

NH media outlets that cover local businesses:

  • New Hampshire Union Leader
  • Concord Monitor
  • Nashua Telegraph
  • Foster’s Daily Democrat (Dover)
  • Seacoast Online (Portsmouth)
  • Keene Sentinel
  • InDepthNH.org
  • NH Business Review

How to get covered: Send a press release when you hire, expand, win an award, launch a new service, or hit a milestone. Journalists need stories — give them one.

7. Sponsorships

Sponsor local events, sports teams, or nonprofits. Most organizations list sponsors on their website with links.

  • Youth sports teams
  • Charity runs and walks
  • School events
  • Community festivals
  • Nonprofit fundraisers

Even small sponsorships ($100-500) can earn valuable local links.

8. Guest Posts on Local Blogs

Write helpful articles for local blogs, news sites, or industry publications. Offer genuine expertise, not promotional content.

  • Write a “seasonal home maintenance” article for a local real estate blog
  • Contribute a “business tips” piece to the Chamber newsletter
  • Write a guest column for local Business Review

9. Testimonials for Other Businesses

Give testimonials to businesses you use (your accountant, insurance agent, software vendor). Many publish testimonials on their website with a link to the reviewer’s business.

10. Local Resource Pages

Search for “[your city] resources” or “[your industry] NH resources.” Many community organizations, libraries, and government sites maintain resource lists. Email them to request inclusion.

Tier 3: Creative Strategies

11. Create a Local Resource

Create something genuinely useful that local websites would want to link to:

  • Local guide: “Complete Guide to Home Renovation Permits in Manchester, NH”
  • Industry data: “Average Home Service Costs in NH (2026 Data)”
  • Seasonal guide: “NH Winter Preparedness Checklist for Homeowners”
  • Local spotlight: “Top 10 Family Activities in [Your City]”

Good content earns links naturally when people find it useful enough to share.

12. Host or Co-Host Events

Host a workshop, open house, or community event. Local event calendars, news sites, and community pages will link to your event listing.

13. Scholarship Program

Create a small scholarship ($500-1,000) for local high school or UNH students. Schools and scholarship directories link to the scholarship page on your website. High authority .edu links are extremely valuable.

14. HARO / Journalist Requests

Sign up for Help A Reporter Out (HARO) or similar services. Journalists looking for expert quotes will reach out. When quoted, you typically get a backlink from major publications.

15. Fix Broken Links

Find local websites with broken outbound links (pages that 404). Contact the webmaster: “I noticed your link to [broken page] is broken. I have a similar resource that might work as a replacement: [your page].”

Use free tools like Check My Links (Chrome extension) to scan local resource pages for broken links.

What NOT to Do

  • Don’t buy links. Google detects paid link schemes. Penalties can tank your rankings for months.
  • Don’t use link farms or PBNs. Private blog networks are easily detected and penalized.
  • Don’t spam blog comments. Comment links are nofollow and look spammy.
  • Don’t trade links. Reciprocal link exchanges (“I’ll link to you if you link to me”) at scale are a red flag.
  • Don’t submit to low-quality directories. Spammy directories can hurt rather than help.

Tracking Your Backlinks

Use free tools to monitor your backlink profile:

  • Google Search Console: Links report shows who links to you
  • Ahrefs Webmaster Tools (free): More detailed backlink data
  • Moz Link Explorer (limited free): Domain authority and link overview

Check monthly as part of your SEO tracking routine.

Realistic Timeline

  • Month 1: 5-10 links from directories, Chamber, BBB, associations
  • Month 2-3: 5-10 links from sponsors, partners, testimonials
  • Month 4-6: 5-10 links from media, guest posts, resources
  • Ongoing: 1-3 new links per month from continued effort

30+ local backlinks within 6 months is very achievable and puts most local businesses ahead of their competition.
