Yelp vs Google for Local Businesses: Where to Focus in 2026 — RankRise

Local Search February 17, 2026 · 10 min read

Google Dominance: The 2026 Reality

Google dominates local search. 90%+ of local business searches happen on Google (Search + Maps). Yelp's traffic has been declining steadily since 2015.

Google Business Profile appears at the top of every local search. Google Maps integration means potential customers see you on their phones immediately. Google reviews are the most trusted review platform. Google is where the traffic is.

Simple truth: if you're only on Yelp and not on Google Business Profile, you're invisible to 90% of searchers in your market. Focus on Google first, Yelp second.

Yelp's Declining Relevance (But Still Matters for Certain Industries)

Yelp's decline is real. Their user base is shrinking. Advertisers are moving to Google Ads. Yelp reviews are seen as less trustworthy than Google reviews (partly due to Yelp's aggressive paid ad filtration).

Where Yelp Still Wins

Yelp maintains dominance in a few categories: restaurants, bars, nightlife, and personal services (hair salons, spas, tattoo shops). Yelpers are foodie-focused and entertainment-focused. If your business is in these categories, Yelp matters.

Where Yelp Is Dying

Home services (contractors, plumbers, electricians), real estate, professional services—these categories have largely abandoned Yelp. Customers search Google. Yelp is irrelevant here.

If you're a contractor in a home services category, prioritize Google 95%, Yelp 5%. If you're a restaurant, it's 70% Google, 30% Yelp.

Where Each Platform Wins

Google Business Profile Wins

  • Traffic volume: 5-10x more traffic than Yelp for most businesses
  • Review trust: Users trust Google reviews more than Yelp
  • Mobile: Google Maps is THE mobile local search tool
  • Ranking factor: Google reviews boost your organic SEO rankings
  • Integration: Google shows in Search, Maps, and Google's local pack
  • Business categories: Google covers all categories equally
  • Free tools: Extensive analytics, post scheduling, Q&A features

Yelp Wins

  • Foodie audience: Restaurants, bars, and food delivery draw Yelp users
  • Entertainment focus: Nightlife, entertainment venues, events
  • Community: Some users prefer Yelp's community and reviews
  • Legacy presence: Yelp has been around since 2004; some businesses rank well
  • Ad options: Yelp advertising can work for restaurants and entertainment

Review Strategy for Both Platforms

Google Review Strategy

Request reviews from every customer. Include a direct Google review link in follow-up emails (easier than asking them to find you on Google). Aim for 30–50 reviews in your first year.

Respond to all reviews. Thank positive reviewers. Address negative reviews professionally offline. Google rewards profiles with engagement.

Yelp Review Strategy

If your industry relies on Yelp (restaurants, services), request reviews there too. BUT: be aware that Yelp filters aggressive review requests and may remove reviews they deem suspicious. Request naturally, without over-promoting.

Respond to Yelp reviews thoughtfully. Yelp shows owner responses prominently and ranks profiles that engage.

Multi-Platform Approach

Ask customers for reviews without specifying where. Many will leave reviews on multiple platforms. Don't say "leave a Google review"—say "we'd love a review" and let them choose where. You'll get coverage on both platforms.

Claiming Both Profiles: The Setup Process

Google Business Profile

Go to google.com/business. Search your business name. Claim your profile (verify via phone, email, or postcard). Complete all sections: description, photos, hours, categories, service areas. This is non-negotiable for local SEO.

Yelp Business Account

Go to yelp.com/biz/claim. Search your business. Claim ownership (Yelp will verify via phone or email). Complete your profile: hours, description, categories, photos. Add Yelp to your website if relevant (some businesses do, some don't).

Keeping Both Updated

Use the same business information (name, address, phone) across both platforms. Inconsistencies hurt your rankings. Update hours seasonally. Add new photos quarterly.

Yelp Advertising Traps: What to Avoid

Yelp aggressively sells advertising. Salespeople call and pitch "featured placements" that supposedly boost visibility. Here's the truth:

Yelp Ads: Expensive, Uncertain ROI

Yelp advertising costs $300–$1,000+/month with no guaranteed results. You're paying to appear in Yelp search results on Yelp.com—not Google or organic search. For most non-restaurant businesses, this is a waste of money.

Restaurants may see ROI from Yelp ads (food delivery, reservations). Everyone else: skip it. Invest that budget in Google Local Services Ads or organic SEO instead.

Filtered Reviews

Yelp filters reviews aggressively. Request too many reviews too quickly, and Yelp will filter them (making them invisible). Build reviews naturally over time.

Recommendation: Skip Yelp Ads

For 95% of businesses, Yelp advertising doesn't convert. Focus your budget on Google Local SEO and Google Ads instead.

Google Business Profile vs. Yelp Business: Feature Comparison

Google Business Profile Features

  • Posts (appears in search results)
  • Q&A (customers ask, you answer)
  • Analytics (see searches, calls, routes clicked)
  • Multiple user management
  • Messaging (customers message you directly)
  • Video upload
  • Service area expansion
  • Booking integration

Yelp Business Features

  • Message filtering and spam prevention
  • Advertising options
  • Analytics (limited compared to Google)
  • Reserve table (restaurants only)
  • Order delivery (restaurants only)

Winner: Google

Google offers significantly more tools, better analytics, and tighter integration with Search and Maps. The gap has widened over the past 5 years.

Industry-Specific Recommendations

Restaurants & Bars

Focus: 70% Google, 30% Yelp. Both platforms are critical. Get reviews on both. Use Google Posts for daily specials. Consider Yelp if you're in a competitive market.

Hair Salons, Spas, Personal Services

Focus: 60% Google, 40% Yelp. Yelp still draws many service seekers. Optimize both profiles. Request reviews on both after appointments.

Home Services (Contractors, Plumbers, Electricians)

Focus: 95% Google, 5% Yelp. Most of your traffic comes from Google. Yelp is largely irrelevant for home services. Don't waste time here.

Professional Services (Lawyers, Accountants, Consultants)

Focus: 100% Google. Yelp is irrelevant for professional services. Skip Yelp. Build your Google profile and online authority.

Retail Shops

Focus: 80% Google, 20% Yelp. Google dominates retail searches. Yelp still relevant for shopping and retail reviews.

Medical & Health Services

Focus: Google + Healthgrades. Google is critical. Yelp is declining for health services. Prioritize Healthgrades and ZocDoc for medical businesses.

Apple Maps, Bing, and Other Platforms: Worth Your Time?

Apple Maps

Apple Maps traffic is growing but still ~5% of total local searches. If your customers are heavily iOS-focused (affluent urban areas), worth a quick setup. Lower priority than Google or Yelp.

Bing Local

Bing's market share is ~3%. Claim your profile but don't invest heavily. It feeds your information into other platforms (Facebook, DuckDuckGo), so it has some indirect value.

Facebook Business

Facebook has local search features. Older users search for local businesses on Facebook. Claim your profile, keep it updated, but prioritize Google.

TripAdvisor (Tourism/Restaurants)

If your business is tourism or restaurant-focused, TripAdvisor matters. Claim your profile. Get reviews. But Google and Yelp remain primary.

Recommendation: Focus on Google First

Don't spread yourself thin across 10 platforms. Master Google (highest ROI), then Yelp (if relevant to your industry), then consider others. 80/20 rule: 80% of your results come from 20% of platforms.

Conclusion: Where to Focus in 2026

For 95% of local businesses: Google Business Profile is where you get results. It's where customers search. It's where reviews matter. It's where your ranking efforts pay off.

Yelp matters if: you're a restaurant, bar, salon, or in a food/entertainment category. Otherwise, it's a secondary platform.

Strategy: Optimize Google Business Profile ruthlessly. Get 30+ Google reviews. Publish content that ranks in Google Search. If relevant to your industry, claim Yelp and get reviews there too. Skip secondary platforms unless they're essential to your category.
