Content Marketing for Local Businesses: What Actually Works in NH

By Alex · Feb 17, 2026 · 10 min read

If you search "content marketing" online, you'll find advice about viral blog posts, thought leadership, and building massive email lists. None of it applies to a plumber in Manchester or a dentist in Nashua.

Content marketing for local businesses is fundamentally different from content marketing for SaaS companies or national brands. Your goal isn't to go viral — it's to show up when someone in your area searches for what you do.

Here's what actually moves the needle for NH local businesses.

The 3 Types of Content That Matter for Local SEO

1. Service Pages (Most Important)

Before you write a single blog post, your service pages need to be dialed in. Each service you offer should have its own dedicated page — not a bullet point on a generic "Services" page.

A plumber should have separate pages for:

  • Emergency Plumbing Services
  • Water Heater Repair & Installation
  • Drain Cleaning
  • Bathroom Remodeling
  • Sump Pump Installation

Each page should answer the questions someone asking about that service would have: what's included, how long it takes, what it costs (ranges are fine), what area you serve, and why they should choose you.

This isn't "content marketing" in the traditional sense, but it's the highest-ROI content you'll ever create. Every one of these pages can rank for its own set of keywords.

2. Location Pages

If you serve multiple cities in NH, you need a page for each one. Not duplicate content with the city name swapped — genuinely localized content that mentions neighborhoods, landmarks, and local context.

We've built location pages for cities like Concord, Dover, Portsmouth, and Keene. Each one ranks for "[service] + [city]" searches — which are exactly the searches that turn into customers.

A good location page includes:

  • Mention of the city in the title, H1, and meta description
  • Local context (neighborhoods, zip codes, nearby landmarks)
  • Why you serve that area / how long you've been there
  • Reviews or testimonials from customers in that city
  • Embedded Google Map showing your service area

3. Blog Posts (Supporting Content)

Blog posts are the third priority — not the first. They support your service and location pages by building topical authority and targeting long-tail keywords.

The best blog posts for local businesses answer questions your customers actually ask:

  • "How much does [service] cost in NH?"
  • "How to choose a [service provider] in [city]"
  • "Signs you need [service]"
  • "DIY vs professional [service]: what to know"
  • "[Service] tips for New Hampshire homeowners" (seasonal angle)

Notice the pattern: every topic has a local angle and connects to a service you offer. Generic posts like "5 Fun Things to Do This Weekend" don't help your SEO unless you're a tourism business.

What NOT to Write About

We see NH businesses waste time on content that doesn't move rankings:

  • "Meet Our Team" blog posts — nice for your About page, useless for SEO
  • Industry news nobody searches for — "New EPA Regulation Affects HVAC Systems" isn't what your customers search
  • Content that doesn't mention your area — a generic "How to Fix a Leaky Faucet" guide competes with national sites. "How NH Homeowners Can Handle Winter Pipe Leaks" is much more winnable
  • Content that doesn't connect to your services — a restaurant blogging about nutrition science isn't going to drive reservations

Every piece of content should serve one of two purposes: rank for a keyword that leads to a customer, or internally link to a service/location page that does.

The Content Calendar That Actually Works

Forget weekly blog posts. For most local businesses, here's a realistic and effective schedule:

Month 1-2: Foundation

  • Write/rewrite all service pages (one per service)
  • Create location pages for your top 3-5 cities
  • Optimize your Google Business Profile description
  • Write your About page with local context and keywords

Month 3-6: Growth

  • Publish 2 blog posts per month targeting customer questions
  • Add internal links from blog posts to service and location pages
  • Create a FAQ page targeting common questions
  • Expand location pages to cover secondary service areas

Month 6+: Authority

  • Maintain 1-2 blog posts per month
  • Update older content with new information
  • Create comparison content ("Our approach vs. [alternative]")
  • Build local backlinks to your best content

This approach generates more results than publishing 52 mediocre posts a year because each piece of content is strategic and purposeful.

How to Find the Right Topics

The best topic ideas come from your customers, not keyword tools:

  1. Check Google's "People Also Ask" — search your main service + city and expand the "People Also Ask" boxes. These are real questions real people search
  2. Review your emails and calls — what do customers ask before hiring you? Each question is a potential blog post
  3. Look at competitor blogs — what are other NH businesses in your industry writing about?
  4. Check Google Search Console — see what searches are already bringing people to your site, then create content to better serve those queries. If you haven't set this up yet, read our guide on getting your business on Google
  5. Seasonal angles — NH businesses have a huge advantage here. Winter prep, spring cleanup, summer maintenance, fall preparation — these seasonal topics repeat annually and always get searches

The Internal Linking Strategy (Critical)

Creating content is only half the job. Connecting it through internal links is what makes it work for SEO.

Every page on your site should link to at least 2-3 other relevant pages. Here's how to think about it:

  • Blog posts link to service pages — "Learn more about our local SEO services"
  • Service pages link to blog posts — "Read our guide on [related topic]"
  • Location pages link to service pages and blog posts — connect the local context to your offerings
  • Blog posts link to other blog posts — create topic clusters around your core services

This creates a web of connected content that tells Google "this site is an authority on [topic] in [location]." It's the same strategy we use on this site — notice how every article links to 10+ other pages? That's intentional.

Content Quality: What Google Actually Wants

Google has made it clear with their Helpful Content Update: content needs to be written for humans first, search engines second. What this means practically:

  • Write from experience — share real examples from your work, real numbers, real stories from NH customers
  • Be specific — "Our drain cleaning service in Dover typically takes 1-2 hours and costs $150-300" beats "Contact us for pricing!"
  • Don't stuff keywords — if you're mentioning "plumber Manchester NH" 15 times on a page, that's too many. Write naturally and the keywords will appear naturally. See our guide on common DIY SEO mistakes
  • Answer the question completely — if someone searches "how much does HVAC replacement cost in NH," give them the actual range with factors that affect price. Don't hide the answer behind a contact form
  • Update content regularly — a pricing guide from 2023 is outdated. Update it annually at minimum

Measuring What Works

You don't need expensive analytics tools. Google Search Console (free) tells you:

  • Which pages get the most search impressions and clicks
  • What keywords you're ranking for (and your position)
  • Which pages are improving or declining
  • Mobile usability issues (see our mobile-friendly guide)

Check monthly. If a blog post isn't getting any impressions after 3 months, it's targeting a keyword nobody searches. Update it or redirect it to a page that does perform.

Real Example: NH Home Services Company

A home services company in southern NH came to us with a 5-page website: Home, About, Services, Gallery, Contact. They were invisible on Google for everything except their business name.

Here's what we built:

  • 6 individual service pages (one per service they offer)
  • 4 location pages (Manchester, Nashua, Concord, Merrimack)
  • 8 blog posts targeting customer questions
  • Every page internally linked to 3-5 related pages

Within 4 months, they were ranking on page 1 for 12 different service+city combinations. Their organic traffic went from ~50 visits/month to ~400. Phone calls from the website tripled.

That's 18 pages of focused, locally-targeted content doing the work that one generic "Services" page couldn't.
