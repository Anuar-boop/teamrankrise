How to Rank in Google's Local Pack (Map Pack)

Alex Feb 17, 2026 11 min read

When someone searches "plumber near me" or "dentist Manchester NH," Google shows a map with three businesses underneath. This is the Local Pack — and it gets 42% of all clicks on local searches. If you're not in those three spots, you're invisible to nearly half your potential customers.

This guide breaks down exactly how Google decides which three businesses to show, and gives you an 8-step playbook to earn your spot.

What Is the Local Pack?

The Local Pack (also called the Map Pack or Local 3-Pack) is the map + three business listings that appear at the top of Google for local searches. It shows:

  • Business name
  • Star rating and review count
  • Address (or service area)
  • Phone number
  • Hours of operation
  • Website link

It appears above the regular organic results, which means Local Pack businesses get seen before any website. For mobile users (60%+ of local searches), the Local Pack often fills the entire screen.

The 3 Ranking Factors Google Uses

Google has confirmed that Local Pack rankings are based on three factors. Understanding them is the key to ranking.

1. Relevance

How well does your business match what the person searched for? This comes from:

  • Your Google Business Profile categories (primary + secondary)
  • Your GBP description and services
  • Your website content and keywords
  • Reviews that mention specific services

2. Distance

How close is your business to the person searching? This factor is largely outside your control — Google uses the searcher's location to determine results. However:

  • Having a verified physical address helps
  • "Near me" optimization ensures Google knows your service area
  • Location-specific content on your website signals where you operate

3. Prominence

How well-known and trusted is your business? This is where most of the optimization happens:

  • Reviews — quantity, quality, recency, and response rate
  • Citations — consistent business listings across the web
  • Backlinks — links from other websites, especially local ones
  • Website authority — domain authority and on-page SEO
  • GBP activity — regular posts, photos, and Q&A responses

The 8-Step Local Pack Playbook

Step 1: Complete Your Google Business Profile (100%)

An incomplete profile won't rank. Period. Fill out:

  • Primary category — the single most accurate category for your business
  • Secondary categories — add 3-5 relevant ones (don't over-stuff)
  • Business description — 750 characters, keyword-rich, follow our templates
  • Services/menu — list every service with descriptions
  • Hours — include special hours for holidays
  • Attributes — wheelchair accessible, women-owned, veteran-owned, etc.
  • Products — add your top services as "products" for more visibility

Step 2: Get Photos Working For You

Businesses with 100+ photos get 520% more calls than average. Upload:

  • Exterior photos from multiple angles (Google uses these to verify location)
  • Interior photos showing your workspace
  • Team photos with real employees
  • Work examples (before/after, completed projects)
  • Product photos if applicable

NH tip: Include seasonal photos. A snow-covered storefront tells Google you're open year-round and gives local context.

Step 3: Build a Review Engine

Reviews are the third most important ranking factor. You need:

  • Volume — more than your competitors in the Local Pack
  • Recency — at least 2-3 new reviews per month
  • Quality — 4.5+ star average
  • Keywords — reviews that naturally mention your services and location
  • Responses — respond to 100% of reviews within 24 hours

Read our guide to getting more Google reviews and our first 50 reviews playbook.

Step 4: Nail Your NAP Consistency

Your Name, Address, and Phone number must be identical across every listing online. Even small variations confuse Google.

  • Audit your top 20 directory listings
  • Fix any inconsistencies — exact character-for-character match
  • Use the same phone number everywhere (preferably local 603 area code)
  • Read our NAP consistency guide

Step 5: Optimize Your Website for Local

Your website backs up your GBP. Google cross-references the two. Ensure:

  • NAP matches GBP exactly (in footer of every page)
  • LocalBusiness schema markup on homepage
  • Service-specific pages targeting local keywords
  • Location pages if serving multiple areas
  • Mobile-friendly design
  • Fast loading speed
  • Embedded Google Map on contact page

Step 6: Build Local Citations

Citations are mentions of your business on other websites. Focus on:

  • Core directories — Yelp, Facebook, Apple Maps, Bing Places, BBB
  • Industry directories — specific to your trade
  • NH directories — NH.gov, local chambers, town websites
  • Data aggregators — Neustar/Localeze, Foursquare, Data.com

Our full local citations guide lists every directory that matters for NH businesses.

Step 7: Earn Quality Backlinks

Links from other websites signal trust to Google. For local businesses, the best links come from:

  • Local news outlets and blogs
  • Chamber of Commerce memberships
  • Community sponsorships
  • Partnerships with complementary businesses
  • Industry associations

Read our guide to getting local backlinks in NH.

Step 8: Stay Active on GBP

Google rewards active businesses. Weekly activities:

  • Post once per week (GBP posting guide)
  • Upload 2-3 new photos per week
  • Respond to all reviews and questions
  • Update hours for holidays/changes
  • Add new services as your business evolves

What Kills Local Pack Rankings

Avoid these common mistakes that keep businesses out of the Pack:

  1. Wrong primary category — "Construction Company" instead of "Roofing Contractor"
  2. Fake address — using a PO box or virtual office (against Google's guidelines)
  3. Keyword stuffing business name — adding "Best Plumber Manchester" to your name
  4. Ignoring reviews — competitors with 200 reviews will outrank you at 15
  5. Inconsistent NAP — different phone numbers or addresses confuse Google
  6. No website — Google cross-references GBP with your site
  7. Inactive profile — no posts, no photos, no responses for months
  8. Duplicate listings — having multiple GBP listings creates conflicts

Advanced Local Pack Tactics

Optimize for "Near Me" Searches

Google doesn't match the literal phrase "near me" — it uses the searcher's location. But you can optimize for this by ensuring strong local signals. Read our "near me" optimization guide.

Use GBP Products and Services

The "Products" section in GBP gives you extra keyword-rich real estate. Add your top 5-10 services with descriptions, pricing (if applicable), and photos.

Answer Questions in GBP Q&A

Proactively add Q&A to your profile. Ask and answer common questions yourself — this creates keyword-rich content directly on your GBP.

Track Your Rankings

Local Pack rankings vary by location — someone in Manchester sees different results than someone in Concord. Use tools like BrightLocal or Google Search Console to track your positions across different locations.

Bottom Line

The Local Pack is the most valuable real estate in local search. Getting into those three spots means:

  • Maximum visibility — seen before any organic result
  • Click-to-call on mobile — instant leads from phone taps
  • Social proof — star rating and review count visible immediately
  • Map visibility — your pin shows when people browse Google Maps

The playbook is straightforward: complete GBP + consistent NAP + reviews + local SEO + activity. It's not complicated — it just takes consistent effort over time. But every NH business that follows these 8 steps will eventually outrank competitors who don't.

Start today. Set up your GBP if you haven't, or optimize your description if you have. Then work through the remaining steps one per week.
