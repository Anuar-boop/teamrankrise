How to Create a Local SEO Content Calendar for Your NH Business

Alex Feb 17, 2026 11 min read

Most local businesses know they should be creating content. But when you sit down to write, the blank page wins. What should you write about? How often? What keywords matter?

A content calendar solves all of this. It's a plan that tells you exactly what to publish, when to publish it, and what keywords each piece targets. No more guessing, no more last-minute scrambles.

Here's how to plan 12 months of content marketing in one sitting.

Step 1: List Your Core Services

Start with what you actually do. Write down every service you offer. For a plumber, that might be:

  • Emergency plumbing
  • Drain cleaning
  • Water heater installation/repair
  • Bathroom remodeling
  • Pipe repair/replacement
  • Sewer line services
  • Faucet installation

Each service becomes a content pillar. You'll create multiple pieces of content around each one, building topical authority that Google rewards with higher rankings.

Step 2: Build Keyword Clusters

For each service, use the keyword research process to find related search terms. Group them into clusters:

Service: Water Heater

  • Transactional: "water heater installation manchester nh" → Service page
  • Informational: "how long does a water heater last" → Blog post
  • Informational: "tank vs tankless water heater" → Blog post
  • Informational: "signs your water heater needs replacing" → Blog post
  • Comparison: "best water heater brands 2026" → Blog post

Repeat for every service. You'll quickly have 30-50+ content ideas.

Step 3: Categorize by Content Type

Organize your ideas into 5 content types that cover the full customer journey:

Type Purpose Example
How-to guides Answer common questions "How to unclog a drain without chemicals"
Comparison posts Help people decide "Tankless vs tank water heaters: pros and cons"
Cost guides Capture pricing searches "How much does a new furnace cost in NH?"
Seasonal content Timely, seasonal relevance "Winterize your plumbing: NH homeowner's checklist"
Local guides Build local authority "Best parks in Manchester NH (for families)"

Step 4: Set Your Publishing Frequency

Consistency beats volume. Here's what works for most local businesses:

  • Minimum viable: 2 posts per month — enough to build authority over time
  • Good: 4 posts per month (weekly) — noticeable ranking improvements within 3-6 months
  • Aggressive: 8+ posts per month — fastest results, but requires significant time or hiring a writer

For NH small businesses, 2-4 posts per month is the sweet spot. That's 24-48 posts per year — enough to cover your key topics without burning out.

Step 5: Map Content to the Calendar

Now slot your content ideas into a 12-month calendar. Use this framework:

Monthly Content Mix (4 posts/month)

  • Week 1: How-to guide related to your #1 service
  • Week 2: Local or seasonal content
  • Week 3: Cost/comparison guide for a secondary service
  • Week 4: FAQ or glossary-style content covering common questions

Seasonal Considerations for NH

Season Content Themes
Spring (Mar-May) Spring cleaning, damage assessment, outdoor prep, allergy season
Summer (Jun-Aug) Outdoor projects, vacation prep, AC maintenance, tourism-related
Fall (Sep-Nov) Winterization, heating prep, back-to-school, holiday planning
Winter (Dec-Feb) Emergency services, winter tips, new year planning, indoor projects

Pro tip: Publish seasonal content 6-8 weeks before the season starts. Google takes time to index and rank content, so "winterize your home" should go live in September, not December.

Step 6: Add Internal Links to the Plan

Before writing each piece, note which existing pages it should link to. This builds your internal link network:

  • Every blog post links to at least 1 service page
  • Every blog post links to 2-3 related blog posts
  • Every blog post includes a CTA to your free audit or contact page
  • Pillar posts link down to supporting posts; supporting posts link up to pillar posts

Step 7: Track and Adjust

Your calendar isn't set in stone. Check Search Console and Analytics monthly to see:

  • Which topics get the most traffic? Create more content in that cluster
  • Which posts generate leads? Promote those and create similar content
  • Which posts have low traffic? Update them with more depth, better keywords, or fresh data
  • What are people searching for that you haven't covered? Add those to next month's calendar

Tools for Managing Your Calendar

You don't need fancy tools. Start simple:

  • Google Sheets — columns for date, topic, keyword, status, URL. Share with your team
  • Google Calendar — set publish dates as events with reminders
  • Trello or Notion — if you prefer kanban boards (Idea → Draft → Review → Published)

The best tool is the one you'll actually use. A Google Sheet you update weekly beats a $200/month content platform you forget about.
