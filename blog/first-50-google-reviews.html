How to Get Your First 50 Google Reviews
(NH Business Guide)

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 10 min read

50 Google reviews. That’s the number where things change. Below 50, you’re one of many. Above 50, you stand out in the map pack, earn more trust from potential customers, and build a defensible competitive advantage.

Most NH businesses have fewer than 20 reviews. Getting to 50 puts you in the top 5-10% of local businesses in your area. Here’s exactly how to get there.

Why 50 Is the Magic Number

  • Google’s trust threshold. Businesses with 50+ reviews are significantly more likely to appear in the local 3-pack for competitive searches.
  • Consumer confidence. Consumers view businesses with 50+ reviews as “well-established.” Under 20 feels unproven.
  • Statistical reliability. With 50+ reviews, one bad review barely moves your average. With 5 reviews, one 1-star drops you from 5.0 to 4.2.
  • Competitive advantage. In most NH markets, 50 reviews puts you ahead of 80-90% of competitors.

The 3-Phase Plan

Phase 1: The Foundation (Reviews 1-15) — Weeks 1-4

Start with the people most likely to say yes: your happiest recent customers.

Make a list of your 20-30 best customers from the past 12 months. These are people who:

  • Said thank you or complimented your work
  • Referred other customers to you
  • Came back for repeat business
  • Expressed satisfaction verbally

Reach out personally:

  • Call or text (not email for this first batch — personal touch matters)
  • Remind them of the specific work you did
  • Ask directly: “Would you mind leaving us a Google review? It would really help our small business.”
  • Send the direct link to your review page immediately after they say yes

How to get your direct review link:

  1. Google your business name
  2. Click “Ask for reviews” on your Google Business Profile
  3. Copy the short link Google gives you
  4. Or search “Google review link generator” and enter your Place ID

Expected result: 10-15 reviews from this personal outreach. Conversion rate is typically 40-60% when asked personally.

Phase 2: The System (Reviews 16-35) — Months 2-4

Now build a repeatable process that runs automatically for every new customer.

The post-job review request:

  1. Complete the job
  2. Confirm customer is satisfied (verbal check)
  3. Within 24 hours, send a text message or email:

“Hi [Name], it was great working with you on [specific project]. If you have a minute, we’d really appreciate a Google review. It helps other people in [City] find us: [direct link]. Thank you!”

Tips for this phase:

  • Text messages get 3-5x higher response rates than email for review requests
  • Send within 24 hours while the experience is fresh
  • Personalize with their name and the specific work done
  • One follow-up is fine (3-5 days later). Two is the maximum.
  • Never offer incentives for reviews (violates Google’s policies and can get your reviews removed)

Expected result: 15-20 reviews over 2-3 months, depending on customer volume.

Phase 3: The Acceleration (Reviews 36-50+) — Months 5-8

Layer in additional review channels to hit 50 and beyond.

In-person signage:

  • QR code linking to your Google review page at your front desk/counter
  • Business card with review link (hand to satisfied customers)
  • “Find us on Google” sticker on your door/vehicle

Email signature:

Add a line to your email signature: “Happy with our service? Leave us a Google review: [link]”

Invoice/receipt follow-up:

Add a review request to your post-service invoice or receipt. Many accounting/CRM systems can automate this.

Social media:

Occasionally post on Facebook: “We love hearing from our customers! If we’ve helped you, a Google review means the world to us: [link]”

Review Request Scripts That Work

In Person (Right After Service)

“Glad we could help today! If you have a minute later, we’d really appreciate a Google review. I’ll text you the link so it’s easy.”

Text Message (24 Hours After)

“Hi [Name]! Thanks for choosing [Business Name] for your [service]. If you’re happy with the results, a quick Google review would mean a lot to us: [link]. Thank you!”

Email (For Larger Projects)

“Hi [Name], thank you for trusting us with your [project]. We hope you’re thrilled with the results! If you have a moment, sharing your experience on Google helps other [city] residents find quality [service]: [link]. We appreciate your support!”

What to Do About the Reviews You Get

  • Respond to every single review within 24-48 hours
  • Positive reviews: Thank them by name, mention specific work, express genuine appreciation
  • Negative reviews: Follow our negative review response guide
  • Include keywords naturally in your responses: “Thank you for choosing us for your bathroom remodel in Manchester!”

Google’s Rules (Don’t Break These)

  • No incentives. Don’t offer discounts, coupons, or gifts in exchange for reviews.
  • No gating. Don’t filter only happy customers to Google and unhappy ones to a private form.
  • No fake reviews. Don’t have friends, family, or employees post fake reviews.
  • No review stations. Don’t let customers leave reviews from your business’s device (same IP = flag).
  • No bulk requests. Don’t email blast your entire customer list at once. A sudden surge looks suspicious.

Realistic Timeline for NH Businesses

  • Home services (5-15 jobs/month): 50 reviews in 6-10 months
  • Restaurants (many daily customers): 50 reviews in 2-4 months
  • Professional services (2-5 clients/month): 50 reviews in 10-18 months
  • Retail (variable): 50 reviews in 4-8 months

The key variable is how many satisfied customers you interact with and how consistently you ask. A business that asks every single customer will get there 3x faster than one that asks “when they remember.”

After 50: Keep Going

50 is a milestone, not a finish line. Your competitors will eventually catch up. Keep your review system running and aim for steady monthly growth. The review velocity signal is one of the strongest local SEO factors — Google rewards businesses that consistently earn new reviews.
