How to Outrank Your Local Competitors on Google

Alex Feb 17, 2026 11 min read

You search for your service in your town. Your competitors show up. You don't. It's frustrating — especially when you know your business is better. The good news: local search rankings aren't permanent. With the right strategy, you can systematically overtake the businesses currently sitting in those top spots.

This isn't about tricks or shortcuts. It's about understanding exactly what your competitors are doing, finding the gaps in their strategy, and executing better and more consistently than they do.

Step 1: Know Who You're Actually Competing Against

Your local search competitors aren't always who you think. Do this search right now:

  1. Open an incognito/private browser window
  2. Search for your main service + your town (e.g., "plumber Manchester NH")
  3. Write down the top 3 businesses in the Local Pack
  4. Write down the top 5 websites in organic results
  5. Repeat for 3-5 of your most important keyword variations

You should see patterns — the same 3-5 businesses keep appearing. These are your real competitors, not necessarily the businesses you think of as competitors in the real world.

Step 2: Win the Google Business Profile Battle

The Local Pack is where most local searches convert. GBP signals account for ~32% of ranking factors. Here's how to beat competitors here:

Category Optimization

Check what categories your competitors use (visible in their listing). Make sure your primary category is more specific than theirs. "Emergency Plumber" beats "Plumber" if you do emergency work. "Cosmetic Dentist" beats "Dentist" for cosmetic searches.

Review Volume Gap

If your top competitor has 150 reviews and you have 30, that's your biggest gap. Close it:

  • Calculate the gap: 150 - 30 = 120 reviews behind
  • Set monthly targets: 10-15 new reviews per month
  • At 15/month, you'll match them in 8 months — and they're probably not growing as fast
  • Follow our review generation playbook

Photo and Post Activity

Most competitors post sporadically or not at all. Weekly GBP posts and regular photo uploads signal to Google that you're an active, legitimate business. If your competitor hasn't posted in 3 months, you win this factor easily.

Complete Every Field

Write a full 750-character description, list all services, add attributes, set special hours. Most businesses leave 40-60% of their GBP incomplete. Being 100% complete is an easy win.

Step 3: Build a Better Website

Your website is the foundation of organic rankings. Here's how to outperform competitors:

Content Depth

Count your competitor's pages (search "site:theirdomain.com"). If they have 10 pages and you have 5, create more. But don't just add pages — add better pages:

  • Dedicated service pages for every service (not a list of all services on one page)
  • Location pages for every town you serve
  • Blog content targeting questions your customers ask
  • FAQ pages addressing common objections

On-Page SEO

Check if competitors are doing basic on-page SEO. Look at their page titles (visible in the browser tab). If they say "Home" or just their business name, they're not optimized. You can easily outrank them by:

  • Writing keyword-optimized title tags: "[Service] in [Town], NH | [Business Name]"
  • Adding schema markup (most competitors don't have this)
  • Building a strong internal linking structure
  • Optimizing for the right keywords

Page Speed

Test your site and competitors' sites at PageSpeed Insights. If they score 40 and you score 85, that's a ranking advantage. Speed optimization is often the easiest technical win because most small business websites are slow.

Step 4: Out-Content Your Competition

Content is the long-term competitive moat. Here's the strategy:

Find Their Content Gaps

  1. Look at their blog or content section
  2. Search for keywords they're ranking for (use free tools)
  3. Identify topics they haven't covered
  4. Write better content on those topics first

The 10x Content Strategy

When a competitor has content on a topic, don't just match it — make yours 10x better:

  • If they wrote 500 words, write 1,500 with more detail and examples
  • If they have no visuals, add tables, checklists, and diagrams
  • If their content is generic, make yours specific to NH
  • If their content is outdated (2023 data), use current 2026 information

Consistency Wins

Most small businesses publish a few blog posts and stop. Use a content calendar to publish consistently — even one post every 2 weeks puts you ahead of 90% of local competitors. Over a year, that's 26 pieces of content targeting 26 different keyword clusters.

Step 5: Build a Stronger Link Profile

Backlinks remain a significant ranking factor. Most local businesses have very few links, so even small efforts make a big difference.

Check Competitors' Backlinks

Use free tools (Ahrefs free backlink checker, Moz Link Explorer) to see who links to your competitors. Common sources:

  • Chamber of Commerce memberships
  • Local business directories
  • News articles or press coverage
  • Sponsor mentions on event websites
  • Partner business websites

Get the Same Links — Plus More

  1. Match their directory listings — get listed everywhere they are
  2. Join what they've joined — same Chamber, same associations
  3. Sponsor what they sponsor — plus find new local events
  4. Create linkable content — guides, tools, or resources people want to reference
  5. Build relationships — local partnerships that include links

You don't need hundreds of links. In local SEO, 20-30 quality local links can be enough to overtake competitors who rely on the same 5-10 directory listings.

Reviews impact both rankings and conversion. A business with more reviews AND a higher rating will almost always win.

Step 7: Target Their Weak Keywords

Every competitor has keywords they're not targeting. Find and claim them:

Keyword Gap Analysis

  1. Long-tail keywords — If they rank for "plumber Manchester NH," target "emergency plumber Manchester NH," "affordable plumber Manchester NH," "24 hour plumber Manchester NH"
  2. Service variations — If they have a page for "AC repair," create pages for "AC repair," "AC installation," "AC maintenance," "ductless mini-split installation"
  3. Location variations — If they target Manchester, also target surrounding towns they miss (Bedford, Goffstown, Hooksett, Auburn)
  4. Question keywords — "How much does a plumber cost in NH?" "When should I replace my furnace?" — these informational queries build authority

The Competitive Overtake Timeline

How long does it take to outrank an established competitor? It depends on the gap:

Gap Size Description Timeline
SmallSimilar reviews, similar website, you just need optimization2-4 months
MediumThey have 2-3x more reviews, better website, some content4-8 months
LargeEstablished brand, 200+ reviews, strong website, active content8-14 months
Very LargeDominant player, 500+ reviews, years of content, strong links12-24 months

Important: you don't need to overtake them on everything at once. SEO is a long game, and consistent effort over time always beats sporadic bursts.

What Most Businesses Get Wrong

  1. Copying competitors instead of analyzing them — Don't replicate; improve on what they do
  2. Focusing on one factor — Rankings are multi-factor. Reviews alone won't overcome a terrible website.
  3. Giving up too early — Most businesses quit after 2-3 months when they don't see instant results
  4. Ignoring their own strengths — Maybe you have better service, niche expertise, or community ties. Lean into those.
  5. Obsessing over rankings — Track calls, forms, and revenue — that's what actually matters
  6. Not tracking progress — Without measurement, you don't know if your strategy is working. Use analytics and Search Console.

The 30-Day Competitive Sprint

If you want to start closing the gap immediately, here's a 30-day action plan:

Week 1: Intelligence

  • Complete the competitor scorecard for your top 3 competitors
  • Identify the top 3 gaps to close first
  • Set up Google Analytics and Search Console if not already

Week 2: GBP Optimization

  • Complete your GBP to 100%
  • Upload 20+ high-quality photos
  • Write your first 2 GBP posts
  • Send review requests to your last 20 customers

Week 3: Website

  • Optimize title tags and meta descriptions on your top 5 pages
  • Add schema markup to your homepage
  • Create 1 new service page targeting a gap keyword
  • Fix any speed issues

Week 4: Content + Links

  • Publish your first blog post targeting a keyword competitors miss
  • Submit to 5 local directories you're missing
  • Reach out to 3 potential local partners for backlinks
  • Continue collecting reviews (aim for 5+ this week)

Bottom Line

Outranking competitors isn't about one big move — it's about winning more small battles than they do, consistently, over time. Better GBP, more reviews, stronger website, deeper content, more links, faster speed. Each improvement compounds.

Your competitors are probably not actively optimizing. They got their rankings by being around longer or having more reviews. An active, strategic approach will overtake a passive one — it just takes patience and consistency.

Start with the competitor scorecard. Know exactly where you stand. Then close one gap at a time, starting with the easiest wins first.
