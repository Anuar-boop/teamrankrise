HVAC SEO: How Heating &
Cooling Companies Can Dominate
Local Search

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 15 min read

It’s 11 PM on a freezing January night. A homeowner’s furnace dies. Their next move? They grab their phone and search “furnace repair near me” or “emergency heating repair [city name].”

That search is worth $40-100+ in Google Ads. But if your HVAC company shows up in the top 3 organic results instead of paying for ads, that lead is essentially free. And you’ll get 10x more of them than your competitors.

HVAC is one of the most lucrative local service industries for SEO. Homeowners are desperate, the service area is local, and the search intent is crystal clear. Yet most HVAC companies leave thousands of dollars on the table by ignoring local search optimization.

This guide shows you exactly how to rank higher on Google, dominate your local service area, and turn seasonal search spikes into consistent revenue.

Why HVAC Companies Need Local SEO Right Now

Let’s look at the numbers.

Seasonal Search Spikes Drive Predictable Revenue

HVAC isn’t random. It’s predictable and seasonal:

  • Summer (June-August): AC repair searches spike 250-300%. People search “AC repair near me,” “air conditioning emergency,” and “AC repair [city].”
  • Winter (December-February): Furnace and heating searches spike 200-280%. “Furnace repair near me” becomes one of the top service queries.
  • Spring (March-April): HVAC maintenance searches spike. Homeowners look for “HVAC inspection” and “spring AC tune-up.”
  • Fall (September-October): Furnace maintenance and inspection searches increase as winter approaches.

If your company ranks on page 1 of Google during these seasonal peaks, you can handle 2-3x your normal call volume without paying for ads.

Google Ads CPCs Are $40-100+

An average Google Ads click for “emergency AC repair” or “furnace repair” costs $40-100 per click. Many HVAC companies in competitive markets pay $75-150/click. With a typical 10-15% conversion rate, that’s $400-1,000 per lead.

A page 1 ranking in organic search? That’s the same lead for pennies.

Emergency Searches Are High Intent

When someone searches “24 hour furnace repair,” they’re not comparing prices. They need help now. If your company shows up first, they will call you first. Emergency call volume drives recurring service and maintenance contracts.

Your Competitors Are Sleeping

Most HVAC companies don’t do SEO. They rely on referrals, ads, or hope. This means the competitive landscape is actually less crowded than other industries. If you invest in local SEO now, you’ll dominate for years.

Step 1: Optimize Your Google Business Profile for HVAC

Your Google Business Profile is where 90% of local searches land. If it’s incomplete or poorly optimized, you’re leaving deals on the table.

Here’s exactly how to optimize it for HVAC:

Use the Right Categories

Google lets you list multiple categories. Use all of them strategically:

  • Primary: “HVAC Contractor”
  • Secondary: “Air Conditioning Repair Service”
  • Tertiary: “Furnace Repair Service”
  • Additional: “Heat Pump Installation” (if applicable)

Each category opens doors to different keyword searches. More categories = more visibility. Learn which categories actually drive leads here.

Define Your Service Areas Precisely

This is critical. Go to your GBP dashboard and list every service area your company covers. Don’t just say “Massachusetts.” List each town, city, and ZIP code.

Why? Because when someone searches “furnace repair in [specific town],” Google shows businesses that claim that area. The more areas you claim, the more searches you capture.

Complete Your Hours and Emergency Hours

HVAC is 24/7 for emergencies. Make this clear:

  • Set your normal business hours
  • Add emergency/after-hours phone number in the description
  • Note in your description: “24/7 Emergency Furnace & AC Repair Available”

Homeowners need to know you’re reachable at midnight on a Saturday.

Fill Your Description With Keywords

Your 750-character GBP description should include key services and keywords:

“Emergency HVAC repair, AC installation, furnace maintenance, and heat pump service for [city name] and surrounding areas. 24/7 availability for heating and cooling emergencies. Licensed, insured HVAC contractors serving [region] since [year].”

This signals to Google what you do, and it helps customers immediately understand your services.

Add Before/After Photos and Videos

The more visual content in your GBP, the higher it ranks. Upload:

  • Before/after installation photos
  • New equipment photos
  • Team photos
  • Ductwork and maintenance photos
  • A 30-60 second video of your team or facility

See our complete GBP optimization guide for more tactics.

Step 2: Build a Seasonal Content Strategy

Content is where you separate yourself from competitors. Most HVAC companies have static websites with the same page about AC repair and furnace repair. You’re going to build seasonal content that captures search demand before your competitors even think about it.

Winter Content (November-February)

Target these keywords:

  • “Emergency furnace repair [city]”
  • “24 hour heating repair”
  • “Furnace stopped working”
  • “Furnace won’t turn on”
  • “Furnace maintenance before winter”
  • “Heat pump repair in freezing weather”

Content ideas:

  • “How to Tell If Your Furnace Needs Repair (5 Warning Signs)”
  • “What to Do if Your Furnace Stops Working in Winter”
  • “Furnace Maintenance Checklist: Get Ready Before Winter”
  • “Why Your Furnace is Making Strange Noises”
  • “Heat Pump vs. Furnace: Which is Best for [Region]?”

Summer Content (May-September)

Target these keywords:

  • “Emergency AC repair [city]”
  • “Air conditioning repair near me”
  • “AC not cooling”
  • “AC repair cost”
  • “Summer AC maintenance”
  • “New AC installation [city]”

Content ideas:

  • “AC Repair Cost Guide 2026: What to Expect”
  • “5 Reasons Your AC Stopped Working (And How to Fix It)”
  • “Should You Repair or Replace Your AC Unit?”
  • “AC Maintenance: Keep Your Unit Running All Summer”
  • “New AC Installation Cost and Benefits in [Region]”

Spring/Fall Content (Year-Round)

Target these keywords:

  • “HVAC inspection [city]”
  • “Duct cleaning cost”
  • “HVAC maintenance plan”
  • “Air quality inspection”
  • “Thermostat installation”

Content ideas:

  • “Complete HVAC Maintenance Checklist for Spring”
  • “Duct Cleaning: Benefits, Cost, and When You Need It”
  • “How Often Should You Replace Your HVAC Filter?”
  • “Smart Thermostat Installation and Benefits”

The key: publish this content 4-6 weeks before the seasonal peak. If winter furnace repairs spike in December, publish furnace content in October. Google needs time to crawl and index. See how to optimize each piece of content here.

Step 3: Create Dedicated Service Pages

Don’t bury your services in a generic page. Each major service should have its own dedicated page optimized for search:

Essential HVAC Service Pages

  • /ac-repair/ — Target “AC repair [city]” and “air conditioning repair”
  • /furnace-repair/ — Target “furnace repair [city]” and “heating repair”
  • /heat-pump-service/ — Target “heat pump repair” and “heat pump installation”
  • /duct-cleaning/ — Target “duct cleaning [city]”
  • /hvac-installation/ — Target “AC installation” and “furnace installation”
  • /hvac-maintenance/ — Target “HVAC maintenance plan” and “maintenance agreement”

Each Page Should Include

  • Service-specific headline: “Emergency AC Repair in [City] — Available 24/7”
  • Problem/solution format: Describe common issues and how you fix them
  • Cost expectations: Budget ranges or call-for-quote messaging
  • Timeline: “Same-day service available” or “Usually same week”
  • Testimonials: Specific reviews mentioning the service
  • Photo/video: Visual proof of the service
  • Call-to-action: “Call [number] for emergency service” or “Schedule Online”

Each page should be 800-1,200 words, well-formatted, and mobile-friendly. Use this on-page SEO checklist to guide you.

Step 4: Target Emergency Keywords Aggressively

Emergency searches are gold. Someone searching “emergency AC repair near me” is ready to pay and ready to call immediately. These keywords convert better than anything else.

Emergency Keyword Targets

  • “Emergency [service] repair [city]”
  • “24 hour [service] repair”
  • “[Service] repair same day [city]”
  • “[Service] repair now [city]”
  • “[Service] stopped working”
  • “[Service] broken [city]”
  • “After hours [service] repair”
  • “Urgent [service] repair [city]”

How to Target These

  1. Create pages or blog posts specifically targeting emergency keywords
  2. Mention “24/7 emergency service” prominently on all service pages
  3. Make your phone number (especially emergency number) easy to find
  4. Add schema markup for local service availability
  5. Include these terms in your Google Business Profile description

A simple page titled “Emergency AC Repair in [City] — Call Now” can rank #1 for this search and generate 20-30 emergency calls per month during summer.

Step 5: Build a Reviews and Reputation System

Google heavily weights reviews in local search rankings. More reviews + higher average rating = higher rank. For HVAC companies, this is a competitive advantage.

Strategic Review Generation

  • After service calls: Text or email customers a link to leave a Google review. Do this while they’re satisfied.
  • After seasonal maintenance: Follow-up email 2 days later: “Thanks for choosing us for your spring AC maintenance. If we did great work, we’d love a Google review.”
  • Make it easy: Use a QR code or shortened URL. The easier you make it, the more reviews you’ll get.
  • Incentivize (legally): Monthly drawing from reviewers, referral bonuses, or discount on next service
  • Target your best customers: If someone calls you back for a second service, they liked you. Ask for a review.

Handling Negative Reviews

You’ll get bad reviews. How you respond matters:

  • Respond within 24 hours — Shows you care
  • Stay professional — Don’t argue or get defensive
  • Offer to fix it — “We apologize for the experience. Can we make this right?”
  • Move it offline — Ask them to call or email you privately
  • Follow up — If you fix the issue, ask them to update their review

Responding to negative reviews actually improves your ranking because it shows engagement. See why reviews matter and how to get them here.

Step 6: Use Before/After and Educational Content

Visual content gets shared more, ranks better, and builds trust. As an HVAC company, you have amazing visual opportunities:

Before/After Photos

  • New AC unit installation (old unit vs. new)
  • Furnace replacement
  • Ductwork repairs
  • Thermostat upgrades
  • Heat pump installation

Post these to your blog with detailed descriptions. Google Images is a huge traffic source for local service companies.

Educational Videos (30-90 seconds)

  • “How to Tell If Your AC Needs Repair”
  • “HVAC Maintenance Tips That Save Money”
  • “Why Your Furnace is Making Noise”
  • “How Often Should You Change Your Filter?”
  • “New AC Installation Timeline”

Post on YouTube, embed on your website, and share on social. Video gets 10x more engagement than text, and YouTube is the world’s second-largest search engine.

Step 7: Build Local Citations in HVAC Directories

Citations are mentions of your business name, address, and phone number on other websites. They help Google verify that you’re a real, local business.

HVAC-Specific Directories

  • HomeAdvisor — Huge for home services; get listed and get reviews
  • Angi (Angie’s List) — Similar to HomeAdvisor, trusted by homeowners
  • Thumbtack — Match with customers looking for services
  • Better Business Bureau (BBB) — Essential for trust and citations
  • Yelp — Every HVAC company needs a Yelp profile
  • Service.com — Growing platform for home services
  • NATE (North American Technician Excellence) — If you’re certified, list here

General Local Citations

  • Google My Business
  • Apple Maps
  • Facebook
  • Local Chamber of Commerce
  • Local business directories for your city

Critical: Ensure NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency across all citations. If your address is slightly different on one site, it confuses Google and hurts your ranking. See our complete citation guide for consistency tips.

Step 8: Multi-Location SEO for HVAC Franchises

If you have multiple locations, you need a different strategy. Improper multi-location SEO will actually hurt your rankings.

The Right Way to Do Multi-Location HVAC SEO

  • Separate Google Business Profiles: Each location gets its own GBP. Don’t use one profile for all locations.
  • Separate location pages: /ac-repair-phoenix/, /ac-repair-phoenix-scottsdale/, /ac-repair-mesa/ etc. Each with localized content.
  • Unique service area maps: Each location should claim different service areas. No overlap.
  • Localized content: Blog posts about Phoenix-specific HVAC issues, Arizona heat challenges, etc.
  • Local reviews per location: Don’t consolidate reviews. Let each location build its own review base.
  • Separate citations: Get each location listed individually in directories.

The most common mistake: Franchisees try to use one website for multiple locations. Google penalizes this. You need separate GMB profiles + location-specific pages + localized content. Read our complete multi-location SEO guide for HVAC franchises.

Step 9: Local Link Building for HVAC

Backlinks signal to Google that your site is authoritative. For HVAC companies, local links are especially valuable:

Link Building Tactics

  • Local sponsorships: Sponsor your local Little League, school event, or charity. Ask them to link to your website.
  • Local partnerships: Partner with a plumber or electrician. You mention each other on your websites.
  • Industry associations: Join local HVAC associations or chambers of commerce that link to members.
  • Local press: Get quoted in local news articles about energy efficiency, winter preparation, etc.
  • Google News: Create locally-focused content that's newsworthy enough for Google News.
  • Local blogger outreach: Build relationships with local home improvement bloggers.

Quality matters more than quantity. One link from your local city council website is worth 10 links from random directories.

Step 10: Track ROI Like an HVAC Pro

You can’t improve what you don’t measure. Here’s how to track SEO results for HVAC:

Essential Metrics

  • Phone call tracking: Use a call tracking service that assigns unique phone numbers to different traffic sources. Know which SEO efforts drive calls.
  • Form submissions: Track “Request Service” or “Schedule Appointment” forms from organic traffic.
  • Rank tracking: Track your position for key keywords: “emergency AC repair [city],” “furnace repair [city],” etc.
  • Traffic from Google Maps: Set up Google Analytics to track traffic from Google Maps vs. organic search.
  • Revenue per lead: How much does an organic lead cost vs. an ads lead vs. referral?
  • Customer lifetime value: Which traffic source brings customers who return for maintenance?

Seasonal ROI Tracking

HVAC is seasonal, so track ROI by season:

  • Summer 2026: How many AC repair calls came from SEO in June-August?
  • Winter 2026-2027: How many furnace repair calls came from SEO in Dec-Feb?
  • Compare to last year: If you ranked better this summer, you should get more calls.

This lets you predict revenue. If summer 2026 brought 150 SEO calls at $500 average service value, that’s $75,000 in revenue from one season of SEO work.

Don’t get me wrong—Google Ads can work for HVAC. But here’s why SEO is better for sustainable growth:

Cost per click $40-100+ during peak season ~$0 once you rank
Seasonal spikes CPCs go up 3-5x in summer/winter Consistent cost, peak seasons = more volume
Long-term ROI Stops immediately when you stop paying Builds over time, keeps working
