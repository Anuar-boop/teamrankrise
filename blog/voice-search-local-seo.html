Voice Search Optimization:
How NH Customers Are Finding Businesses in 2026

By Alex · February 17, 2026 · 8 min read

“Hey Siri, find a plumber near me.” “Alexa, what’s the best dentist in Manchester?” “OK Google, auto repair open now in Nashua.”

Voice search isn’t coming — it’s here. Over 50% of local searches now involve voice, and that number is growing. For NH businesses, this means the way customers find you is fundamentally changing.

The good news: if you’re already doing solid local SEO, you’re most of the way there. Voice search optimization is largely about adapting what you’re already doing.

How Voice Search Differs from Typed Search

When people type, they use shorthand: “plumber manchester nh.” When they speak, they use natural language: “Who’s the best plumber in Manchester, New Hampshire?”

Key differences:

  • Longer queries. Voice searches average 7-9 words vs. 2-4 for typed searches.
  • Question format. Voice queries often start with who, what, where, when, why, how.
  • Local intent. 76% of voice searches have local intent (“near me”, “in [city]”, “open now”).
  • Conversational tone. “Where can I get my car fixed on a Sunday in Concord?” vs. “auto repair sunday concord nh.”
  • Single answer expected. Voice assistants typically give ONE result, not 10. Position 1 gets everything.

Why This Matters for NH Businesses

New Hampshire’s demographics make voice search especially important:

  • Rural areas. People driving through the White Mountains or Lakes Region use voice search while driving to find businesses.
  • Seasonal tourism. Visitors ask Siri for “best restaurant near me” or “ski shop open today” throughout winter and fall foliage season.
  • Aging population. NH has one of the oldest median ages in the US. Older adults increasingly use voice assistants because they’re easier than typing on small screens.
  • Smart home adoption. Amazon (headquartered just south of NH in Boston) has heavy Alexa penetration in New England.

7 Ways to Optimize for Voice Search

1. Nail Your Google Business Profile

When someone asks “Hey Google, find a dentist near me,” Google pulls from Google Business Profiles. This is the single most important factor for voice search visibility.

Action items:

  • Verify your business name, address, and phone number are accurate
  • Fill out EVERY field — hours, services, attributes, description
  • Add correct business categories (primary + secondary)
  • Keep hours updated, especially holidays and seasonal changes
  • Ensure your NAP is consistent across all directories

2. Add FAQ Content to Your Website

Voice queries are questions. Answer them directly on your website.

Create FAQ sections on your key pages that answer:

  • “How much does [your service] cost in [your city]?”
  • “What are your hours?”
  • “Do you offer free estimates?”
  • “What areas do you serve?”
  • “How long does [service] take?”
  • “Do you offer emergency service?”

Use the actual question as a heading (H2 or H3), followed by a concise 1-3 sentence answer. Google can pull these directly as voice search answers.

3. Use Schema Markup

Schema markup helps voice assistants understand your business data. At minimum, add:

  • LocalBusiness schema with name, address, phone, hours, and coordinates
  • FAQ schema for your question-and-answer content
  • Service schema listing what you offer
  • Review/AggregateRating schema to surface your star rating

When Alexa or Google Assistant processes a voice query, structured data gives them confidence in the information they provide.

4. Write in a Conversational Tone

Your content should sound like how people talk, not how they type.

Instead of: “Professional HVAC services available in the greater Nashua metropolitan area.”

Write: “We provide heating and cooling services for homes and businesses throughout Nashua and surrounding towns.”

Tips for conversational content:

  • Use “you” and “your” instead of “clients” and “customers”
  • Write in short sentences (under 20 words)
  • Answer questions directly in the first sentence, then elaborate
  • Use contractions (we’re, you’ll, it’s) — they sound natural

5. Optimize for “Near Me” and Local Modifiers

The most common voice search pattern is “[service] near me” or “[service] in [city].” Google determines “near me” results based on:

  • Your GBP location and service area
  • Your website’s city/state mentions
  • Your NAP citations across the web
  • The searcher’s GPS location

Make sure your website naturally mentions the cities you serve. Dedicated city landing pages help enormously for voice search visibility across multiple locations.

6. Prioritize Mobile Speed

Most voice searches happen on mobile devices. If your site is slow on mobile, you won’t be the answer voice assistants choose.

Targets:

  • Page load under 3 seconds on mobile
  • Core Web Vitals passing (LCP under 2.5s, INP under 200ms, CLS under 0.1)
  • Mobile-friendly design that works on any screen size

Google explicitly favors fast, mobile-optimized sites for voice search results because the user experience matters even more when someone is on the go.

7. Get More Reviews (Seriously)

When someone asks “What’s the best pizza in Portsmouth?” — Google often surfaces the highest-rated option with the most reviews.

Reviews are the ultimate voice search ranking factor for “best” queries. The business with the highest rating + most reviews + most recent reviews wins the voice result.

Voice Search Queries by Industry

Here are the most common voice queries for NH business types:

Home Services

  • “Find a plumber near me”
  • “Emergency HVAC repair in [city]”
  • “Who’s the best electrician in [city]?”
  • “How much does a roof replacement cost in New Hampshire?”

Healthcare

  • “Dentist accepting new patients near me”
  • “Walk-in clinic open now in [city]”
  • “Best chiropractor in [city]”
  • “Pediatrician near me that takes [insurance]”

Restaurants & Food

  • “Best Italian restaurant in Manchester”
  • “Pizza delivery near me open now”
  • “Breakfast places in Keene”
  • “Restaurants with outdoor seating near me”

Auto Services

  • “Oil change near me”
  • “Cheapest tire shop in [city]”
  • “Auto repair shop open Saturday”
  • “Tow truck near me”

The Position Zero Goal

Voice assistants typically read from “Position Zero” — the featured snippet that appears above regular search results. To earn featured snippets:

  1. Answer questions directly. Start your answer with the question, then give a clear 40-60 word response.
  2. Use structured formats. Lists, tables, and step-by-step formats are featured snippet favorites.
  3. Target question keywords. “How much,” “what is,” “how to,” “where can I” — these trigger featured snippets.
  4. Already rank on page 1. Most featured snippets come from top-10 organic results.

What NOT to Worry About

Despite the hype, you don’t need to:

  • Build a separate “voice app” or Alexa skill. For local businesses, this is unnecessary. Focus on web presence.
  • Completely change your SEO strategy. Voice optimization is an extension of good SEO, not a replacement.
  • Obsess over exact voice query matching. Google understands context. You don’t need to match every possible phrasing.

The Bottom Line

Voice search optimization for NH businesses comes down to three things:

  1. Perfect your Google Business Profile — this is where most voice answers come from
  2. Answer questions on your website — clear, conversational, FAQ-style content
  3. Be the best result — fast site, great reviews, accurate information

If you’re doing these things, you’re already ahead of 90% of NH businesses. Voice search isn’t a separate channel — it’s another way people interact with the same Google results. Do SEO well, and voice search takes care of itself.
