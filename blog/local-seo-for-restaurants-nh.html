Local SEO for Restaurants: The Complete NH Guide

Alex Feb 17, 2026 11 min read

When someone's hungry in Manchester, Nashua, or anywhere in NH, they reach for their phone and search "restaurants near me" or "best pizza in [town]." If your restaurant doesn't show up in those results, you're losing customers to competitors who do — every single day.

Restaurant SEO is different from other local businesses. You're competing on food photos, reviews, hours, menus, and delivery options — not just keywords. This guide covers everything specific to getting NH restaurants found on Google.

Why Restaurant SEO Is Different

Restaurants have unique challenges and opportunities:

  • High search volume — "Restaurants near me" is one of the top local searches globally
  • Visual-first — Food photos matter more than any other industry
  • Reviews are everything — A 4.2 vs 4.5 star rating is the difference between full and empty tables
  • Hours and real-time info matter — Wrong hours = frustrated customers = negative reviews
  • Multiple platforms — Google, Yelp, TripAdvisor, DoorDash, Uber Eats all need attention
  • Seasonal in NH — Tourist areas (Lakes Region, Seacoast) have huge seasonal swings

GBP Optimization for Restaurants

Your Google Business Profile is the most important digital asset for any restaurant. It appears in the Local Pack, Google Maps, and the knowledge panel when people search your name.

Categories

Choose the most specific primary category:

  • "Pizza Restaurant" beats "Restaurant" for pizza shops
  • "Thai Restaurant" beats "Asian Restaurant" for Thai places
  • "Seafood Restaurant" is perfect for Seacoast NH spots
  • Add secondaries: "Restaurant," "Takeaway Restaurant," "Catering Service" if applicable

Menu

Google has a dedicated "Menu" section in GBP. Upload your full menu with:

  • Clear section names (Appetizers, Entrees, Desserts)
  • Every item with price
  • Descriptions for popular items
  • Dietary labels (vegetarian, gluten-free, vegan) where applicable

Keep your menu current. Nothing frustrates customers more than showing up for a dish that's no longer served.

Photos — Your Secret Weapon

Restaurants with 100+ photos on GBP get 520% more calls than average. Upload:

  • Food photos (50+) — Well-lit, appetizing shots of your best dishes
  • Interior photos (10+) — Show the ambiance, seating, bar area
  • Exterior photos (5+) — Helps customers find you
  • Team photos — Chef, staff, friendly faces
  • Event photos — Special occasions, live music, seasonal events

NH tip: Seasonal photos work great. Snow-covered patio with cozy interior contrast, fall foliage from your patio, summer outdoor dining — these create emotional connections.

Hours — Get Them Right

Wrong hours is the #1 source of negative reviews for restaurants on Google. Update:

  • Regular hours (including kitchen close time if different from restaurant close)
  • Holiday hours for every major holiday
  • Special hours for events, seasonal changes
  • NH-specific: Winter hours vs summer hours if you change seasonally

Attributes

Google has restaurant-specific attributes. Enable all that apply:

  • Outdoor seating, patio, rooftop
  • Takeout, delivery, dine-in
  • Reservations accepted
  • Good for groups, romantic, family-friendly
  • Live music, sports on TV
  • Happy hour
  • Wheelchair accessible
  • Vegetarian/vegan options

Review Strategy for Restaurants

Reviews are the lifeblood of restaurant marketing. A strong reputation fills tables; a weak one empties them.

The Numbers

Rating Customer Impact
4.5+ starsActively attracts new customers. "Best in area" perception.
4.0-4.4 starsSolid. Customers choose you confidently.
3.5-3.9 starsHesitation. Customers check reviews more carefully.
Below 3.5Major red flag. Most people skip you entirely.

Getting More Reviews

  • Print your Google review link on receipts
  • Create a table tent or card with a QR code linking to your review page
  • Train servers to mention reviews at the right moment (after a compliment)
  • Follow up with catering/event customers via email
  • Read our complete review guide

Responding to Restaurant Reviews

Restaurant reviews often mention specific dishes, servers, or experiences. Use this in your responses:

  • "Glad you loved the lobster roll — it's our chef's favorite too!" (reinforces popular items)
  • "Sarah will be thrilled to hear this — she takes great pride in her service." (personal touch)
  • For negatives: always offer to make it right with a specific action, not just an apology

Full guide: how to respond to reviews and handling negative reviews.

Website Optimization for Restaurants

Your website converts Google searchers into reservations and orders. Key pages:

Must-Have Pages

  1. Homepage — Cuisine type, location, hours, phone, online ordering link above the fold
  2. Menu page — HTML text (not just a PDF). Google can't read PDFs well for SEO.
  3. About page — Your story, chef bio, local sourcing (NH farms, local ingredients)
  4. Contact/Location page — Embedded Google Map, parking info, directions
  5. Online ordering page — Or a clear link to your ordering platform
  6. Events/Specials page — Weekly specials, live music schedule, private events

Restaurant Schema Markup

Add Restaurant schema to your homepage. Include:

  • Business name, address, phone
  • Cuisine type
  • Price range
  • Hours of operation
  • Accepts reservations
  • Menu URL
  • Serves cuisine (American, Italian, etc.)

The Menu Page — Critical for SEO

Your menu page is often the highest-traffic page on your site. Make it SEO-friendly:

  • HTML text, not just images or PDFs — Google needs to read your menu items
  • Include prices — "How much is [dish] at [restaurant]" is a common search
  • Structured by section — h2 for categories, clear item names
  • Allergen/dietary info — "gluten-free options [city]" is a growing search

Multi-Platform Presence

Unlike most businesses, restaurants need to be active on multiple platforms simultaneously:

Priority Platforms

  1. Google Business Profile — #1 priority, always
  2. Yelp — Still the #2 restaurant discovery platform
  3. TripAdvisor — Essential for tourist areas (Seacoast, Lakes Region, White Mountains)
  4. Instagram — The most important social platform for restaurants. Food is visual.
  5. Facebook — Events, specials, community engagement
  6. Delivery apps — DoorDash, Uber Eats, Grubhub (if you offer delivery)

Consistency Across Platforms

Your name, address, phone, and hours must be identical everywhere. When one platform shows you're open until 10 and another says 9, the customer who shows up at 9:30 leaves a 1-star review.

NH Restaurant SEO: Seasonal Strategy

New Hampshire has distinct seasonal patterns for restaurants. Follow this calendar (aligned with our seasonal marketing guide):

Spring (March-May)

  • Promote patio/deck reopening
  • Mother's Day specials and brunch
  • Graduation celebration catering
  • "Spring menu" launch

Summer (June-August)

  • Tourist traffic optimization (especially Seacoast and Lakes Region)
  • Outdoor dining photos and promotion
  • Fourth of July, local festival tie-ins
  • Late-night summer hours

Fall (September-November)

  • Foliage season — tourists searching "restaurants near [NH attraction]"
  • Harvest menu, local ingredient features
  • Thanksgiving catering and special menus
  • Oktoberfest, apple cider, comfort food

Winter (December-February)

  • Holiday parties and catering
  • Valentine's Day (huge for restaurants)
  • Ski season traffic (if near mountains)
  • Cozy interior photos, warm comfort food
  • Storm-proof messaging ("We're open even in the snow!")

Content Ideas for Restaurant Blogs

Most restaurants don't blog — which means even minimal content gives you a huge competitive advantage. Ideas:

  • "The story behind our [signature dish]"
  • "Meet Chef [name]: From [background] to [your restaurant]"
  • "Best [cuisine type] in [city]: What makes ours different"
  • "Your guide to dining in [city] — from breakfast to late night"
  • "Local ingredients: the NH farms we source from"
  • "How to plan the perfect [event type] at [restaurant name]"
  • Seasonal recipe shares
  • "What to order if you have [dietary restriction]"

Even one post per month puts you ahead of 95% of restaurant competitors on Google.

Common Restaurant SEO Mistakes

  1. Menu as image/PDF only — Google can't read it. Use HTML text.
  2. Wrong hours — Especially holiday hours. Check monthly.
  3. Ignoring Yelp — It's still the #2 restaurant platform. Claim and manage your profile.
  4. No food photos on GBP — The #1 thing customers look at before choosing a restaurant.
  5. Not responding to reviews — 53% of customers expect a response within 7 days.
  6. Flash/JavaScript-heavy website — Kills mobile performance and SEO.
  7. No online ordering link — 60% of restaurant orders now happen online.
  8. Ignoring basic SEO — No title tags, no meta descriptions, no schema.

The Restaurant SEO Checklist

  • GBP claimed, verified, and 100% complete
  • Specific restaurant category (not just "Restaurant")
  • Full menu uploaded to GBP and website (HTML text)
  • 50+ food photos on GBP, uploaded regularly
  • Hours accurate, including holidays
  • All attributes enabled (outdoor seating, delivery, etc.)
  • Review response rate: 100%
  • Yelp and TripAdvisor profiles claimed and current
  • NAP consistent across all platforms
  • Website mobile-friendly with click-to-call
  • Online ordering prominently linked
  • Restaurant schema on homepage
  • Active Instagram with food photography
  • Seasonal content/specials updated quarterly

Bottom Line

Restaurant SEO boils down to: look delicious, be findable, make it easy to order. Great photos, accurate information, tons of reviews, and a mobile-friendly way to see your menu and place an order.

NH restaurants have a unique advantage — we're a tourist destination with seasonal peaks that bring waves of new diners searching Google for where to eat. The restaurants that show up in those searches win business that their competitors never even knew existed.

Start with your GBP: upload 20 food photos this week, make sure your hours are correct, and respond to your last 10 reviews. That alone can measurably increase your visibility within 30 days.
