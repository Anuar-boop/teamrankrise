How to Use Google Search Console for Local SEO

Alex Feb 17, 2026 11 min read

Google Search Console (GSC) is the most underused free tool in local SEO. It gives you data directly from Google about how your site appears in search results — what queries trigger your pages, your click-through rates, indexing issues, and technical problems.

If Google Analytics tells you what happens on your site, Search Console tells you what happens before people get to your site. For NH businesses trying to improve their local SEO, this data is gold.

Setting Up Google Search Console

If you haven't set it up yet, here's the quick version:

  1. Go to search.google.com/search-console and sign in with your Google account
  2. Click "Add property" and enter your website URL
  3. Choose a verification method:
    • DNS record (recommended) — add a TXT record to your domain registrar
    • HTML file — upload a verification file to your website
    • HTML tag — add a meta tag to your homepage
    • Google Analytics — auto-verify if GA is already installed
  4. Submit your sitemap (yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml) under "Sitemaps"
  5. Wait 2-3 days for data to start appearing

Pro tip: Add both https://yourdomain.com and https://www.yourdomain.com as properties, then set the preferred version.

The 5 Reports That Matter for Local SEO

1. Performance Report — Your Ranking Dashboard

This is where you'll spend most of your time. The Performance report shows:

  • Queries: What people searched to find your site
  • Pages: Which pages appear in search results
  • Clicks: How many people clicked through to your site
  • Impressions: How many times your site appeared in results
  • CTR (Click-Through Rate): Clicks ÷ Impressions — how compelling your listing looks
  • Position: Your average ranking for each query

How to Use It for Local SEO

Filter by query containing your city name. Click "New" → "Query" → contains "manchester" (or whatever city). This shows only local searches. Look for:

  • High impressions, low clicks — your title/description needs work. Rewrite it to be more compelling
  • Position 4-10 — these are "striking distance" keywords. A little optimization could push them to top 3
  • Queries you didn't expect — people might be finding you for services you haven't optimized for. Create content targeting those

2. Coverage/Indexing Report — Is Google Seeing Your Pages?

This report shows which pages Google has indexed (can show in search) and which have problems.

Key things to check:

  • "Not indexed" pages — if important pages aren't indexed, they can't rank. Common causes: noindex tag, thin content, crawl errors
  • "Excluded" pages — pages Google chose not to index. Check if any are pages you WANT indexed
  • "Error" pages — server errors, redirect issues, or blocked pages that need fixing

For a local business, make sure these pages are indexed:

  • Homepage
  • All service pages
  • City/location pages
  • Contact page
  • All blog posts

3. Mobile Usability — Are Phone Users Happy?

Since most local searches happen on mobile, this report is critical. It flags issues like:

  • Text too small to read
  • Clickable elements too close together
  • Content wider than screen
  • Viewport not configured

Fix every issue listed here. Mobile-friendliness is a ranking factor, and for local businesses, it's even more important since customers are often searching on the go.

4. Core Web Vitals — Speed and User Experience

Google measures three metrics that affect rankings:

Metric What It Measures Good Score
LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) How fast the main content loads Under 2.5 seconds
INP (Interaction to Next Paint) How fast the page responds to clicks Under 200ms
CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) How much the layout jumps around Under 0.1

If any URLs show "poor" status, prioritize fixing them. See our speed optimization guide for specific fixes.

5. Links Report — Who Links to You?

The Links report shows:

  • External links: Other websites linking to yours (the most valuable SEO signal)
  • Top linking sites: Which domains link to you most
  • Top linked pages: Which of your pages get the most links
  • Internal links: How your own pages link to each other

For local businesses, look for links from:

  • Local directories and chambers of commerce
  • NH news sites and blogs
  • Industry directories
  • Partner/supplier websites

If you're not getting local backlinks, check our guide to getting local backlinks in NH.

5 Local SEO Actions You Can Take Today with GSC

Action 1: Find Your "Striking Distance" Keywords

  1. Go to Performance → Queries
  2. Filter: Position between 4 and 20
  3. Sort by impressions (highest first)
  4. These are keywords where you're close to page 1 but not in the top 3

Fix: Find the page ranking for each keyword. Improve the content — add more detail, better headings, internal links, and make sure the keyword appears in the title and H1.

Action 2: Improve Low-CTR Pages

  1. Go to Performance → Pages
  2. Sort by impressions (highest first)
  3. Look for pages with high impressions but low CTR (under 3%)

Fix: Rewrite the page's title tag and meta description. Make them compelling — use numbers, power words, and a clear benefit. Your title is your ad copy in search results.

Action 3: Discover New Content Ideas

  1. Go to Performance → Queries
  2. Sort by impressions
  3. Look for queries that don't match any page on your site

Fix: Create new content targeting those queries. If people are searching for "emergency plumber nashua nh" and you don't have a page about emergency services, that's a content gap. Use our keyword research guide to expand these into content clusters.

Action 4: Fix Indexing Problems

  1. Go to Pages (Indexing) report
  2. Check "Not indexed" for any important pages
  3. Click through to see the specific reason

Fix: Common solutions include removing accidental noindex tags, adding more content to thin pages, fixing server errors, and submitting URLs for re-indexing.

Action 5: Monitor Your Sitemap

  1. Go to Sitemaps
  2. Verify your sitemap is submitted and showing "Success"
  3. Check "Discovered URLs" — this should match (roughly) the number of pages you have

Fix: If your sitemap shows errors, check the XML for formatting issues. Make sure every URL in the sitemap returns a 200 status code.

Monthly GSC Routine for Local Businesses

Set a calendar reminder. Here's your 30-minute monthly check-in:

Time Task
5 min Check Performance overview — are clicks and impressions trending up?
5 min Review new queries — any surprising keywords you should target?
5 min Check for indexing errors — any new pages not getting indexed?
5 min Review Core Web Vitals — any pages showing "poor" status?
5 min Check mobile usability — any new issues flagged?
5 min Review new backlinks — anyone linking to you? Thank them or build the relationship

GSC + Google Analytics: Using Them Together

Search Console and Analytics complement each other:

  • GSC: What queries bring people to your site (pre-click data)
  • GA: What people do once they arrive (post-click data)

Connect them in GA4 under Admin → Product Links → Search Console Links. This lets you see the full picture: which search queries lead to which pages and which pages convert.

For tracking SEO results, use both tools together. GSC shows if your rankings are improving; GA shows if those improvements are generating leads and revenue.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Checking daily: GSC data has a 2-3 day delay. Weekly or monthly reviews are more productive
  • Obsessing over position changes: Rankings fluctuate daily. Look at monthly trends, not daily shifts
  • Ignoring the "Pages" tab: Most people only look at queries. The Pages tab shows which content is actually performing
  • Not filtering by date range: Always compare to the previous period to spot trends
  • Forgetting to submit new pages: After publishing new content, use "URL Inspection" and click "Request Indexing" to speed up discovery
