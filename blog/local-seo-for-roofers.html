Roofing SEO: How to Get More Roofing Leads From Google

The complete SEO strategy for roofers to generate $5K–$25K+ leads through organic search and Google Maps.

Roofing SEO 14 min read

Why Roofers Need SEO Now

Roofing is one of the highest-value home services. A typical roof replacement runs $5,000 to $25,000+. That means each qualified lead is worth hundreds or even thousands in gross profit.

But here's the problem: your competition is bidding on Google Ads. And those bids are brutal. Roofing keywords like "roof repair near me" and "roof replacement" cost $50–$150 per click in Google Ads. A single bad month of clicks can cost you $10K+ with nothing to show for it.

SEO is the antidote. Once your website and Google Business Profile rank on the first page, those clicks are free. You get qualified homeowners searching "roofer near me" or "roof repair [your city]" — and you show up without paying-per-click.

The data backs this up:

  • 76% of searches on mobile include "near me" — and most homeowners search on their phones when damage happens (storm, leak, inspection reminder).
  • Local search drives 2x more foot traffic and phone calls than national ads.
  • Roofing companies ranking in the Google Maps "Local Pack" (top 3) get 40–60% of qualified calls in their service area.

Your goal: Rank on the first page of Google organically and own the top 3 positions in Google Maps for roofing keywords in your cities. This guide shows you how.

1. Google Business Profile: The Foundation of Roofing SEO

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the single most important asset for local roofing SEO. It's where homeowners find your hours, call button, reviews, and photos — and it's the first place Google decides whether to show you in the Local Pack.

Set Up Your Profile Correctly

Category selection: Use these primary categories (in priority order):

  • Roofing Contractor (primary)
  • Roof Repair Service
  • Gutter Service (if applicable)
  • Home Improvement Contractor (secondary)

Secondary categories matter. Google uses them to match your profile to search intent. If a homeowner searches "flat roof repair," Google checks if your GBP mentions flat roofing in categories or service descriptions. See our full GBP categories guide for all service-based businesses.

Optimize Your Business Description

Your GBP description (750 chars max) should include:

  • What you do: "Residential and commercial roof repair, replacement, and installation."
  • Specific services: "Shingle, metal, flat roof, gutters, storm damage repair."
  • Your differentiator: "Family-owned since 2005. Licensed, insured, 24/7 emergency repairs."
  • Service area: "Serving Dallas, Fort Worth, Arlington."

Example: "Licensed roofing contractor serving Dallas, Arlington, and Fort Worth. We specialize in asphalt shingle, metal roofing, flat roof repair and replacement. 24/7 emergency storm damage services. Free inspections. 20+ year warranties. Call now for same-day estimates."

Photos Win Rankings and Calls

Google gives ranking priority to GBPs with photos. But here's the leverage: roofing is visual. Homeowners want to see your work before calling.

Photo strategy:

  • Before/after gallery: 20–30 high-quality photos of completed roofs (roof after replacement, close-ups of shingles, wide shots of full homes).
  • Drone photos: Aerial shots of completed roofs. These convert better than ground photos because homeowners see the full scope.
  • Team/tools: Photos of your crew, equipment, van, and office. Builds trust.
  • Storm damage: If you do storm repairs, show damaged roofs before and after repair. These get high engagement.
  • Detail shots: Close-ups of roof penetration sealing, flashing, gutter work. Shows craftsmanship.

Upload at least 20 photos. Update them seasonally (fresh photos get ranking boosts). Use captions with keywords: "Metal roof replacement in Arlington" or "Hail damage repair — Denver."

Verify and Lock Your Profile

Verify ownership through Google's postcard method (takes 1–2 weeks). Add your team members as editors. This prevents competitors or spammers from claiming your profile or editing details.

2. Master Reviews and Reputation Management

Google's algorithm favors profiles with more reviews. Roofing profiles ranking in the Local Pack typically have 50–200+ reviews. But review quantity alone doesn't win — review velocity (new reviews each month) and recency matter most.

Build a Post-Job Review System

The best time to ask for a review is right after the job is done, when the customer is happy. Create a process:

  • On completion day: Hand the homeowner a card with a QR code linking to your Google review page. Example: "We'd love your feedback! Scan here or visit [Google review link]."
  • Follow-up email (2–3 days): Send a personalized email from your owner/manager: "Hi [Name], thanks for choosing us for your roof replacement. Would you mind leaving a 30-second review on Google? It helps local families find us."
  • Follow-up call (1 week): If they haven't reviewed yet, call them. Most people will review when you make it easy.

Respond to All Reviews**

Respond to every review — both 5-star and negative — within 24–48 hours. Google sees response rate as a ranking signal.

5-star reviews: "Thank you for the kind words! We're proud of the roof we installed. If you ever need gutters or maintenance, give us a call. Thanks for choosing [Company]!"

Negative reviews: Stay professional. Address the issue, offer to make it right, and take the conversation offline (ask them to call). Example: "I'm sorry to hear you had this experience. We take pride in our work. Please call me directly at [phone] so I can make this right. — [Your name]."

Handle Insurance Claim Disputes with Care

Roofing involves insurance claims. Sometimes customers dispute claim denials or settlements. Document everything, and be transparent about what insurance will and won't cover. This prevents negative reviews and shows integrity.

3. Storm and Seasonal SEO Strategy

Roofing demand is seasonal and event-driven. Spring brings storms and hail. Fall brings leaves and gutters. Winter brings ice damage. Smart roofers create content around these patterns and rank for seasonal + damage-specific keywords.

Storm Damage Content Calendar

Create landing pages and blog posts targeting storm damage keywords:

  • "Hail damage roof repair" + city (e.g., "Hail damage roof repair Denver")
  • "Storm damage roof repair near me"
  • "Hurricane roof damage restoration" (if in hurricane zones)
  • "Ice dam removal" + city (winter)
  • "Wind damage roof repair"
  • "Flat roof leak repair after storm"

Each post should include:

  • What to look for after a storm (visual inspection checklist)
  • How to file insurance claims (educate customers)
  • Your emergency response process (call 24/7, same-day inspections, etc.)
  • Customer testimonials from past storm repairs
  • Before/after photos from storm jobs

Seasonal Pages by Service Type

Spring: "Spring roof inspection," "hail damage," "storm preparedness"
Summer: "Roof replacement," "UV damage," "cooling costs and roof color"
Fall: "Gutter cleaning," "leaf damage," "roof maintenance before winter"
Winter: "Ice dam removal," "snow load," "winter roof safety"

Seasonal Keyword Targeting

Use Google Search Console and Google Trends to identify which storm/seasonal keywords spike in your region. Rank early (create the page in January for spring demand), so you're ready when search volume peaks in March–April.

4. Service Page Optimization

Your roofing website likely has a homepage and maybe a "Services" page. That's not enough. Google ranks specific service pages higher than generic category pages. Create a dedicated page for each major service:

Core Roofing Service Pages

  • Roof Repair — emergency repairs, leak fixes, flashing repair
  • Roof Replacement — full roof tear-off and installation
  • Asphalt Shingle Roofing — most common material, pricing, durability
  • Metal Roofing — advantages, cost vs. shingle, lifespan
  • Flat Roof Repair — commercial and residential flat roofs
  • Gutter Installation and Repair — leaf guards, cleaning, replacement

Optimize Each Page: The Framework

For each service page:

  1. Title tag (60 chars): "[Service] in [City] — [Company Name]" (e.g., "Metal Roof Installation in Denver — [Your Company]")
  2. Meta description (155 chars): Describe the service + benefit + CTA. "Expert metal roof installation in Denver. Durable, energy-efficient, 50+ year warranty. Free inspection and quote today."
  3. H1 (page title): Service + city. "Professional Metal Roof Installation in Denver"
  4. H2 subheadings: Break content into logical sections: "What is a Metal Roof?", "Benefits of Metal Roofing", "Metal vs. Asphalt Cost", "Our Process", "Why Choose Us?", "FAQ"
  5. Body content (1,500–2,500 words): Explain the service, why homeowners need it, your process, materials, timeline, costs (ballpark ranges), warranties, and why you're the best choice locally.
  6. Before/after images: 3–5 high-quality photos showing the service in action.
  7. Local mentions: Mention your service area multiple times: "We serve Dallas, Arlington, Fort Worth, and surrounding areas."
  8. Schema markup: Use LocalBusiness and Service schema (see our on-page SEO guide).
  9. CTA buttons: "Get Free Inspection," "Call (555) 123-4567," "Schedule Quote"

Link Between Service Pages

Link from general pages to specific ones. Example: On your "Services" page, link "Metal Roofing" to the dedicated metal roof page. Link from blog posts to service pages when relevant. This helps Google understand your service hierarchy and improves internal link authority.

5. Before/After Gallery and Visual Content Strategy

Roofing is visual. Your marketing should be too. Before/after photos and drone imagery convert better than text alone and improve SEO.

Drone Photography Investment

If you don't already have drone photos, hire a local drone photographer ($500–$2,000 for 20–30 shots). Aerial views of completed roofs:

  • Show the full scope of work
  • Build credibility and trust
  • Get more engagement on Google, social media, and review sites
  • Help Google understand the scale of your projects

Gallery Page Structure

Create a dedicated gallery page (or gallery section) with:

  • Filter by service: Users can filter by "Roof Repair," "Replacement," "Metal," "Shingle," etc.
  • Project location tags: Tag each project with city/neighborhood. This helps with local SEO.
  • Captions with keywords: "Complete shingle roof replacement in South Denver" (includes service + location).
  • Image SEO: Add alt text to all images: alt="Metal roof replacement completed in Arlington, Texas"

Social Proof and Trust Signals

Include customer testimonials below project photos: "[Customer name] — Homeowner in [City]" + quote: "They did an amazing job. Highly recommend!" These boost credibility and SEO.

6. Local Citations: The Underrated Ranking Factor

Google uses citations (mentions of your business name, address, and phone number on other websites) to verify your legitimacy and local relevance. Missing or inconsistent citations hurt your rankings.

High-Priority Citation Sites for Roofers

Claim or verify your business on these sites (prioritized by impact):

  • HomeAdvisor — #1 for home services. Roofers here get high-intent leads. Claim and maintain profile.
  • Angi (formerly Angie's List) — Similar to HomeAdvisor. Claim and get reviews.
  • BBB (Better Business Bureau) — Local credibility signal. Verify and respond to reviews.
  • GAF (GAF Materials): GAF Certified Contractor directory (if you're GAF certified).
  • Owens Corning Contractor Network: If Owens Corning certified.
  • Roofing Contractor Magazine / Directory: List your business if available in your region.
  • Local Chamber of Commerce: Join and claim your listing.
  • Google Maps + GBP: (Already covered above.)
  • Yelp: Claim and optimize. Monitor reviews.
  • Houzz: Popular for home contractors. Add your projects and testimonials.

NAP Consistency

"NAP" = Name, Address, Phone. Your business name, address, and phone must be identical across all citations. If your address is "123 Main St." on your website but "123 Main Street" on HomeAdvisor, Google sees them as two different businesses. This hurts your ranking.

How to audit:

  • Search "[Your Business Name]" + "address" in Google.
  • Check the top 10 results.
  • Fix any inconsistencies (contact sites and request updates).
  • Use a tool like SEMrush or Moz Local to audit citations automatically.

7. Neighborhood and City Targeting Pages

If you serve multiple cities, create city-specific landing pages. Google gives ranking priority to pages that mention specific cities and neighborhoods.

City Page Structure

Create a page for each major city (e.g., /seo-dallas.html, /seo-denver.html). Each page should include:

  • City-specific headline: "Roofing Contractor in [City]" or "[Service] in [City]"
  • Service area map: Visual map showing your coverage area.
  • Why this city: Why you serve it (local office, crew based there, etc.).
  • Neighborhoods served: List specific neighborhoods/zip codes.
  • Local testimonials: Reviews from customers in that city.
  • Projects in that city: Before/after gallery filtered to projects in that location.
  • Local partnerships: "We partner with [Local Insurance Company]" or "Preferred contractor for [Local Builder]."

Service Area Pages

In addition to city pages, create service-area pages if you cover neighborhoods or suburbs. Example: "/roofing-contractor-north-dallas.html"

8. Insurance and Financing Content

Roofing is expensive. Many homeowners finance it or go through insurance. Educational content on these topics builds authority, drives organic traffic, and answers customer questions before they call.

Insurance Content Ideas

  • "Understanding Roof Insurance Claims: A Homeowner's Guide"
  • "What Does Home Insurance Cover for Roof Damage?"
  • "How to File an Insurance Claim for Hail Damage"
  • "Insurance Adjuster Inspection: What to Expect"
  • "Does Insurance Cover Roof Repairs vs. Replacement?"
  • "Storm Damage Documentation: How to Help Your Claim"

Financing Content Ideas

  • "Roof Replacement Financing Options: Loans, Credit Cards, and More"
  • "Is a Roof Loan Worth It? Cost Comparison"
  • "How to Get a Home Equity Line of Credit for Roof Replacement"
  • "Roof Replacement Cost: What to Expect in [Your Region]"
  • "Roof Warranties Explained: What's Covered?"

Content Format

Each article should be 1,500+ words, well-researched, and cite authoritative sources (insurance company websites, financing partners, etc.). Include CTAs like "Ready to replace your roof? Call for a free estimate."

These posts rank for educational keywords (lower intent but high traffic) and establish your company as a trusted authority. They also support your higher-intent service pages through internal linking.

9. Link Building and Authority

Backlinks (links from other websites to yours) are one of Google's top ranking factors. You need them. Here are ethical, industry-appropriate link-building tactics for roofers:

Local Link Opportunities

  • Local business directories: Claim listings on Yelp, HomeAdvisor, BBB, Chamber of Commerce (all link to you).
  • Local news and events: Sponsor a local Little League team, charity event, or school fundraiser. They'll link to your site.
  • Local partnerships: Partner with local real estate agents, home inspectors, or contractors. Link to each other's sites.
  • Local media mentions: If you're quoted in local news about storm prep or home safety, ask for a link to your site.

Industry Links

  • Manufacturer directories: GAF Certified Contractors, Owens Corning Network, etc.
  • Industry associations: National Association of Roofing Contractors, state roofing associations.
  • Industry blogs: Write guest posts for roofing trade publications or home improvement blogs.

PR and Authority

  • Press releases: If you expand, win an award, or develop a unique service, send a press release to local media.
  • Case studies: Document a complex or interesting roof project with before/after photos. Share it publicly. Others may link to it.

10. Measuring ROI: Cost Per Lead and Revenue Per Lead

SEO is an investment. Track your returns to justify the spend and optimize your strategy.

Set Up Tracking

Phone tracking: Use a call-tracking tool (CallRail, Invoca, DialogTech) to assign phone numbers to different marketing channels (Google Organic, Google Maps, Ads, etc.). This shows which channel drives the most calls.

Google Analytics and Conversions: In Google Analytics 4:

  • Create a conversion event for "Phone Call" (when a visitor clicks your phone number).
  • Create another conversion for "Contact Form Submission."
  • Tag all traffic sources (organic, paid, direct, etc.).

Calculate Cost Per Lead and Revenue Per Lead

Cost Per Lead (CPL):

  • Google Ads: $50–$150 per click. With a 10–20% lead conversion rate, CPL = $250–$1,500.
  • SEO: Initial investment ($3K–$10K+ over 3–6 months) + ongoing monthly ($500–$2K/month). After ramp-up, CPL drops to $50–$300 as traffic stabilizes.

Revenue Per Lead (RPL): Track which leads convert to jobs and their job value. If you get 100 leads from organic search and 20 convert to jobs averaging $10K each, RPL = $2,000 per organic lead.

Key metrics to track:

  • Organic leads (monthly)
  • Organic lead conversion rate (leads to jobs)
  • Average job value
  • Total revenue from organic (leads × conversion rate × average job)
  • Cost per organic lead
  • Return on investment (revenue / cost)

Example ROI Calculation

Month 3 of SEO campaign:

  • Organic traffic: 200 visits
  • Organic leads: 20 phone calls + form submissions
  • Conversion rate: 30% (6 jobs)
  • Average job value: $12,000
  • Total revenue: 6 × $12,000 = $72,000
  • SEO cost (3 months): $3,000
  • ROI: 2,400% return (or 24:1 ratio)

This doesn't include ongoing maintenance, but it shows why SEO pays off quickly for high-value services like roofing.

Compare to Google Ads

Google Ads for the same period:

  • Monthly spend: $2,000
  • Total spent (3 months): $6,000
  • Cost per click: $80
  • Total clicks: 75
  • Lead conversion: 25 leads
  • Job conversion: 8 jobs × $12,000 = $96,000
  • ROI: 1,600% return (or 16:1 ratio)

Both are profitable, but SEO has better long-term ROI because clicks are free after ranking. Google Ads cost per click keeps climbing (due to seasonality and competition).
